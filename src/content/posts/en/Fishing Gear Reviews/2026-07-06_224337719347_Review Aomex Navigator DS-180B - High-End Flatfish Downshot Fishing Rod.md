Let's examine the rod in detail. The Navigator downshot rod features a 2-piece design, utilizing a butt-joint connection (separating just above the reel seat) rather than a center-cut design. Since downshot fishing demands fighting large flatfish or seabass in strong currents, a butt-joint design is optimal for preserving maximum backbone strength and a natural blank action curve.

​

The tip section features a total of 12 guides. The spacing is tight near the tip and widens toward the butt, which distributes load evenly and preserves a natural action curve.

The tip diameter is 1.053mm, the thickest part of the tip section is 7.204mm, and the butt diameter is 9.621mm. Given that standard downshot rods have tip diameters around 1.8mm, the tip on this rod is exceptionally thin. The mid-section and butt diameters are also thinner than average. Achieving such a thin profile while preserving strength confirms that they utilized high-elasticity carbon blanks.

Like the tai rubber rod, the balance on this downshot rod is excellent. Because downshot rods support heavy size 40 sinkers that pull the tip down, manufacturers rarely optimize rod balance. However, AOMEX successfully positioned the center of gravity just ahead of the reel seat.

​

It features a 6:4 action curve, leaning toward a moderate-fast bend. Although the tip is twice as thin as average downshot rods, it remains crisp. Under load, it transitions smoothly into a 6:4 curve. Once the load is removed, the blank recovers quickly, highlighting the qualities of a high-elasticity carbon blank.
