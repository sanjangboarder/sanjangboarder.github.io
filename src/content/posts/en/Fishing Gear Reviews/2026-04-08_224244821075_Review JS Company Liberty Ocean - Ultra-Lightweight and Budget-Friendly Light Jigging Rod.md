Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Spring has arrived, cherry blossoms are blooming, and reports of flatfish (halibut) are coming out of the Incheon area. I have a bait-fishing trip scheduled for this weekend and will pack my flatfish downshot gear as well.

​

For West Sea boat fishing targeting species like flatfish, rockfish, sea bass, greenling, and white croaker, light jigging rods (commonly called downshot rods) are the primary choice. Since we fish with 100g to 180g (30–50호) sinkers, a single rod can target a wide variety of species. Today, I review JS Company's new light jigging rod, the **Liberty Ocean**.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDhfMTYx/MDAxNzc1NjA2NzQzMDc6.CAQIzlKg6Q7V_rKZCXdBUUnSgRBCsFD1rGwxik-GaoQg.NCPQPNQExQ9T7STHP6jPles6eKP-7sNEAJzoB7IVfaIg.JPEG/SE-85f70ece-324c-11f1-ae93-cbc59fe5b46e.jpg?type=w800)

### Liberty Ocean B61-4

​

The 4-power model carries a recommended sinker rating of 110g to 225g (30–60호). I plan to use it primarily with 150g to 180g (40–50호) sinkers. It serves as a versatile rod for rockfish, flatfish, sea bass, and white croaker. The higher power rating provides solid control when fighting large flatfish or seabass, covering nearly all light jigging requirements in the West Sea.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfNTAg/MDAxNzc1Mzc1Mjk3ODU5.z_i-o2YWzd0fAqi4mHj1aXMklqWg3bLEcV9FnDQw7nUg.u_nr8LQ1ob1JBk0gVzLdRtinQHPTw0ZbA5_uED_sB-wg.JPEG/SE-3afa8f9f-30bc-11f1-b5df-ddafd279b873.jpg?type=w800)

​

For a light jigging model, the weight is competitive: the tip section weighs 13.8g, and the butt section weighs 90.2g, producing a total weight of 104g. This is 1g lighter than the catalog specification. Minor variances of 1–2g are standard because of epoxy and adhesive volume variations during assembly.

​

The rod saver weighs 16.1g. Operating without the rod saver reduces rod weight to 104g, which feels light. With the saver attached, total weight is roughly 120g, which remains comfortable.
