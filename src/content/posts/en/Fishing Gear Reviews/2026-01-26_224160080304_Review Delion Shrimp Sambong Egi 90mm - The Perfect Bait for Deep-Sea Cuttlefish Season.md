Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

For anglers who target cephalopods during the winter season, a highly popular technique has emerged in the South Sea and Jeju over the past few years: **deep-sea cuttlefish boat fishing**. Recent catch reports indicate that the Jeju deep-sea cuttlefish season has entered its peak. In the South Sea (departing from Goheung, Yeosu, or Samcheonpo toward Geomundo or Baekdo), offshore trips are heavily dependent on winter weather, but that fishery is also expected to become active shortly.

​

Rather than taking the longer trips to the South Sea, I am planning a cuttlefish trip to Jeju. Because this trip requires specialized tackle, I looked for new jigs (*egis*) to supplement my gear. I obtained and reviewed the newly released **Delion Shrimp Sambong 90mm Egi** from **Tulbo Fishing**.

​

Many anglers are familiar with jigs from Tulbo Fishing, which offer solid quality and are effective for webfoot octopus, cuttlefish, spear squid, and common octopus. The new Delion release updates the classic *Sambong* (bait-wrapped) jig by combining it with the popular "real-shrimp" soft-leg styling.

​

The items arrived securely packaged to prevent damage, and I received two jigs of each colorway.

Examining the details of the Shrimp Sambong Egi, two features distinguish it from conventional bait-wrap jigs. First, it features soft silicone legs designed to mimic the natural movement of live shrimp. Second, it utilizes a large single hook instead of the standard double-hook crown.

A key design feature is the large-diameter single hook. It utilizes a high-strength carbon steel hook that curves back toward the body, which helps maintain tension and prevents cuttlefish from escaping. The single-hook configuration also makes it less prone to snagging, which is useful when targeting common octopus near structure.

When securing the bait with the wire, care is required to avoid wrapping the silicone legs under the wire, as this restricts their movement. I recommend wrapping and preparing your jigs before heading onto the boat rather than doing it on the water.

The weight ranges between 16g and 16.6g, with minor variations depending on the wire weight. While relatively heavy for a 90mm jig, the larger profile matches the size of deep-sea cuttlefish and the sinking speed remains controlled.
