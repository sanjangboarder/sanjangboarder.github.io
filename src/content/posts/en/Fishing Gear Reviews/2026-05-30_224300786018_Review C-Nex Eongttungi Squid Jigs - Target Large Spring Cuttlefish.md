Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

The West Sea spring cuttlefish season has commenced, and I am planning a cuttlefish trip out of Ocheon Port tomorrow. While I have reviewed several C-Nex squid jigs recently, today I focus on a model I have yet to cover in detail: the Eongttungi.

​

C-Nex manufactures its jigs in domestic factories. Their three primary lines are the Chokkomi, the Silver-Scale Anchovy, and the Eongttungi. They also produce the Mekkeuni model. In terms of popularity, the Chokkomi is widely known, followed by the Eongttungi, the Silver-Scale Anchovy, and the Mekkeuni.

​

The Eongttungi serves as the standard-sized version of the Chokkomi. If we classify jig sizes as Small, Normal, and Large: the Chokkomi sits in the Small category, while the other models represent the Normal size. Large sizes (roughly 12cm in length) are rarely used in modern setups.

​

Normal-sized jigs are preferred when targeting large spring cuttlefish (*cannonballs*), octopus, spear squid, or spring webfoot octopus. Outside of early autumn (September to early October) when webfoot octopus and cuttlefish are small, the Normal size is suitable for most seasons, including winter deep-water cuttlefish trips.

​

This means the C-Nex Eongttungi is suited for spring cuttlefish in the West Sea, winter deep-water fishing in the South Sea, or mid-autumn cuttlefish trips. I plan to use a rotation of Eongttungi and Silver-Scale Anchovy jigs for my upcoming trip.
