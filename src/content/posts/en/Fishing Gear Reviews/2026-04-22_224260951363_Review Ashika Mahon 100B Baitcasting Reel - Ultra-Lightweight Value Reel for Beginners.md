​

The West Sea boat fishing season has officially begun, and I have been preparing new baitcasting reels for this year's trips. Today, I introduce a new ultra-lightweight baitcasting reel that I plan to use starting with this week's webfoot octopus (jukkumi) outing.

​

The product is the **Ashika Mahon 100B Baitcasting Reel**. It is a highly budget-friendly reel priced under 100,000 KRW.

​

The drive gear is constructed of 7075 aluminum (commonly known as duralumin), which represents a highly durable material choice for gear components. It features a 5.x:1 gear ratio and a max drag of 5kg, which is plenty of power to cover various West Sea boat fishing target species. The body utilizes a carbon frame combined with an aluminum spool. It is equipped with a 120mm carbon double handle, a drag clicker, and most importantly, weighs in at just 124g.

​

The packaging includes all the essential components. It includes a warranty card, a user manual, and a soft drawstring reel pouch—a welcome accessory at this price point. It also features the main reel body pre-equipped with a lightweight carbon double handle.

Measuring the weight, it registers at 124.5g. This represents an exceptionally light reel. When paired with a lightweight 70g boat rod, the total setup weight falls under 200g, providing massive advantages.

​

Measuring the body width across the thumb bar, it registers at 6cm. The width is noticeably narrower than standard baitcasting reels, providing a comfortable palming grip even for anglers with smaller hands.

The drag clicker provides loud, distinct click feedback when line is pulled out, delivering excellent feedback.
