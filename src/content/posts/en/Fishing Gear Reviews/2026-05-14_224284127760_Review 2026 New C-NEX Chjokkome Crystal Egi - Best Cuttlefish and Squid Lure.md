Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

The 2026 spear squid (han-chi) season in the South Sea has officially begun, and in the West Sea, we are approaching the large cuttlefish (gap-o-jing-eo) season. I am planning a spear squid trip to Tongyeong this year and have a boat cuttlefish trip scheduled for June in the Chungnam region.

​

I have a strong interest in egis, which are the core tackle for cephalopod fishing. Recently, I researched egi colors, phosphorescent glow, and UV-reactive Keimura properties using Google's NotebookLM. I plan to share the detailed findings in a separate post, but the core takeaway is that egi colors, luminous glow, and UV-reactive Keimura have a major impact on cephalopod catches.

​

Consequently, I have been examining egi color patterns and glow responses much more closely. Only a handful of manufacturers produce egis directly in Korea, and **C-NEX** is one of them. Under their popular "Chjokkome" lineup, they focus heavily on high-quality paint finishes, color layouts, luminous glow, and Keimura properties. They have finally released their new 2026 Chjokkome Crystal colors, which place a strong focus on Keimura UV response. In this post, I detail these two new colors.

​

In spear squid and cuttlefish fishing, a bright glow is not always the best choice. In spear squid fishing, boat lights emit UV rays, which trigger the Keimura response under the water to attract squid. Because squid often show a stronger preference for Keimura over standard phosphorescent glow, many squid-specific lures include Keimura coatings.

​

Because squid preferences for Keimura vary from day to day, carrying a variety of egis is critical. Packing lures like these Crystal models, which deliver distinct Keimura responses depending on UV exposure, helps manage changing conditions.

​
