Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Although the webfoot octopus (jjo-kko-mi) season is still a bit away, the traditional favorite color for octopus and cuttlefish is undoubtedly "Gochujang" (named after the red chili paste, featuring a red head and white body). This color performs exceptionally well during the early season and serves as a core part of any lure rotation. Today, I'm reviewing and comparing the different Gochujang color variants of the **C-NEX Chjokkome Egi**.

​

In octopus and cuttlefish fishing, "Gochujang" refers to a color scheme featuring a red head and a white-based body. It is the absolute standard lure color. Because it is highly versatile, performing well in both clear and turbid water regardless of depth, keeping it in your rotation is always a safe bet. However, when looking closely at these red-head egis, you'll find subtle design differences.

​

Below are the 2024 and 2025 C-NEX Chjokkome Gochujang models. They are categorized into "Natural" and "Laser" styles, which look similar but carry slight differences.

The C-NEX hooks are extremely sharp, ensuring solid hooksets with minimal rod movement. They are made of semi-soft steel rather than rigid carbon steel, making it easier to pull them free from bottom snags with a firm, steady pull.
