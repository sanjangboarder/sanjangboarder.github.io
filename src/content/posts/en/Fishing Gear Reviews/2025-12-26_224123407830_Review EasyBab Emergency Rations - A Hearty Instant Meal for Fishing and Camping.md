Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Hot meals or snacks enjoyed during outdoor excursions are generally satisfying due to the outdoor atmosphere. When hungry during sea fishing, camping, or hiking, cup ramen is my primary choice. However, there are times when a rice-based dish is preferred.

​

When lure fishing in remote locations where finding a restaurant is difficult, I resolve meals by using a portable camp stove to boil water in my car, or by using hot water from public facilities to prepare military-style MREs. Today, I introduce a product designed for these scenarios.

​

It has been over 20 years since I was discharged from military service. During my service, I worked as a supply clerk, which provided me with familiarity with military rations. In the South Korean Army, there were two primary types of rations: Type 1, which was a ready-to-eat wet pack with integrated heating elements, and Type 2, which was a dehydrated freeze-dried ration requiring hot water.

​

Type 1 featured greasy options that I preferred, whereas Type 2 required adding hot water and occasionally resulted in undercooked rice grains. However, the freeze-dried type had its own appeal when properly prepared.

​

While I researched commercial versions of both configurations, only the Type 2 dehydrated freeze-dried options are readily available for retail purchase. I purchased a set last year and ordered another batch recently.

​

The brand is **EasyBab**, and the series is titled **"March of the Emergency Rations"** (진격의 비상식량). I ordered a variety pack of ten, which includes disposable spoons. The kit offers four flavors.

Preparation is straightforward. You fill the pouch with hot water up to the marked line and wait 10 minutes. Because it contains rice rather than noodles, it requires longer preparation times than instant cup noodles. Opening the pouch too early results in hard, undercooked rice grains. I recommend waiting the full 10 minutes. The flavor is standard.

​

My preferred preparation method is to pair the emergency ration with a cup of ramen. Start by pouring water into the emergency ration first, then fill the cup ramen. The ramen will cook in 3 minutes. While eating the noodles, the 10-minute timer for the rice will finish.
