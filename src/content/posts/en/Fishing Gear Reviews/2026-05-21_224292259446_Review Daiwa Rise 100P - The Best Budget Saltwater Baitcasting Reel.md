Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I purchased a few new baitcasting reels this year. Today, I share my review of one of them.

​

This is the **Daiwa Rise 100P**, a new release from Daiwa. Shipping was prompt, and the packaging was protected.

​

The red box details the key specifications of the RIZE 100P: a 5.3:1 gear ratio, a weight of 185g, 5.5kg of drag power, 53cm of line retrieve per handle turn, and an 11-bearing layout.

​

At under 190g, it qualifies as a lightweight baitcasting reel. The 5.3:1 gear ratio is suited for West Sea boat fishing targets, covering flatfish (*flounder*), sea bass, rockfish, white croaker, red seabream, webfoot octopus, and cuttlefish.

Opening the side cover reveals the Rise-specific spool. The internal frame is machined from aluminum, which provides a rigid and durable platform.

​

The Rise utilizes 11 corrosion-resistant ball bearings, including two in the spool assembly, two on the pinion gear, two on the drive shaft, one on the worm shaft, and four in the double handle knobs.

To measure individual weights, I removed the double handle. The star drag assembly uses a spring clicker plate; keep track of these two components during assembly and maintenance. This spring system produces the clicking sound when adjusting the star drag. The internal drive gears are moderately lubricated from the factory.
