Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

While beginner anglers rarely carry them, fishing forceps are a tool that experienced saltwater anglers often purchase. Although some anglers use pliers as a substitute, dedicated forceps are useful in many situations.

​

I recently purchased the **Haedong Titanium Straight Forceps** to replace two pairs I lost on previous trips.

​

I wanted a higher-end option and chose the titanium-plated straight model from Haedong. The tool uses saltwater-resistant stainless steel as a base material and is coated with a titanium plated finish.

​

Because forceps are exposed to saltwater, rust resistance is critical. Stainless steel resists corrosion in saltwater, and this model features a full-body titanium rainbow finish. The HDF Haedong logo is engraved in the center.

Sizing ranges from Size 4 to Size 8. Based on my experience, the largest size is the most practical. The Size 8 model measures approximately 20cm in length and weighs 51g. The weight details a solid feel, and the build quality is durable.

The primary application for these forceps is removing hooks from deep-hooked rockfish, flatfish, or greenling. A longer shaft simplifies hook extraction when a fish has swallowed the hook deep. Without forceps, removing deep hooks is difficult.

​

​

The forceps can also serve as an alternative to a lip gripper. A larger size is required to handle larger species securely. While I carry a dedicated lip gripper, I use these forceps to manage hook removals, and find them essential for safety on the water.

The Haedong Titanium Straight Forceps are a reliable option among available models. Budget forceps are more susceptible to saltwater corrosion and can bend under pressure. Investing in a durable pair is recommended.
