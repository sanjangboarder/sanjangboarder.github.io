Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

As you may know, I frequently visit Seonjae Fishing Park during the winter season. When targeting winter rockfish (*Korean rockfish / 우럭*), there are a few standard soft plastics that most anglers carry. The most popular option is the **Half Lure Yamadesu 2**, followed by the **OMG No-Sinker King (Geoshigi Worm)** as primary choices, and the **Berkley Swimming Mullet 4-inch** as a secondary option. Anglers also utilize standard grub worms.

​

Among these, Berkley worms are well-known for their strong scent attractant. Lures like the Swimming Mullet or the Gulp! Sandworm are effective from spring through autumn, largely due to this scent. However, large-profile scented worms suitable for winter rockfish have been limited.

​

This year, Berkley, known for the Swimming Mullet, and Half Lure, known for the Yamadesu, released a collaboration product: the **Yamadesu 2 SP (Special PowerBait)**.

The Berkley collaboration worm costs approximately 1,100 KRW per unit, while the original Yamadesu 2 costs around 750 KRW. This represents a 30% price premium. Given that the Yamadesu 2 is already a mid-tier worm in terms of price, the premium may be a consideration for some anglers.

When targeting net cages at Seonjae Fishing Park, the common configuration is to position the hook eye toward the tail and insert a nail sinker (*insert sinker / 삽입싱커*) into the nose. This shifts the center of gravity forward, which aids in skipping casts and allows the lure to glide away from the angler during the fall.

​

I used these lures on two subsequent outings to Seonjae Fishing Park. On the first trip, I did not observe a performance advantage compared to the original Yamadesu 2. The Berkley scent attractant did not appear to produce a notable difference in the bite rate.

​

This assessment remained consistent during my second trip two weeks later. Scent attractants gained popularity in cephalopod fishing this season; while they may have some effect, they often function as a confidence factor for the angler rather than significantly increasing strikes.

​

​

The Berkley PowerBait x Half Lure Yamadesu 2 SP is a well-manufactured soft plastic that offers good casting distance, skipping ability, and back-sliding action. However, whether it justifies the 30% price premium over the original Yamadesu 2 remains open to question. While I will continue to use them through the winter season, I would likely purchase the original version for better value in the future.
