Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

About three weeks ago, Oslo offered a limited pre-order run of their 39g webfoot octopus and cuttlefish rod, capping it at approximately 200 units.

​

The pre-order was scheduled to ship around November 5. After a shipping delay, the date was adjusted to November 12-13. The package was eventually shipped on November 14 and arrived on November 15.

​

I had scheduled a webfoot octopus trip on November 15 to test the rod on the water, but because the delivery arrived later that day, I was unable to use it. Since additional trips are unlikely this late in the season, I am sharing an initial review based on its design, weight, and specifications.

​

**Self-Purchased Verification**

*   **Product:** 39G (1-Piece) / Case: None
*   **Status:** Confirmed purchase, November 2025.

​

Oslo is a manufacturer focused on trendy, lightweight rods. I ordered the 39g model during the limited pre-order event for 198,000 KRW, which remains its current listing price.

​

I chose the package without an extra hard case since I have several already. The rod arrived in a cardboard shipping tube containing a soft protective sleeve.

The package contains the rod itself and a tip protective cover.

​

The rod utilizes a 1-piece design with an overall length of 150cm. While a 1-piece configuration is less convenient to transport than a multi-section rod, it was likely chosen to minimize weight. Prior to this release, one of the lightest commercial options was the NH Korea Dark Air at 49g. A 2-piece setup around a similar weight might have offered a practical alternative.

The actual weight of the rod measured 37.1g, which is lighter than the model name indicates. This light weight helps offset the forward balance. When paired with an ultra-light baitcasting reel (like the NH Korea Core Solid with a carbon double handle upgrade), the combined assembly weight is 168.5g. Given that standard baitcasting reels alone weigh around 160g, matching this total weight is notable.

We measured the blank thickness using a digital caliper. The tip measures 0.774mm, close to the specified 0.7mm, a thickness enabled by the metal tip construction. The blank directly above the reel seat measures 7.755mm, and the thickest section at the butt measures 8.276mm.

​

The blank remains thin for a webfoot octopus rod.

The bend profile of the rod is moderate to slow. While advertised as an 8:2 fast action rod, it feels more moderate than comparable 8:2 options, tending toward a 7:3 action. The midsection flexes easily, suggesting the blank does not utilize high-modulus carbon.

​

Due to the late delivery, I have not had the opportunity to fish the Oslo 39G. I generally prefer fast action (9:1) rods for webfoot octopus and cuttlefish, though moderate action rods can be effective once adjusted to the different feel. I plan to use this rod next season to evaluate its moderate action performance.

​

While 50g rods were common last year, 40g options have been popular this season. The Oslo 39G represents an early entry targeting the upcoming season, reflecting the ongoing reduction in tackle weights.
