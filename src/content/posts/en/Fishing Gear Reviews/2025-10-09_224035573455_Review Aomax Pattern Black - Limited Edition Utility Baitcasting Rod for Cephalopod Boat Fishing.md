Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

At the Leisure Fishing Expo held in Songdo at the end of August, Aomax introduced a limited edition rod named **Pattern Black**. This rod was designed in collaboration with the fishing YouTube creator *Gamdung-a Nolja*, which raised my interest, and I had the opportunity to evaluate it. I used this rod during three recent cuttlefish and webfoot octopus boat trips.

​

​

The Pattern Black B-183 Utility Edition is not classified specifically for webfoot octopus, cuttlefish, or squid. The "Utility" designation indicates its versatility. In my use, it functioned as an all-round rod for various boat eging methods.

​

The rod specifications indicate the use of a titanium tip and high-strength graphene nano fabric. While the tip diameter is typical for titanium-tipped rods, the butt diameter of 8.13 mm is relatively slim for a boat eging rod. This suggests a moderate-action profile, which was confirmed in use. The sinker load rating is listed as size 10 to 30, with size 20 providing the best balance in my tests.

The manufacturer does not specify the carbon modulus of the blank. According to the designer's YouTube channel, it is a blend of 24-ton, 30-ton, and 40-ton carbon. Combined with the graphene components, this mix suggests higher durability than rods built exclusively with high-modulus (40–46 ton) carbon, resulting in a moderate-action rod rather than a stiff one.

​

Under load, the rod bends throughout the entire tip section rather than flexing only at the titanium tip. While typical cuttlefish rods have a 9:1 action and webfoot octopus rods have an 8:2 action, the Pattern Black has an action of approximately 7.5:2.5.

The 7.5:2.5 action allows the blank to flex continuously starting from the midsection. The profile is closer to a shore eging rod than a typical stiff boat rod. This is a distinct design direction.

The moderate action supports jigging motions (shakree). While it felt less direct initially, continued use showed that the moderate action helps detect subtle weight changes when cephalopods touch the jig.

​

I am still adapting to the Aomax Pattern Black. It may present a learning curve for beginners. However, for intermediate and advanced anglers who understand its moderate-action properties, it serves as a specialized tool. While tested on webfoot octopus and cuttlefish, it has characteristics that make it suitable for squid metal and deep-sea cuttlefish outings in Jeju.

​

It functions as a versatile rod, though it may lack some of the specialized benefits of dedicated single-species rods. The design is suited for anglers who prioritize the feel of jigging actions.

​

The design concept appears focused on providing a responsive feel during casting and jigging rather than maximizing raw efficiency. It represents a different option for experienced anglers seeking a rod with moderate action.

​

My catch rates during the test outings were modest, as the trips coincided with low-activity days. I expect its characteristics would be more apparent during high-activity feeding windows. I plan to continue using this rod during the remainder of the autumn season.
