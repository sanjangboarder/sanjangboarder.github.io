In my previous post, I introduced the budget-friendly Tomato 2 baitcasting reel. I ordered a braided line suited for this reel at the same time. The length matched the spool capacity, the price was reasonable, and the line performed well during my initial tests, so I am sharing my evaluation here.

​

When choosing a braided line for the webfoot octopus and cuttlefish season, I generally recommend sizes between 0.6 and 1.0. For sensitivity, the thinnest option, size 0.6, is the most effective. However, it has lower breaking strength, which can result in frequent loss of jigs and sinkers.

​

While high-strength 0.6 braids exist, a thin line is physically limited in strength. To balance sensitivity and gear security, anglers often choose size 0.8, or size 1.0 if using expensive jigs. Because I invest significantly in quality jigs, I opted for size 1.0 to prioritize gear retention, setting up my baitcasting reels with a mix of 1.0 and 0.8 lines.

​

I chose the **Sunline Basic PE Braided Line size 1.0**. Let's examine the packaging first. Despite the low price point, the packaging quality is consistent with other standard lines, offering clear product details.

A notable specification is the 200 m length for size 1.0, which is generous. The maximum strength is listed at 7.7 kg, which is a moderate rating. The label indicates it is a 4-strand braid.

​

While many modern braided lines feature 8-strand, 9-strand, or 12-strand construction to prioritize smoothness and thin diameters, this line uses a 4-strand structure to maintain a budget-friendly price point.

Measuring the line's thickness yielded 0.14 mm, which is thinner than the typical 0.16 mm diameter standard for size 1.0 braided lines. This thinner profile likely contributes to the smooth texture.

​

Having used this line on several boat trips during the webfoot octopus and cuttlefish season, I found it delivers sufficient strength for cephalopod boat fishing. The size 1.0 line does not break prematurely under normal load, though it is not noticeably stronger than comparable alternatives.

​

In summary, it offers adequate strength for cephalopod boat fishing. For shore casting or lure fishing, the strength might feel slightly modest, but the thinner profile and relatively smooth texture for a 4-strand braid provide advantages in casting distance. It represents a practical budget-friendly option.
