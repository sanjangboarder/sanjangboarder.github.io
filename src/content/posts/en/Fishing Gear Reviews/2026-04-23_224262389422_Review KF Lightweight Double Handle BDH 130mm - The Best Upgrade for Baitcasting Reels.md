Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

The West Sea boat fishing season has started, beginning with rockfish trips in April. When it comes to charter boat baitcasting reels, swapping out the stock handle for a double handle is an easy and effective upgrade. To improve the reeling feel of my new baitcasting reel, I purchased a lightweight double handle.

​

In this post, I introduce the **KF Lightweight Double Handle (BDH-130mm)** sourced from Assa Fishing. They also retail the Ashika Mahon budget ultra-lightweight baitcasting reel that I reviewed recently. They offer a range of practical tackle accessories. The shipping was prompt, and the packaging was configured to prevent damage during transit.

The model name is the **KF Baitcasting Reel Double Handle BDH-130mm**. It is available in Standard and Premium variants with multiple color options. I selected the Premium Line model.

KF stands for Korea Fishing. The packaging lists compatibility with most baitcasting reel brands, and is well-finished.

The kit provides dedicated cap nuts for Shimano and Daiwa shafts, available for both right-hand (RH) and left-hand (LH) reels. It includes a lock plate, a nut spinner wrench, a hex key, shims for tolerance adjustment, and a Shimano shaft spacer.

​

Because most brands follow Daiwa's shaft standard (with Shimano being the exception), you only need to select the correct RH/LH cap nut to complete the installation on non-Shimano reels.

I measured the dimensions. The knob diameter is 30mm, and the center-to-center handle length is 130mm. The weight is 47.7g. While 120mm is common for modern handles, this 130mm length is designed to increase leverage for a lighter reeling feel.

​

The swap is straightforward. Remove the retainer plate screw, and loosen the center nut using the included wrench spinner or a dedicated reel wrench.
