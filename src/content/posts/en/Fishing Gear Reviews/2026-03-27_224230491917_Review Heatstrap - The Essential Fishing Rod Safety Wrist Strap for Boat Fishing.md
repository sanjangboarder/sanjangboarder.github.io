​

The concept addresses a common issue for boat anglers. Mid-range and premium rods generally cost over 300,000 KRW, and even budget options retail around 200,000 KRW. Anyone who has accidentally dropped a rod overboard can understand the value of securing the rod to your wrist.

​

​

During the 2025 season, I lost a premium rod overboard while setting the hook in the waters off Mageompo. Anglers typically drop their rods due to fatigue or carelessness. On that trip, we had reached the final point. It had rained all day, and I removed my wet gloves at the end of the session to pack up my gear.

​

🔗 [JS Company Charm Air Webfoot Octopus/Cuttlefish Rod (Naver Purchase)](https://naver.me/FhupTU2Q)

My rig caught on a bottom snag. When I flicked the rod to clear it, my bare hand slipped on the wet handle, and the rod fell into the water. Losing gear during a brief moment of packing up was frustrating. After that incident, I realized the utility of a safety tether.

​

I saw this safety strap at the Korea International Fishing Show at Kintex in Ilsan, and decided to purchase it.

The Heatstrap wraps around your wrist as shown below. It prevents the rod from falling to the bottom; even if you release your grip entirely from the handle, the rod remains suspended.

​

Adjusting the strap tension stabilizes the rod against your forearm, which helps distribute the weight load and reduce wrist fatigue.

​

If you prefer a looser fit, setting a moderate tension still provides a buffer to catch the rod if it slips. For anglers using high-end gear, adding this strap is a practical precaution.
