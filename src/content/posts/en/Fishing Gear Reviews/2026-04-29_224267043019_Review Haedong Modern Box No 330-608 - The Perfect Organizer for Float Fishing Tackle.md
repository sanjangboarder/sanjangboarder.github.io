Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

In this post, I introduce a tackle accessory that I use when going float fishing on shore rocks or at saltwater fishing ponds. Unlike lure fishing where you tear open soft plastic bags as you go, float fishing requires managing a range of rigging components. As a result, organizing them in a tackle box is preferred. Today, I showcase a box suitable for float fishing rigs.

​

The product name is the **Haedong Modern Box No. 330-608 (HT-1034)**.

​

Examining the size compared to my hand, it is larger than a standard container for jig heads or small 2-to-3-inch worms. It is sized to accommodate squid jigs (*egi*), metal jigs over 30g, or Tairaba rigs.

My rig configuration remains similar when visiting saltwater fishing ponds or platforms. Carrying this single Modern Box covers my rigging needs.

​

I prefer using swivel sinkers over egg sinkers, relying on Size 1.0 and 2.0 weights. These fit in the compartments alongside night-fishing light sticks. I use electronic stem floats, which fit in the long compartments. My preferred Haedong Master float fits inside as shown below. Battery packs fit without removing their retail card packaging.

While novice float anglers often use accessories directly from their retail packaging, organizing them in a compact tackle box like the Haedong Modern Box is more convenient. Sorting gear before a trip is part of the fishing experience, and choosing a mid-sized organizer is recommended.
