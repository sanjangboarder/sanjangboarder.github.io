Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

More boat captains in the West Sea are scheduling spring webfoot octopus (jukkumi) trips. However, water temperatures remain low, with experienced anglers landing barely double-digit catches. Given the high price of spring octopus, buying it to eat is expensive.

​

I plan to book a spring octopus trip once water temperatures rise. Spring octopus is highly sensitive compared to the fall season, and getting bites is difficult, making it critical to optimize your presentation. To increase bite frequency, I purchased a UV charger flashlight.

​

The product is the **HDF Pinatura Solar UV Charger Flashlight**. While UV charger lights are uncommon, most cuttlefish and squid anglers carry large, cup-style charging boxes. I own one as well, but because of its bulk, it stays packed away. I wanted a lightweight, portable UV charger and selected the Pinatura Solar UV light. It features rapid 0.5-second charging and supports solar charging. Flashlights can be tedious to keep charged, and solar recharging resolves this issue.

UV flashlights are highly useful when testing Keimura (UV-reactive) properties rather than standard phosphorescent lures. When UV light is shone on a Keimura-treated lure, it glows brightly, as shown below.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDZfOTMg/MDAxNzc1NDYyMTk1NDY1.RuSqAtVbdoKKy4ACZt9j-BZtxJYalJOAB5ApERn26dsg.y0s40hllABIpGLnPbMWfi8UNRX67Q0yfoFag-SdnyxQg.JPEG/SE-27144bd6-2fba-11f1-b5c5-2fd3c9899ab2.jpg?type=w800)

​

Keimura-coated lures do not store light energy to emit glow in the dark like phosphorescent paint. Instead, Keimura react to ultraviolet light present in water. Shining a UV light on your lures is simply a method to confirm if they carry Keimura coatings.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDRfMTc3/MDAxNzc1MjYxMjA4MTAx.ooxV3-NIkkzSOYU7cS-lxlKR5u64HyzhQeHLEYoYb-8g.l_LjgbAvmviAgn6D1PQCV4AyRSo0dW_4yMPNtFTIRpcg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260403%EF%BC%BF213410.jpg?type=w800)

​

For glow-in-the-dark (phosphorescent) lures, UV light provides the fastest charging rate. When charged with UV light, the phosphorescent parts glow intensely once the light is turned off, and reactivating the UV light shows the Keimura response.
