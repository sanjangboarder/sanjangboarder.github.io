Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

As the fishing season draws to a close, it is time to prepare and clean tackle for storage. While I use a salt neutralizer to remove salt deposits and prevent rust on my reels and rods, one persistent issue has remained: fish odor.

​

Odors from bait and fish with high oil content, such as Spanish mackerel, largehead hairtail, and chub mackerel, tend to linger. Even cephalopods like webfoot octopus, cuttlefish, and octopus leave a distinct fishy scent on hands after cleaning.

​

Standard soaps require repeated scrubbing and time to neutralize these odors, which is inconvenient. To address this, I purchased a specialized product line: the **Kuroshio Bye-Bye Fish Odor** hand wash and gear cleaning spray.

​

The package arrived safely, wrapped in protective bubble wrap.

​

Kuroshio is a brand known for polarized sunglasses and fishing apparel. I was interested to see their expansion into functional cleaning products with the Bye-Bye Fish Odor series.

​

The hand wash features a bubble-foam pump dispenser, similar to common household hand soaps. It is formulated to target organic fish odors, utilizing persimmon extract for deodorization and aloe vera extract to prevent skin dryness.

The grip handle retained a strong scent. I placed the tool in the sink, sprayed it thoroughly with the cleaning solution, and let it sit for approximately one minute.

​

After rinsing, the fish odor was eliminated, and the tool carried a fresh forest scent.

​

Using regular towels to dry the grip previously transferred the odor, but this treatment resolved the issue.

​

I also tested the spray on a small cooler bag. The cooler had acquired a fishy odor from holding cephalopod zip bags that leaked ink and mucus. After a quick water rinse, I added a shallow layer of water and sprayed the solution inside. After letting the foam sit for ten minutes, I rinsed it out. The odor was neutralized, leaving only a mild phytoncide scent.
