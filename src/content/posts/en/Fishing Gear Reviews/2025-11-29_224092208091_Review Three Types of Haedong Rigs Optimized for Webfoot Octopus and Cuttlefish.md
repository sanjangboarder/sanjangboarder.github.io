Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

The boat fishing season for webfoot octopus and cuttlefish is drawing to a close. During my trips in the West Sea region, I analyzed rig designs to identify configurations that simplify target presentation.

​

By the 2026 season, several optimized configurations have become commercially available, allowing anglers to purchase pre-made rigs easily. The diagram below illustrates a custom rig design I evaluated; while not commercialized in this exact form, it serves as a multi-purpose setup that accommodates cuttlefish, Omori-style presentations, and webfoot octopus by adjusting the branch line length.

​

Today, I review **three types of Haedong rigs** designed for cuttlefish and webfoot octopus: the **Cuttlefish T-Knot Ball Bearing Rig**, the **T-Type Ester Line Rig**, and the **Obama Knot Rig**.

### **1. Cuttlefish T-Knot Ball Bearing Rig**

​

This rig features a central rolling swivel connected directly to the main line, which splits the sinker dropper line and the branch line. The sinker dropper is set at 15cm, and the branch line is 60cm. Anglers can shorten either line by cutting and re-tying the leader to fit local conditions.

The rig is tied using No. 5 black hard nylon line. The black coloration is designed to minimize line visibility for cuttlefish, though it remains effective for webfoot octopus. The swivels and ball bearing swivels are high quality. The egi connection uses a standard No. 2 round snap without an integrated swivel; however, since 95% of modern egis include built-in swivels, this does not restrict performance.

This setup is configured for targeting cuttlefish. A 60cm branch line is suitable when tidal currents are running well; when currents slow or fish activity drops, shortening the branch line can improve bite detection.

For webfoot octopus, coupling a weighted octopus jig (Aeja) at the bottom with an egi on the branch line is effective. This configuration is useful during the early season. On days with fast tidal currents, shortening the dropper length helps keep the lures near the bottom.

​

This review covered three pre-made rigs from Haedong HDF designed for cuttlefish and webfoot octopus. Modern rigs for these species generally favor thinner leader lines—typically No. 4 (around 16lb test) carbon, Ester, or hard nylon—configured with a branch line presentation. This design trend is likely to continue. These pre-made options are suitable for beginner, intermediate, and advanced anglers looking to simplify rigging.

​

Because the current season has concluded, field demonstrations of these rigs are limited. I plan to use these configurations for spring egg-bearing webfoot octopus outings next season and will report on their field performance.
