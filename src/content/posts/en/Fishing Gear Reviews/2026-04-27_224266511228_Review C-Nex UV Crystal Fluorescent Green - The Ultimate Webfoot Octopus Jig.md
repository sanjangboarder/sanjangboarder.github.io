Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week, I booked a webfoot octopus boat trip out of Ocheon Port boarding the **Valkyrie**. While the skipper recommended standard red-head and laser jigs, I wanted to test the new C-Nex UV Crystal Fluorescent Green jig. Today, I share my field review and specifications for this model.

​

I chose to test this C-Nex UV Crystal Chokkomi jig during the spring egg-carrying webfoot octopus season because webfoot octopus showed a strong preference for UV Crystal models during last autumn's season. The new fluorescent green color is formulated to appeal to egg-carrying webfoot octopus, and I wanted to evaluate its performance.

​

The C-Nex Chokkomi Crystal egi series was introduced in 2025, and this fluorescent green option has been added to the 2026 lineup. The design details are shown below.

The hook points are sharp and feature a semi-soft temper. Very rigid hooks can cause gear loss on seabed snags; hooks that have moderate flexibility combined with sharp points are preferred for webfoot octopus and cuttlefish, and this model matches those characteristics.

The UV response of this crystal series is distinct. Under a UV torch, the body displays a bright glow. In cuttlefish and webfoot octopus fishing down to 30m, UV light penetrates the water column, and the Keimura response acts differently from standard night glow.

​

During the morning, most anglers on deck rigged red-head setups. While the red-heads drew hits, I continued with the fluorescent green jig and recorded the highest count on deck during the morning session. The spring webfoot octopus were sluggish, often emerging inside conch or clam shells rather than hitting aggressively—indicating a low activity state. The green jig produced bites despite these conditions.

​
