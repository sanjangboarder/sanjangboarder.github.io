Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

If you ask where the absolute hotspot for catching small hairtail (locally called *pulchi*) in Korea is, the majority of anglers will answer: the **Saemangeum Seawall**. Saemangeum is a massive fishing point that draws crowds of anglers around late August to early September when the hairtail lure fishing season kicks off.

​

In this area, a brand named *153 Workshop (153 Gongbang)* built its reputation starting with premium fishing gather-lights. They run a popular Naver Cafe packed with valuable seawall fishing tips, maps, and local updates, which I visit frequently ahead of the fall season.

🔗 [153 Workshop Official Community](https://cafe.naver.com/led153)

​

Last year, they released a dedicated Saemangeum hairtail rod and reverse-jig heads that performed beautifully. This year, they expanded their lineup by introducing a dedicated hairtail PE line: the **153 Workshop Mangeumi Long Cast X8**. As the name suggests, it is an 8-carrier braided line optimized to maximize casting distance.

​

Since Saemangeum hairtail size increases rapidly as the season progresses, anglers transition from ultralight spinning reels to standard-sized reels. Accordingly, the line was released in various sizes from PE 0.4 to PE 1.0. I acquired the PE 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 spools.

Why does the Mangeumi line place such emphasis on casting distance? Anglers who have fished the Saemangeum seawall will immediately understand.

​

The distance at which hairtail schools feed shifts daily. Sometimes they feed just 10–20 meters from the rocks, but often they feed at distances exceeding 50 meters. In these situations, whether you can cast your lure far enough to reach the feeding school dictates your success, making casting distance critical.

​

Factors that dictate casting distance include rod length and action, spool design, line diameter, line smoothness (coating), lure weight, and casting technique. Among these, the line-specific variables are diameter and slickness (coating quality).

​

The 153 Workshop Mangeumi Long Cast X8 was specifically formulated to optimize thickness and slickness. The packaging strips away unnecessary flashiness to minimize cost, focusing entirely on delivering maximum value.

The manufacturer's tensile strength ratings are 8.2kg for PE 1.0, 6.8kg for PE 0.8, and 5.5kg for PE 0.6. While I lack the laboratory gear to test this precisely, pulling the line manually showed no lack of strength compared to premium braids. Field tests will verify this, but the initial strength is promising.

​

I measured the actual diameter using a digital caliper accurate to 0.001mm. The thinner the line, the better the casting distance. The PE 1.0 measured 0.14mm, PE 0.8 measured 0.13mm, and PE 0.6 measured 0.114mm—which are slightly thinner than the official specs. It lacks the thick, rough profile of budget braids, and the smooth coating makes it feel even thinner.

While caliper measurements are useful, errors can occur depending on pressure, so I believe the ultimate test is spool capacity. I wound the line onto a Shimano Vanquish C2500S spool.

​

The C2500S is rated to hold exactly 150m of PE 0.8 line. Budget braids almost never fit entirely onto this spool because they are consistently thicker than rated. The Mangeumi Long Cast X8 looked thin enough to fit easily.

The Mangeumi Long Cast 8-carrier line prioritizes casting distance, accurate diameter, tensile strength, and water-repellent coating. While optimized for hairtail, its thin diameter and strength make it useful for other styles of fishing as well.

​

I plan to use it for seawall hairtail, lure fishing for flounder, webfoot octopus, cuttlefish, and rockfish at local sea fishing parks. For boat fishing starting in September, spooling the PE 0.8 or 1.0 will be highly versatile.

​

My only suggestion is that while I prefer multi-color braids (which change color every 10m to track depth and distance), this is only released in solid aqua blue. I hope a multi-color version is introduced in the future.

​

With the hairtail season just a month away, anglers are preparing their gear. I look forward to making long casts with the Mangeumi Long Cast X8 to target distant schools this season.
