Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Among West Sea boat fishing targets, my personal favorite is red seabream (*snapper*) tairaba fishing. Red seabream activity commences in the warmer waters of Gunsan, pushing sequentially north through Chungnam and into the Incheon area.

​

Incheon red seabream tairaba charters have already begun. While this fishery typically opens around June, exploration trips started in mid-May this year, and charter fleets are now running full tairaba schedules. Having booked a mid-season trip out of Yeongjongdo Island, Incheon, I am preparing my tackle.

​

My rods and reels are ready, and I have prepared a selection of sliding heads and skirts for this season. Today, I review the new tairaba sliding heads and **Boom Boom Skirts** from **NH Korea**.

​

NH Korea manufactures saltwater rods and lightweight baitcasting reels. They have now expanded their lineup to include tairaba terminal tackle.

First, let's examine the **NH Korea Tairaba Sliding Heads**. These are sliding-style heads for West Sea snapper. The lineup extends up to 200g, accommodating East Sea cod tairaba (*cod-rava*) applications. Since I am targeting West Sea snapper, I prepared 40g, 60g, 80g, and 100g sizes. Unless you are fishing deep zones in the East Sea or strong currents in the South Sea/Jeju, a 40g to 100g selection is sufficient.
