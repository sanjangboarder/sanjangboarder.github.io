Recently, I have been fishing at the Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone's Mini Float Pond, targeting rockfish with float setups. Today, I share a review of the float responsible for my catches: the **Haedong Master Wontu (Long-Cast) Electronic Float**.

​

To understand its utility, examine the configuration of the Seonjae Fishing Park Mini Float Pond. Casting from the platforms requires a casting distance of 30 meters to 40 meters to reach the fish zones. Standard stem floats lack the weight to reach this distance consistently.

​

The Haedong Master Wontu float is designed specifically to cover long casting distances. I will detail why it works well.

​

---

​

The key feature of the "Master Wontu" model is its long-cast capability. The float is weighted to support casting distances, with most sizes in the range weighing over 20g. For saltwater fishing ponds, sizes between 1.0 and 4.0 are preferred. Because they weigh over 20g, they achieve the necessary distance.

​

The Master Wontu operates as a self-standing float. In saltwater float fishing, stem floats are classified as semi-self-standing, non-self-standing, or self-standing. A self-standing float remains vertical even when the lead sinker rests on the seabed. While non-self-standing floats lay flat and semi-self-standing floats tilt to signal when the sinker is on the bottom, they require lighter bodies that reduce casting distance.

​

Choosing a self-standing float prioritizes casting distance. Although identifying when the sinker hits the bottom is more difficult, it allows anglers to intentionally drag the bait along the seabed. Because the float stem registers alignment as soon as the sinker suspends, monitoring the settling phase remains manageable.

​

The float measures approximately 18cm in length and weighs 20.4g (Size 2.0 model). This weight aligns with the specification, and is adjusted to balance the battery.

I use this float primarily because of its stem tip visibility. The top-light section sits high, making it visible at a distance. The build quality and finish are clean. The stem tip utilizes a thick rubber O-ring seal to prevent water ingress from damaging the battery compartment, maintaining buoyancy.

The LED tip is bright and compatible with both BR425 and BR435 batteries. During night sessions, it stands out clearly on the water. However, the tip structure is vulnerable to hard impacts. While regular casting and water entries do not affect it, avoid hitting hard structures. (If you require tips on repairing the stem tip, feel free to contact me).

The low center-of-gravity design concentrates weight at the base, stabilizing the float during casts. The float achieves a 30-meter casting distance with ease, and can reach 40 meters. This puts the artificial reefs at Seonjae Fishing Park within range.

​

The tall stem tip keeps the float visible at long range. Thru-floats (dome floats) can be difficult to spot at 30 meters without close concentration, which causes eye strain. While thru-floats can achieve longer distances, their utility decreases if they cannot be monitored.

These are the results from three trips to the Seonjae Mini Float Pond. Early in the season, I targeted the reef lines, while on the third trip I caught fish near the net cages. Accessing these points would have been difficult without long-casting capability.

​

I also use this float when targeting Spanish mackerel and rockfish at the Sihwa Seawall or Songdo. Its compact length makes it easier to handle than standard long stem floats.

​

It is suited for pairing with lure rods at Manjeong Mint Fishing Park on Yeongjongdo or the Seonjae Mini Float Pond, where rocket floats are restricted. If you find thru-floats difficult to monitor, this is a recommended alternative.
