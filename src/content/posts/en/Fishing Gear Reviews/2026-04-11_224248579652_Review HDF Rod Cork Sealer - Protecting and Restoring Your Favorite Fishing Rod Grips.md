Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Recently, I have been reviewing various maintenance accessories. After returning from trips or on my days off, I spend time cleaning, tightening, and servicing my setups. Today, I review an accessory designed to protect natural cork grips, which are commonly featured near the reel seat or rear grip of premium fishing rods.

​

The product is the **HDF Rod Cork Sealer** by Haedong Tackle. It is a functional care item that applies a protective barrier coat over natural cork materials.

​

Unboxing the product reveals a container resembling a nail polish bottle. It holds 18ml of sealer. Having used it, the volume is substantial and will last for a long time.

​

The instructions specify cleaning the cork surface, applying one or two thin coats, and allowing it to dry. The process is simple. The chemical composition is listed as a specialized urethane resin coating.

Looking through my rod rack, I found several rods featuring natural cork grips. I pulled out five, and will demonstrate the process on three of them.

​

---

​

The first model is a trout stream casting rod purchased from AliExpress, which I use for recreational pier fishing. While budget-friendly, it carries a premium finish and features natural cork on the butt section. After several trips, the cork showed noticeable staining from dirt and hand oils.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDRfMiAg/MDAxNzc1MjYxMTUyMDQ2.5IB5DHNbnaWpgKOmJFawaw4MG1UcEjo6JRQDfeCPCaMg.TEjJ8mRfziFR8vU1RByYKbQ3aks0jn4mNdo5zievEcMg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260403%EF%BC%BF215036.jpg?type=w800)

I cleaned the cork surface with a wet wipe to remove dirt before applying the sealer. The dirt wiped off easily.
