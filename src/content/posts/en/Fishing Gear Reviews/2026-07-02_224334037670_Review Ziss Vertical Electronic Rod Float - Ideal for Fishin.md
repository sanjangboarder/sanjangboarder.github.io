Hello, this is Sanjang Border.

​

In this post, I'm going to introduce an electronic stick float that's great for small-scale sea fishing spots like fee-based fishing platforms such as Samgilpo, Dobi-do area, Chang-ri or Dangam Port in Seosan, or Mint Sea Fishing Spot in Yeongjongdo, Incheon, Jeongseong Mini Fishing Spot, Manjeong Sea Fishing Platform, Seonjae Fishing Park Mini Float Spot in Seonjaedo, Incheon, Ganghwado Sea Fishing Spot, and Daebudo Dokdo Mini.

​

I first saw this electronic float earlier this year, so it probably hasn't been out for very long. Personally, whether it's a ball float or a stick float, I prioritize visibility when choosing a product, and for stick floats, I prefer non-self-standing or semi-self-standing types over self-standing ones.

​

A product that perfectly fit these criteria and really caught my eye was the ZISS Vertical Electronic Stick Float. I occasionally check the MOLTEC (ZISS) website to see new arrivals, and as soon as I saw it, I knew this was it, so I ordered it. Since I was ordering anyway, I got three of them, and if you look closely, you'll see I ordered size 2.

You can immediately tell that the ZISS VERTICAL Electronic Stick Float has a low-center-of-gravity design. Self-standing floats usually have a low-center-of-gravity design, so the float stands upright when it enters the seawater. That's why it seems that sizes up to No. 1, with lighter buoyancy, are self-standing, and they could make them non-self-standing from No. 2 onwards.

I mainly use stick floats from Haedong or ZISS. As far as I know, they come from the same factory, and I believe they produce the most trendy and high-quality floats in Korea.

​

This type of stick float doesn't achieve as much casting distance as rocket floats used in open-field fishing spots (noji yuteo style). However, considering its length (height), its structural low-center-of-gravity design causes the bottom part to fly first during casting, which helps achieve good casting distance. Therefore, it can generally be classified as a long-cast float.

It's well waterproofed, and most importantly, after-sales service (AS) is available. This type of float tip is rarely damaged by the impact of hitting the seawater. However, when we cast and retrieve it at rocky shores or fishing spots, it can sometimes break or get waterlogged if it hits rocks or the net cage structures at the fishing spot. If you're careful about that, durability won't be an issue. But when you go to a fishing spot and the net cages are the hot spot, how can you not cast there? You have to cast! If it breaks after casting, you can just send it in for AS.

One reason I chose size 2 is because it's non-self-standing, but also to help the hook sink a little faster from the fishing platform. And as shown in the image below, even when the rig is aligned, not only the orange part of the float tip is visible, but also the yellow fluorescent part, which gives it excellent visibility. The exposure of the yellow part is due to a slight excess buoyancy. If you attach a slightly heavier bait, this excess buoyancy will be accounted for. If you want a sensitive setup, it's good to eliminate the excess buoyancy, but using it as is won't make a big difference in fishing platforms or mini float spots.

I've already caught a lot of rockfish (ureok), sea bream (chamdom), and more with this one since spring this year. While it casts well and I'm satisfied with its visibility, one of the advantages of stick floats is their high sensitivity, but honestly, its sensitivity isn't outstanding. If you want to buy a highly sensitive stick float, products with a similar thickness from the float tip to the bottom are more advantageous than those with a low-center-of-gravity design.

​

In other words, the ZISS Vertical Stick Float is a product focused on enhancing visibility for long casts and securing casting distance, fitting the characteristics of sea fishing. It's safe to say it's a good product for use in fishing platforms or mini-type sea fishing spots where an extremely sensitive setup isn't necessary.
