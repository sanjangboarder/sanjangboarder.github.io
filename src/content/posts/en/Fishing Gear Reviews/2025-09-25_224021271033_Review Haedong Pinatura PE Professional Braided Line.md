I recently acquired a new ultra-lightweight baitcasting reel. Since I plan to use it for webfoot octopus and cuttlefish boat fishing, I evaluated options for a suitable braided line and selected the **Haedong Pinatura PE Professional Braided Line**.

‚Äã

Pinatura is a sub-brand of Haedong Tackle that focuses on lure fishing gear. This line is designed specifically for lure fishing. The raw fiber and braiding process are handled in Japan, while the packaging is processed in Korea.

‚Äã

The line is available in seven sizes: 0.3, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0. Since cuttlefish is the primary target starting in late September, I selected the Size 1.0 line. For webfoot octopus exclusively, I would have selected Size 0.8.

‚Äã

The specifications list the tensile strength of the Size 1.0 line at 20lb (9.2kg), which is high compared to typical Size 1.0 lines. The spool contains 150 meters of line, which is standard for spinning and baitcasting reels used in lure fishing. This size is suitable for shore sea lure fishing targeting Spanish mackerel, chub mackerel, rockfish, flatfish, and red seabream. For light game fishing, sizes from 0.4 to 0.6 are applicable.

‚Äã

My choice of Size 1.0 was influenced by the cost of cuttlefish rigs. A standard setup involving a sinker, rig, and jig costs approximately 10,000 KRW, rising to 20,000 KRW when utilizing tungsten sinkers or premium jigs. Losing a rig represents a notable expense.

‚Äã

Using Size 1.0 line provides the tensile strength to pull rigs free from bottom snags. When paired with jigs featuring soft hooks, the line strength is often sufficient to bend the hooks and recover the rig, making Size 1.0 a practical configuration for these sessions.

‚Äã

The line color is white. While multi-color lines are often used to gauge depth, I rely on counting the fall time of the sinker. A size 20 sinker typically reaches bottom in about 10 seconds in 10-meter depths, though current and wind introduce variations. This makes multi-color configurations less critical for my use.

The PE Professional is an 8-carrier braided line. The structure relies on eight braided filaments, which supports its high tensile strength.

‚Äã

Braided line diameters vary by manufacturer for a given size rating. I measured the line diameter using digital calipers with 0.001mm precision, recording a value of 0.123mm.

‚Äã

Standard Size 1.0 braided lines typically measure around 0.16mm in diameter. A measurement of 0.12mm indicates that this line is thin for its rating. A thinner line profile reduces water resistance, limiting line belly in strong currents and allowing the use of lighter sinkers. This supports the choice of a high-quality line.

Here is the line spooled onto the NH Korea Core Speed Air reel (103g). The reel spool specification indicates a capacity of 180 meters for Size 1.0 line.

‚Äã

Spoiling the full 150 meters of the Pinatura line left some clearance on the spool shoulder, which is my preferred spooling volume.

‚Äã

Spool capacities vary by manufacturer and line diameter. For a spool rated for 180 meters of Size 1.0 line, this is how the 150-meter spool of Pinatura line fits. For Size 0.8 line, a 200-meter length would be suitable.

The manufacturer indicates that the white PE Professional line features a three-stage water-repellent coating. It is a high-performance braided line that is applicable for sea lure shore casting alongside boat fishing.

‚Äã

The white color offers high contrast against the sea surface, which helps monitor line angles during boat drifts and manage lines on crowded decks. I am testing this setup during my upcoming Incheon session.
