Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Sea bass fishing has commenced in the Incheon area, with promising early catches reported. The arrival of sea bass season signals the start of the live bait boat fishing (*oe-su-jil*) season. Live shrimp is a highly effective "cheat key" bait at pay-ponds and boat setups.

​

*Note: For live bait fishing, water temperatures must remain above 16°C for live shrimp to function properly. At temperatures below 15°C, shrimp quickly become sluggish or faint. Around Incheon, ocean temperatures exceed 15°C by mid-May, while commercial pay-ponds reach suitable temperatures by early May. In mid-June, conditions are optimal for live shrimp bait without temperature concerns.*

​

While some anglers transport live shrimp in plastic tackle bags (*bakkans*) or Styrofoam containers, using a dedicated live bait cooler keeps them active for longer and simplifies transport.

​

Today, I review the **Haedong (HDF) New Multi Shrimp Cooler 7L**, exploring its features and suitability for live bait transport.

​

---

​

I previously transported live bait in small 9L coolers or EVA bags. The 9L coolers are slightly bulky for bait duty, while EVA bags fail to maintain stable water temperatures, leading to high bait mortality. To resolve this, I purchased the HDF New Multi Shrimp Cooler.

​

Shipping from Haedong (HDF) was prompt, and the packaging kept the cooler protected.
