Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

As you may know, I participate in many styles of sea fishing, including boat fishing, float fishing, and lure casting. As I get older, close-up vision challenges (presbyopia) can sometimes cause frustration on the water. Even setting vision issues aside, we often set up tackle in low-light conditions—during dawn or dusk feeding windows. Additionally, strong ocean winds can make simple rigging tasks unexpectedly frustrating.

​

This frustration is primarily caused by braided line (braid). Unlike monofilament, nylon, or fluorocarbon, braided line lacks stiffness and bends or collapses easily. Threading limp braid through rod guides, small float holes, or especially the narrow level-winder of a baitcasting reel can be highly difficult.

​

There is a simple tool designed to address these vision, low-light, and wind challenges: the **Guide-Through Line Threader**.

​

Once you try it, you will likely keep one in your tackle box.

​

The Guide-Through Line Threader by HDF (Haedong Tackle) utilizes a 0.6mm spring steel wire designed to slide smoothly through even micro-sized rod guides.

​

Another major challenge is threading braided line through center-hole floats (koo-mung-찌) for float fishing. If the float interior is dry, you can generally push the line through. However, if the rig breaks and you need to feed braid through a wet float, the task becomes highly difficult.

​

Anglers who have experienced this will understand the frustration.

​

Using the Haedong Guide-Through, you can easily thread the braid straight through the center of the float, as shown below. It is an essential tool for float fishing.

​
