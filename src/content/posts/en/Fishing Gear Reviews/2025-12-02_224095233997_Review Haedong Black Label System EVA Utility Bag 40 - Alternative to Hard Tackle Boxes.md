Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

When going boat fishing or lure fishing at Seonjae Fishing Park, most anglers carry a hard-shell tackle box as an essential gear piece. While hard tackle boxes are durable and offer customized internal organizers, they present two main drawbacks for my use: first, their internal storage volume can be restrictive, and second, they are heavy. To address these issues, I purchased the **System EVA Utility Bag** recently released by Haedong Tackle (HDF) to evaluate its functionality.

​

---

### **Unboxing and Features**

​

The official product name from HDF Haedong Tackle is the **Black Label System EVA Utility Bag 40**. It resembles a large bakkan (EVA gear bag) or a fish keep bucket at first glance, but it incorporates several design details that allow it to serve as a direct replacement for a hard tackle box.

​

It includes a shoulder strap, a carry handle, and a molded lid designed with recessed compartments to prevent drinks or terminal tackle from sliding off the top.

### **Field Review**

​

With the West Sea boat fishing season drawing to a close, I utilized this bag during my trips to Seonjae Fishing Park. I previously carried a standard fish keep bucket or a hard tackle box, and switching to the Haedong System EVA Utility Bag provided a functional upgrade.

​

I packed soft plastics, jig heads, shock leaders, and fish stringers in the main compartment. The large mesh pocket under the lid held my gloves, neck buffs, towels, power banks, and action camera cases.

​

As shown below, the internal volume remains open even after packing. I added a warm winter hat and a heated vest, and the bag accommodated them without issue.
