Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

When fishing in the summer, I protect my skin from direct sunlight by covering up. While I manage to avoid body sunburns, wearing a loose-fit neck gaiter (buff) that exposes my ears for breathability has a drawback: my ears end up sunburned.

​

After using ball caps and snapbacks last season and getting sunburned ears, I began looking for bucket hat or boonie hat configurations. Because having options is practical, I purchased this new release from Daiwa.

​

I prefer New Era hats due to their fit and construction. This model represents a collaboration between Daiwa and New Era.

​

The hat is priced slightly over 50,000 KRW. Because this qualified for free shipping, I ordered it alone. The package arrived in a large box, with the hat protected in standard plastic packaging.

The side features the New Era logo embroidery alongside a small utility pocket. The Daiwa logo is also embroidered on the opposite side. The side panel includes a mesh section.

​

This mesh section was the primary reason for my purchase. My existing bucket hats lack mesh, which can trap heat on warm days. The mesh band supports breathability and helps vent heat.

​

Another factor in this choice was size availability. Anglers with larger head sizes often face limited choices. This model is offered in L/XL size. I had tried the M/L size at a fishing show, but it was too tight.

​

I normally wear large-size snapbacks or ball caps, corresponding to military hat sizes 60 to 62. The L/XL size provides a comfortable fit for larger head sizes.

The hat includes a chin strap. While a hat blown off during shore fishing can be recovered, a hat lost to the wind on a moving boat is difficult to retrieve. The chin strap addresses this issue.

​

This is a functional feature for boat anglers. The ears remain covered, and the chin strap does not require heavy tightening, allowing for comfortable pairing with a neck gaiter.
