Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Recently, I have not posted reports featuring fish finders. My previous wireless fish finder developed durability issues, and as it was from an overseas brand, getting after-sales service was difficult, leaving it unused. Recently, Gosea Fishing released the **GOSEA Smart Fish Scanner** at a budget-friendly price, prompting me to acquire one.

​

The retail price on the Gosea Fishing Naver Store is approximately 150,000 KRW, which is reasonable compared to other wireless fish finders in the market.

​

Upon arrival, I unboxed and examined the device. The packaging is well-designed. The box displays the technical specifications of the GOSEA Smart Fish Scanner.

​

I tested the GOSEA unit during a shore lure fishing trip. The unit's weight is significant compared to standard lures. To cast it safely, I recommend using at least a Size 1.0 braided line. Casting the unit up to 20 meters with Size 1.0 braided line proved stable.

​

​

Because the weight exceeds the lure weight rating of most standard rods, the cast should rely on rod elasticity rather than quick whip actions. I tied the line to the top eyelet bolt as shown above, using a hairtail rod. Over ten casts, the setup performed reliably, provided long-distance casting was avoided.

​

​

To scan at greater distances, you can use the tidal current to drift the unit or set up a heavier rod and line combination. On the water, the unit floats with half the sphere exposed, as shown above. When retrieving the unit for scanning, slow retrieval is recommended. Since the sonar transducer is located at the bottom, rapid retrieval can cause the unit to wobble, resulting in inaccurate scans.

​

Based on field testing, the sonar successfully mapped depth and fish swimming layers. During the outing, although bottom-targeted actions produced no results, the sonar screen indicated that fish were moving primarily in the mid-water columns, allowing me to adjust my strategy to mid-water targets.
