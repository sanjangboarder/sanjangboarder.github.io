Today, I want to introduce a semi-floating monofilament line that is well-suited for Incheon saltwater pay-fishing ponds. While braided lines are dominant in marine lure casting, semi-floating monofilament lines remain highly popular for float fishing.

​

Specifically, most open-water pay-fishing ponds in the Incheon metropolitan area ban braided main lines for float fishing, restricting setups to floating or semi-floating monofilament lines. While a few smaller ponds permit braid, using a semi-floating main line ensures compliance with local rules across different destinations.

​

In southern coastal waters, float anglers commonly use light 1.0 to 2.0 (4–8LB) main lines. However, metropolitan pay-fishing ponds require heavier setups—typically 3.0 to 4.0 (12–16LB) lines. The fish stocked in these commercial ponds are large and hard-fighting, necessitating a 3.0 (12LB) fluorocarbon leader. To support this leader, using a 4.0 (16LB) main line is highly recommended.

Avoid overfilling the reel spool. Because the diameter of the packaged spool is larger than the reel spool, monofilament line carries memory that can cause it to spill off the spool. Spooling the line under firm tension is essential.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDRfMTY1/MDAxNzc1MjYxMjU4MTAw.wTbdfBEBZqfqpqB_tMaOdhR7FMsoUUQ9FmrKDJTXhGIg.La0N2FDnN9ShCGQ1wJ8ncsJ5xBp89Gwz07MQSUHBnKQg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260403%EF%BC%BF212426.jpg?type=w800)

​

To relax line memory, remove the reel spool and submerge it in warm water for 10 minutes. This allows the monofilament to settle and conform to the spool diameter, preventing line spills and tangles during casting.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDZfMTYy/MDAxNzc1NDYwOTg1MzY2.S-okUyeVPzyPGj9DvG6KXhViQ6tZJoSm5Hhili9ZI00g.A6ZJG1HdC14ZOMhvotNhbn09r-7opQfrPqfsxn5rddYg.JPEG/SE-48827e30-2fba-11f1-b5c5-71eb56889249.jpg?type=w800)

​

I tested the line on a recent trip to an Incheon pay-fishing pond. The warm-water treatment successfully prevented line spills. The line cast smoothly, and its tensile strength proved reliable. When snagged, the line broke cleanly at the 3.0 leader knot rather than the main line, which was a welcome improvement.

​

My previous main line frequently failed before the leader, and this setup resolved that issue. I look forward to using this HDF Triple line throughout the 2026 season.
