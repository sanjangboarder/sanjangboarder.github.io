Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Recently, I visited Seongsu-dong with my wife, which is a popular neighborhood in Seoul. While we were less interested in sightseeing, we wanted to find a good restaurant. My primary reference when looking for places to eat is the Michelin Guide, followed by TV features or local recommendations. During my search, I found a pork rice soup (*Dwaeji-gukbap*) restaurant listed in the Michelin Guide.

​

The restaurant is named **Anmok**. Having enjoyed authentic pork rice soup during a trip to Busan earlier this year, I was keen to visit.

​

The restaurant is located near Seongsu Station. While it is set back from the main Seongsu-dong Cafe Street, it remains within walking distance. It is accessible for visitors exploring Seongsu-dong.

​

Instead of a standard sign, the awning displays "Anmok x Boksundoga." The exterior displays plaques highlighting its Michelin Guide listings from 2024 through 2026.

The dining area is clean, with spacing between tables and dedicated single-seat counters. While pork rice soup joints often have a vintage, local feel, this interior is modern and minimalist.

​

Notably, about half of the customers on deck were international tourists.

For the pork rice soup, customers can select their preferred meat cut; I ordered the mixed option (*seok-eo*), which includes pork head meat and pork shank.

​

The broth is opaque and white, indicating a long-simmered, rich soup profile.

By the time we left, the waitlist had grown. On weekdays, wait times increase during peak lunch hours. When we departed before 12:00 PM, there were twenty parties waiting.

​

Arriving early is recommended.

​

I rate the Seongsu-dong pork rice soup restaurant Anmok at **4.3 out of 5 stars**. It is a restaurant I would revisit when returning to Seongsu-dong. While the prices are on the higher side, they are in line with local rents. Parking can be difficult in Seongsu-dong generally, though valet services are available. If you are looking for a clean, modern take on pork rice soup, this spot is worth visiting.
