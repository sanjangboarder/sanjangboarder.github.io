Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week, a furniture fair was held at Songdo Convensia. After exploring the exhibitions with my wife for about two hours, we looked for a nearby lunch spot and decided to visit a buckwheat noodle restaurant I had frequently noticed while driving past.

​

​

The restaurant is named **Samgyori Dongchimi Makguksu**. Seeing that it is the Incheon Songdo branch, it is a chain franchise. While I had not encountered the brand frequently, a quick search revealed they have several locations. The head branch is located in Gangneung—a spot I visited years ago. Happy to see it, we stepped inside.

​

*Tip: Exhibitions held at Songdo Convensia generally do not provide free parking vouchers. Parking fees inside the venue can be expensive, but dining at restaurants within Songdo Convensia usually secures you a 2-hour free parking voucher, which is helpful to know.*

​

The interior is clean. It has been about two or three years since I first noticed this restaurant and wanted to visit, but having rarely dined inside Songdo Convensia, this was my first time. The space is bright and airy with large picture windows, providing a comfortable dining environment.

My wife opted for the standard **Dongchimi Makguksu**, while I ordered the **Hoe (Raw Fish) Makguksu** to eat as a spicy bibim-style noodle.

​

The basic side dishes consist of the dongchimi broth and two side dishes. We also ordered a buckwheat pancake (*memil-jeon*) to round out the meal.

Given the restaurant's name, the dongchimi broth is its core strength. Tasting it, it passes with honors. Some water radish kimchi broths carry a fermented odor, but this broth was clean, ice-cold, and refreshing.

Because the dongchimi broth is served as a slush, following the instructions exactly yields a slightly dry result. There is plenty of broth provided, so adding extra is recommended. The quality of the dongchimi broth kept the dish tasty.

​

While eating, I realized I should have set aside some noodles to mix with perilla oil. Perilla oil makguksu is highly aromatic; I plan to try that variation on my next visit.

​

​

While franchise operations can be inconsistent, this branch performed well, helped by the warm weather and my preference for cold noodle dishes. I rate it **4.1 out of 5 stars**. It is a reliable option for cold buckwheat noodles, and I plan to visit the main branch in Gangneung on my next trip.
