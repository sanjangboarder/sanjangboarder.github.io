â€‹

Last week, after returning from an egg-carrying webfoot octopus trip at Ocheon Port, we docked around 6:00 PM. The drive back to Incheon at that hour is typically congested, turning a standard 2-hour drive into a 3-hour commute. To avoid this, I have started visiting local restaurants near Chungnam ports after my trips before driving back.

â€‹

I recently learned that Ocheon Port is the largest pen shell (*key-jogae*) supplier in Korea, accounting for roughly 70% of domestic production. The prevalence of pen shell menus in local diners makes sense given this supply. Based on a local recommendation, I visited **Ocheon Port Fish Market Stall No. 7** to try a pen shell course meal.

â€‹

Check the map card for location details. Like most ports in Chungnam, local diners here do not remain open late.

â€‹

Since we arrived shortly after 6:00 PM, the diner was open and operating in daylight.

The tanks at the front are stocked with pen shells rather than fish. The pen shells in the plastic tubs are large. Local divers harvest these pen shells from waters close to Ocheon Port.

The interior layout is a standard country-style diner, and is clean. The tables are arranged in three rows, providing seating space.

â€‹

While single a la carte dishes are priced at 60,000 KRW, ordering the course meal includes most options for 100,000 KRW for a group of 4 (approx. 25,000 KRW per person), which represents better value.

â€‹

â€‹

For our group of seven, we ordered one 4-person set and one 3-person set across two tables. The standard table setting for four is shown below.

â€‹

The setup includes side dishes, wrapping vegetables, and a shabu-shabu pot. At this stage, I had not anticipated the quantity of pen shell meat that would follow.

â€‹

The side dishes were prepared well. The cabbage kimchi is made in-house. A side dish that looked like braised burdock root proved to be braised pen shell muscle (*chorigi*). Pen shell adductor muscle toughens if cooked for long periods. They braise the trimmings in soy sauce and slice them thin, making a savory side dish that pairs well with rice.

The savory pancake (*jeon*) served as a starter contained minced pen shell trimmings, which was a good start.

The first course was raw pen shell adductor muscle (*sashimi*). The muscle is cleaned and sliced into thick portions.

â€‹

The texture is tender and fresh, and it pairs well with *cho-jang* (spicy sweet chili dipping sauce).

â€‹

The next course was shabu-shabu. The broth contains oyster mushrooms, vegetables, and radish, in which you dip the pen shell pieces.

The shabu-shabu platter contains adductor muscle and auxiliary pen shell cuts. (Note: the sashimi and shabu-shabu platters shown are 2-person portions; our tables received two platters each).

While the adductor muscle works well in shabu-shabu, the auxiliary cuts are also flavorful. Avoid overcooking the meat to prevent it from becoming tough. I preferred the shabu-shabu over the raw sashimi; if you are less fond of raw seafood, you can cook the sashimi slices in the hot pot.

The next course was spicy stir-fried pen shell. It features sliced oyster mushrooms and vegetables stir-fried with pen shell meat in a spicy gochujang-style seasoning. The server stir-fries the dish at the table to prevent the meat from overcooking.

â€‹

â€‹

The pen shell meat and auxiliary organs are stir-fried quickly. While I have had pen shell shabu-shabu and sashimi before, this stir-fried variant was new to me, and was the highlight of the meal.

â€‹

â€‹

The seasoning is balanced, and this cooking style is worth recommending. Having enjoyed this dish, I purchased 50,000 KRW worth of fresh pen shells (10 clams) to take home and recreate it.

â€‹

â€‹

The final course was pan-seared buttered scallops. While this is an add-on item, the owner served it on the house because we were dining with a regular customer. Buttered scallops are a familiar dish, and the fresh scallop meat provides a tender texture.

â€‹

<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MjZfMTQx/MDAxNzc3MTg0NTkzODk8.NW-CPcQAF1JJV2px4R5ubrCAvIrh62GSckCNZntiK7Ag.Yeom0vsVuTXjVgCQIU_rQ08t2x-2MSPcD-ne3ohvCXEg.JPEG/900_20260425_185926.jpg?type=w800" alt="Buttered Pan-Seared Scallops" />

â€‹

We added noodle (*kalguksu*) to the shabu-shabu broth and fried rice (*bokkeumbap*) to the spicy stir-fry sauce. I forgot to take photos during the meal; the kalguksu noodles were average, while the fried rice was excellent. The fried rice is a recommended finish to the course.

â€‹

My rating for Ocheon Port Fish Market Stall No. 7 is **4.3 out of 5 stars**. The cost per person is reasonable, the course selection is balanced, and the freshness of the prepared pen shell clams stands out.

â€‹

When visiting Ocheon Port for boat fishing, many visitors return home immediately. Taking the time to try the local pen shell courses and driving back after the evening traffic clears is a recommended alternative.
