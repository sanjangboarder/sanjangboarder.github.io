Hello, this is Sanjang Border.

Last week, I went by myself to the Incheon Fishing Expo held in Songdo, Incheon. After looking around, it was lunchtime. I thought about just having a quick meal at home, but I ended up having lunch at a restaurant within Songdo Convensia. Without any prior information, I just chose a place with available seating since I was eating alone... it was Ho Cheon Dang, a specialty restaurant for donkatsu and soba.

There are a few restaurants within Songdo Convensia, but honestly, I've lived in Songdo for over 10 years, and it seems like no restaurant has lasted for that long. Rather than fierce competition, I think it might be because a truly great restaurant hasn't moved in yet, or perhaps the rent is too expensive, making it difficult for them to sustain.

I've seen this place occasionally when coming to Songdo Convensia, but this was my first visit. The restaurant interior is spacious and clean, which is nice. The menu features donkatsu, which kids love, and men also enjoy, so I entered with a bit of expectation.

This is the composition of the single-person Super Jumbo Set. I was worried it might be too much food because of "Super Jumbo," but it wasn't quite at that level. Haha.
For a man, it's a sufficient amount to eat completely. For women, it might feel a bit generous.

The side dishes are for cleansing the palate.. it would have been better if there was something like rakkyo (pickled shallots).. I'm not sure what kind of broth the soup was, but it was seasoned adequately, though it didn't feel particularly excellent.

The curry was edible, but it felt like there was a bit too little curry compared to the amount of rice. As it was a set menu, the amount of rice was fine, but I thought customers would appreciate it if they added a more generous portion of curry.

The donkatsu had a nice crispy and golden-brown exterior, which was good, but unfortunately, the meat was cooked too well-done, so the inside was definitely not juicy..

Personally, I prefer meat with a good mix of fat rather than lean meat, but this was a cut with almost no fat. Since it was just lean meat cooked to a well-done level, it reminded me of dry chicken breast... People who dislike fatty cuts might prefer it, but for me, who enjoys the rich flavor of moderately fatty, juicy meat, it was very disappointing.

Ho Cheon Dang in Songdo is a chain restaurant, and since I haven't visited any other branches, I'll evaluate based solely on the Songdo branch: 2.9 out of 5 points. Personally, I had high hopes for the donkatsu, but the overcooked, dry meat really didn't suit my personal taste. The curry was edible, and the buckwheat soba was just too ordinary without any special characteristics, which was a bit disappointing. I probably won't visit again. However, if you come to Songdo Convensia and visit with children, they might enjoy the food.

Thank you.
