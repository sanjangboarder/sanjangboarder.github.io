Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Today, I share my review of Ichiryu in Magok, a lamb specialty restaurant I had been looking forward to visiting. The restaurant is located near Magok Station and is in close proximity to the Magok Science Park.

​

Although I have worked in Magok for a considerable period, this was my first time noticing this establishment, indicating it is a relatively recent addition. It appears to have opened around summer of this year. Magok's restaurant market is highly competitive with frequent turnover, and this establishment introduces a style that was previously uncommon in the area.

​

Ichiryu is a franchise specializing in Japanese Genghis Khan-style charcoal-grilled lamb. The exterior features classic Japanese-style design elements, which carry over into the interior styling.

The curry uses a Japanese solid curry block profile as a base, with a spicy finish. Despite its mild appearance, the spice level is notable and may be challenging for those with low spice tolerance (*맵찔이*). The vegetables retained a crisp texture that complemented the spiced broth.

​

Ichiryu in Magok focuses on Japanese-style charcoal-grilled lamb. The pricing sits in the mid-to-high tier for this category. Because I prefer pairing lamb with cumin powder, the provided dipping options were a drawback for my taste. However, the signature soup curry was well-executed.

​

I would rate the establishment a **3.6 out of 5.0**. While I may not return specifically for the lamb, the soup curry remains a notable dish. The venue is suited for smaller groups of two to three diners rather than larger gatherings.
