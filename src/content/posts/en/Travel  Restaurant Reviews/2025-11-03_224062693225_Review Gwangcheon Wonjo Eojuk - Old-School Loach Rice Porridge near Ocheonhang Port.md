Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

During the autumn season, the West Coast Expressway experiences heavy traffic congestion on Saturday and Sunday evenings. Having driven the route from the Boryeong region for several years, I have noticed that departing between 5:00 PM and 6:00 PM compared to departing after 7:00 PM results in a difference of at least 30 minutes in driving time. Essentially, leaving an hour earlier only saves about 30 minutes.

​

As a result, I have developed a habit of dining at a local restaurant after finishing boat fishing trips in the Chungnam area before heading home. This avoids arriving home late only to have to prepare a meal. I plan to share reviews of practical dining options near major fishing hubs such as Ocheonhang Port, Daecheonhang Port, Namdanghang Port, and Mageompo Port.

​

Today, I am reviewing Gwangcheon Wonjo Eojuk, an old-school local eatery located in Gwangcheon, on the route from Ocheonhang Port to the Hongseong IC. The location is shown on the map below. On-street parking is available in front of the restaurant, or you can park at Gwangcheon Station if the street spaces are full.

​

The sign at the entrance identifies the establishment as the originator of pumpkin-fed loach rice porridge, and the exterior reflects its long history.

​

Although the name Gwangcheon Wonjo Eojuk suggests a standard freshwater fish porridge (minmul-eojuk), the specialty here is loach rice porridge (chueo-eojuk). It is similar to loach soup (chueotang) prepared in a porridge style. While this is the signature dish, a long-time customer recommended the ox head rice soup (somori-gukbab) as a notable menu option.

We ordered one special portion of ox head rice soup and two portions of loach rice porridge to sample both options.

The side dishes are shown below. The kkakdugi (radish kimchi) was crisp and well-fermented. The cabbage kimchi, while house-made, was less notable but paired acceptably with the soup and porridge. We refilled the kkakdugi three times.

​

The food quality at this established eatery was solid. The ox head rice soup was particularly well-prepared.

​

I rate this establishment 4.1 out of 5.0. It is a practical addition to the rotation of dining options when fishing out of Ocheonhang. It is particularly suited for visits during the spring and autumn seasons.

​

While many anglers visit Ocheonhang, this establishment in Gwangcheon is relatively unknown to out-of-town visitors. I will continue to review and share local dining options near regional fishing ports.
