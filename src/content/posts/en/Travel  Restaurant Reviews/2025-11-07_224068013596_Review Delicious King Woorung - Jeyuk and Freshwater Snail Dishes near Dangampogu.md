Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week, I went fishing in Mageompo, Taean. The trip ended with heavy rain and strong winds all day, making it difficult to eat lunch on the boat. Upon returning to the port, I checked the navigation system for my return route to Incheon, which indicated a travel time of over three hours. To bypass some of the highway congestion, I decided to eat dinner locally before driving.

​

This restaurant is situated along the main road leading back from major fishing ports in the area. Anglers returning from Shinjindo ports (such as Anheunghang, Mado, and Cheonlipo) or the Anmyeondo area (including Naepohang, Daeyado, and Yeongmokhang) will pass by it. It is a convenient stop before accessing the highway via Hongseong IC or Seosan IC.

​

The location is close to Dangampogu Port, and is situated directly on the return route, making it easily accessible without detouring. I selected this spot based on its location and menu options.

​

Despite its proximity to the sea, the restaurant's name, **Delicious King Woorung**, suggests a focus on freshwater snail dishes. However, my primary reason for visiting was the availability of jeyuk-bokkeum (spicy stir-fried pork). Stir-fried pork is a reliable option for a hearty meal, offering a consistent taste.

​

Dining options along the highway connecting Taean, Mageompo, and Baek사장 to the main expressway are relatively limited. Many restaurants serve gegukji (salted crab soup), a local specialty, though finding a high-quality venue can be challenging. Stir-fried pork is a reliable alternative.

​

While the side dishes were simple, the quality of the main course was satisfactory. I would rate this restaurant 3.8 out of 5 stars. It is a practical stop for dinner when returning from the area, offering a reliable meal.
