Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week, my wife and I visited Seongsu-dong, which is currently one of the trendiest districts in Seoul. We visited several local spots and cafés, and in this post, I want to introduce an exceptionally impressive bakery café: **ETF Bakery**.

​

Having lived in Seoul for a long time before moving to Songdo, Incheon, I generally avoid visiting crowded hotspots in Seoul on weekends. Since we visited on a weekday morning, the crowds were manageable. It had been nearly 20 years since I last visited Seongsu-dong, and it has changed dramatically. While it retains some of its old industrial feel, the entire district is now packed with trendy storefronts.

​

After lunch, we walked through the residential streets, and my wife noticed a bakery café nearby. I simply followed her. We visited without any prior research, simply spotting it on the map. The café is named ETF Bakery.

​

We visited without high expectations, but the entrance immediately gave off a premium, well-maintained vibe. It features a small garden with a pond and beautifully blooming flowers.

Across from the entrance, a sign lists butter rice cakes (butter-tteok) for just 490 KRW. That is incredibly cheap. The sign also details their "Top 10 Material Cost" items, which reveal an exceptionally high cost-of-goods (COG) ratio. While most restaurants and bakeries aim for a COG ratio around 30%, this shop averages above 40%, with several items exceeding 50%.

​

Korea's most famous value-oriented bakery is Daejeon's Seongsimdang, which is known to run a COG ratio slightly above 50%. ETF Bakery in Seongsu matches that standard. Considering the massive difference in real estate and rent prices between Daejeon and Seongsu-dong, running a 50% COG ratio in the heart of Seongsu is shocking.

ETF Bakery in Seongsu-dong stands out for its value, immediately recalling Daejeon's Seongsimdang. The space delivers the atmosphere of a premium flagship bakery, making its low prices in a high-rent district highly impressive. Applying my ratings, I rate this café **4.5 out of 5 stars**. Aside from difficult parking in the area, it is one of the best locations to enjoy coffee and bread in Seongsu-dong.

​

While ETF Bakery sits slightly off the main commercial street, it is only a 5-minute walk. After enjoying our bread, we walked around the surrounding Seongsu-dong alleys.
