As the year-end season approaches, gatherings with acquaintances become more frequent. I have attended several year-end dinners, and I plan to share reviews of the venues that stood out. In this post, I review **Mammoth Raw Fish Center Stall 8**, a location at Incheon Yeonan Pier I had been planning to visit.

‚Äã

The center is located between Incheon Yeonan Pier and the Incheon Complex Fish Market. Access via public transit is somewhat limited, making travel by car or local bus routes the practical options. For anglers visiting Yeonan Pier or Namhang Port, it is a convenient location to stop by after a fishing trip.

‚Äã

I visited on a Friday evening. Despite the limited public transit access, the center was busy with patrons. The parking lot surrounding the Mammoth Raw Fish Center is spacious, and parking assistants help manage the traffic. The center houses multiple numbered stalls; we dined at **Stall 8**, which is located near the main entrance.

By this point in the meal, we had consumed a significant amount of food, leaving the spicy fish stew (maeuntang) as the final course.

‚Äã

‚Äã

Mammoth Raw Fish Center Stall 8 at Incheon Yeonan Pier delivered a high-quality dining experience. For patrons who prioritize abundant, fresh seafood side dishes over simple raw fish fillets, it represents an excellent option.

‚Äã

I would rate the establishment a **4.5 out of 5.0**. I plan to return with family members for a future visit.

‚Äã

Currently, finding raw fish restaurants that serve a large selection of fresh seafood side dishes has become more difficult. The market has shifted toward budget-friendly venues focusing on raw fish with minimal side dishes. If you prefer the traditional, seafood-heavy dining style, this venue is highly recommended. It is a well-established location that attracts many regular visitors.
