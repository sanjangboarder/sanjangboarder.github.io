Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

On a previous boat fishing trip to Mokpo Bukhang Port, I had an early dinner near the docks. Looking through my photos, I recalled the flavor of the spicy pork bulgogi (jeyuk bokkeum) at this diner and decided to share a review.

​

The diner is located near Bukhang Fish Market and Bukhang Dock. It is not in a primary tourist district, being visited mostly by anglers and fish market operators. While named Ihan Gimbap, the cooked meals represent the main draw.

​

It is a compact diner operated by the female owner. Given the proximity of fishing charter boats, she also prepares boxed meals for boat trips. The menu is diverse for a gimbap-focused diner.

​

The interior has a simple, clean layout with high ceilings that create an open atmosphere.

​

​

The menu lists classic snack options and gimbap alongside several meal entrees. The most expensive entree is the spicy pork bulgogi. On this visit, our group of four ordered four portions of the pork bulgogi.

​

​

When the spicy pork bulgogi was served, I noted its presentation. While standard diners often use thin slices of pork shoulder or hind leg, this preparation featured thicker cuts.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNTA4MjRfMjMz/MDAxNzU1OTk5NTQ0MDgz.N4dfrK2h61qfeK0TMRFwjAWYNI3W1eCa9fFXFVBCr1Ag.l2TUqHMygj60VKx8xglic2HWguBYoYSZ0D8GUcmCMwQg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20250823%EF%BC%BF170927.jpg?type=w800" alt="Spicy pork bulgogi platter" />

​

The dish was accompanied by lettuce, sesame leaves, hot peppers, raw garlic, and house-made soybean paste (doenjang), which is suitable for preparing wraps (ssam).

​

​

The seasoning has a sweet and spicy profile, similar to tteokbokki sauce, which is a common style.

​

The pork meat was fresh. Based on the texture, it did not appear to be frozen pork; it retained the characteristics of fresh refrigerated pork.

​

The cut utilized pork belly (samgyeopsal/ogyeopsal) rather than cheaper hind leg cuts. It featured a balance of fat and lean meat, which supports the texture.

The pork bulgogi is best eaten wrapped in lettuce or sesame leaves with garlic and doenjang. The combination worked well.

​

This is one of the more satisfying spicy pork bulgogi preparations I have evaluated.

​

If you visit Mokpo Bukhang Port and need a meal, this diner represents a functional option. While the pricing of the pork bulgogi is slightly higher than standard neighborhood rates, the food quality matches the price.

​

​

I would rate this establishment **4.2 out of 5.0 stars**. I plan to return for the spicy pork bulgogi during future fishing trips out of Mokpo Bukhang Port. While Mokpo offers several dining options, this remains a reliable choice.
