​

Over the weekend, I went flower viewing with my family. After having pasta in Daebudo, my sibling suggested visiting a nearby scenic cafe. Below, I share my impressions of the destination.

​

The cafe is named **BALiiDA**, located on Gubongdo Island, which is situated near the entrance of Daebudo after crossing the Sihwa Seawall. As shown on maps, it sits directly in front of the beach. The bay behind it used to house a fishing pond that has since been filled in. Given its proximity to the Daebudo saltwater fishing pond that I visit regularly, the area felt familiar.

​

Since I primarily visit this area for sea fishing, I rarely stop by trendy cafes unless I am traveling with my wife. I was surprised to discover such a beautiful cafe on Gubongdo. We visited around Saturday lunchtime, and the main parking lot was already full. The access road into Gubongdo is a narrow single-lane street, so entering during busy Saturday afternoons can result in traffic gridlock. Discovering BALiiDA's Parking Lot 2 at the entrance of Gubongdo made sense once I saw the traffic.

​

Exiting the parking lot and walking to the entrance, the front facade appeared average.

Once inside, however, the focus on tropical interior design and atmosphere was immediately apparent. I expected the pricing to match premium ocean-view cafes, and it did. A standard Americano is priced at 6,800 KRW, indicating the general price range.

​

This pricing is standard for coastal cafes in regional tourist zones like Yeongjongdo or Ganghwado. Given the premium layout, the atmosphere justified the price.

The beachfront in front of Gubongdo consists of tidal mudflats. Our visit coincided with low tide during a spring tide cycle, meaning the water receded far offshore, leaving the mudflats fully exposed. Checking the tidal schedule to visit during high tide will yield a much better ocean view.

---

​

While Daebudo is home to numerous oceanfront cafes, BALiiDA is well-regarded for good reason. I rate it **4.1 out of 5 stars** (assuming high tide conditions). If visiting during low tide, the rating drops to **3.8 stars** because of the dry mudflats.

​

It is a recommended destination for couples dating in Daebudo, and serves as a nice spot for families to enjoy the beach. Because of strong sea winds, the area can be cold in the winter, making spring through autumn the ideal seasons to visit.
