The West Coast boat fishing season is winding down, with about a month or a month and a half remaining in the year. The webfoot octopus and cuttlefish season continues through late November, followed by flatfish downshot and rockfish trips in early December before the winter break.

â€‹

During my recent trip to Namdanghang Port, a key harbor for West Coast angling, I visited a local venue for dinner after docking. Here is a brief review of the establishment.

â€‹

The restaurant is situated near the harbor, carrying the name **Namdangbanjeom**.

â€‹

The building exterior shows its age. When fishing in the Chungnam region, checking restaurant hours is advisable as many local venues close early in the evening. Because we docked relatively early on this day, we visited without issues. However, calling ahead to confirm availability is recommended in this area, as mid-day breaks and early closures are common.

â€‹

The dining area has an old-school atmosphere, but is kept clean.

â€‹

The side dishes include yeolmu-kimchi (young radish kimchi), which is unusual for a Chinese restaurant.

â€‹

â€‹

Since I was dining with a party of four after fishing, we ordered a portion of tangsu-yuk (sweet and sour pork) to share. The style is traditional, featuring a crispy batter.

â€‹

The sweet and sour sauce is poured directly over the pork. While I generally prefer a chewy, tapioca-based batter, the classic crispy style offers a pleasant variation.

â€‹

Though not a standout, it represents a solid version of the traditional style.

â€‹

Dining options near Namdanghang Port after docking are somewhat limited. Anglers looking for a simple meal generally prefer to avoid expensive seafood restaurants. This venue serves as a practical choice for a quick meal.

â€‹

I would rate this restaurant 3.2 out of 5 stars. While not a destination venue, it is a practical choice for a meal after a fishing trip if you are looking for Chinese dishes in the area.
