Recently, Gyodongdo Island, located next to Ganghwado Island, was featured on the variety show "Country Village Lee Jang-woo 2". This increased my interest in the area, and I decided to visit. Since the market is reportedly very crowded on weekends, I visited Daeryung Market on a weekday afternoon to sightsee and have lunch.

‚Äã

My initial plan was to visit a North Korean-style dumpling hot pot restaurant, but since Daeryung Market is the primary attraction on Gyodongdo Island, I explored the market first. Today, I will share my impressions of Daeryung Market. The location is indicated on the map below.

‚Äã

Even on a weekday, the free public parking lot was nearly full. Visitors on weekends will likely need to use paid parking or search for space along the side streets.

‚Äã

A sign marking "Daeryung Market" stands at the entrance. The overhead utility poles and tangled power lines contribute to the retro atmosphere.

Ganghwado Island produces approximately 70% of South Korea's salted shrimp (saeujeot). Consequently, salted shrimp vendors are common in the market, offering varieties such as yukjeot (harvested in June), ojeot (harvested in May), and chujeot (harvested in autumn). The prices did not appear low.

While Daeryung Market preserves a retro layout, many shops cater to tourists by selling snacks. A number of snacks utilize locally grown Ganghwa Island rice, and traditional sweets and fried snacks are widely available.

Several shops sell Gangajitteok (puppy rice cake), which is similar in texture to standard glutinous rice cake (chapssalteok). It is soft and flavorful when eaten fresh, but it hardens when cold, so it is best to purchase only what you plan to eat immediately.

Sweet potatoes and turnips are notable agricultural products of Ganghwado. Turnip kimchi (sunmu kimchi) is sold at multiple locations. A container costs between 10,000 and 13,000 KRW. Its flavor profile differs from standard radish or young radish kimchi, representing a distinctive regional option.

The market atmosphere differs from standard rural markets. Because the inventory is tailored for tourists, food items like regional kimchi and rice cakes are the primary offerings. The variety of snacks makes it possible to skip a formal lunch and eat at the stalls instead.

Anrye Chalteok (glutinous rice cake) was a notable find. The cakes cost between 2,000 and 4,000 KRW each and were flavorful. The dried persimmon chalteok (3,000 KRW) was particularly good.

‚Äã

While the prices are moderate, this is a viable alternative if you bypass the puppy rice cakes.

‚Äã

My wife enjoys visiting local five-day and traditional markets when we travel. Daeryung Market on Gyodongdo offers a distinctive experience. Because it is oriented toward tourists, it features specialized regional food items and snacks.

‚Äã

However, the prices are not typical of a budget rural market, and the narrow alleys can make navigation difficult. Visiting on a weekday rather than a weekend offers a more relaxed experience.
