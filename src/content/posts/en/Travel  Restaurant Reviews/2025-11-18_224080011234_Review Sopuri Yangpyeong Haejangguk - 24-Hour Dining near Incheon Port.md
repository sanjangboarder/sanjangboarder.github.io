Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week, I went boat fishing from South Port in Incheon. I took a half-day morning boat, and since my companions and I wanted to eat before heading home, we decided to get lunch. While raw fish restaurants dominate the area near Yeonan Pier and South Port, other options are scattered around the neighborhood, particularly near Incheon Complex Fish Market. We visited a Yangpyeong-style hangover soup restaurant that I had noticed on previous trips.

​

The venue is located close to Yeonan Pier, and is less than a five-minute drive from South Port, making it a convenient stop after a fishing trip.

​

Street parking is available directly in front of the restaurant, so driving there is manageable. The restaurant is a recently opened establishment.

The menu focuses on hangover soups. As a first-time visitor, I ordered the first item listed on the menu.

​

I ordered the Special Yangpyeong Haenaetang for 14,000 KRW.

A self-serve side dish bar is located in the dining room, stocked with kimchi and fresh vegetables suitable for soup pairings. The cabbage kimchi and diced radish kimchi (kkakdugi) appeared to be house-made.

Each table has condiments: chopped Cheongyang hot peppers, ground perilla seeds, chili oil, soy sauce, and wasabi.

​

Mixing these together makes a good dipping sauce for the meats, though I did not capture a photo of the mixed sauce.

​

The Special Yangpyeong Haenaetang was served with a bowl of rice. The restaurant offers unlimited rice refills. The Haenaetang is a broth-based soup centered on beef tripe and mixed offal. While traditional Yangpyeong hangover soup includes congealed oxblood (seonji), those who prefer it can order the standard Yangpyeong Haejangguk.

​

The boiling hot soup pot had an appealing presentation.

​

I ordered the special size to get a larger serving of tripe and offal, and the portions were generous.

​

The broth contains bean sprouts, and the tripe and offal were chewy and clean without off-odors. Adding perilla seed powder and chili oil to the broth enhanced the flavor.

​

Since I generally eat the solid ingredients with rice rather than drinking all the broth, the special size provided a satisfying quantity of meat to finish a bowl of rice. For those who finish the entire pot of soup, extra rice may be necessary. The standard size would likely balance well with a single bowl of rice if you consume both soup and solid ingredients.

​

I would rate this restaurant 4 out of 5 stars. I visited without specific expectations and had a satisfying meal. Its proximity to Yeonan Pier and South Port makes it a convenient option. I plan to return when looking for a hot soup dish in the area.
