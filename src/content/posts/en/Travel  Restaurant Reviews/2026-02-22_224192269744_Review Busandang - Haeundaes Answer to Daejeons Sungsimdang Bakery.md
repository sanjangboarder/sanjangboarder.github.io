​

During my recent trip to Busan, I discovered **Busandang** in Haeundae. While it was not in my original itinerary, I stopped by after spotting it from the street because the concept reminded me of Daejeon's famous Sungsimdang bakery.

​

​

The bakery is located adjacent to the main alley of Haeundae, near the beach. The location is convenient for tourists. While there are a few designated parking slots for pick-up customers, visiting without a vehicle is recommended.

​

The name "Busandang" was recently adopted; the business previously operated under the name "Baekjo Bakery." The rebranding appears designed to position the venue as "Busan's Sungsimdang." Having visited Sungsimdang several times, I can offer a direct comparison.

I noted a moderate number of customers inside. While the new building is cleaner than Sungsimdang's older premises, the floor area is smaller. Sungsimdang's main store features a larger variety of breads and higher customer density, whereas Busandang operates on a smaller scale.

The bread varieties also differ from Sungsimdang's lineup. Rather than copying Sungsimdang's specific items, Busandang seems to follow the concept of offering a diverse selection of baked goods at reasonable prices. Considering Haeundae's general price levels and comparing it to large bakery cafes, the pricing here is moderate.

---

​

I rate this bakery **3.5 out of 5 stars**. Busandang is not a destination that warrants a dedicated trip to Haeundae. However, if you are visiting the beach area and need a dessert cafe, it represents a reasonable option. If the intention is to emulate Sungsimdang, expanding the selection of affordable, high-quality items would be preferred. Because it is a new business, I expect further refinements, and plan to check it again on a future trip to Busan.
