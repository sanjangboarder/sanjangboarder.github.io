Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I participate in a social gathering of school alumni and friends called PlatformK. During a recent meeting, the group went running in Gwacheon and gathered for dinner afterward at **Hanseong Kalguksu** near Seonbawi Station. Although we went specifically to eat dumpling hot pot, the restaurant carries "Kalguksu" in its name.

​

The location details are shown below.

​

I enjoyed the dumpling hot pot during that initial group dinner and noted it as a menu my wife would enjoy. A few days ago, I took her there for lunch. The autumn weather was clear, making it a good day for an outing.

Hanseong Kalguksu features a large parking lot and serves a steady stream of patrons. While it was my first time visiting, online reviews indicate it is an established local favorite. Since the area lies outside my usual activity range, I was unfamiliar with it, but plan to return.

The dining area shows its age, and each table includes an integrated burner. We arrived around 2:30 PM, yet several tables remained occupied. The restaurant does not have a mid-day break, listing only standard opening and closing hours.

The menu offers several choices, but the dumpling hot pot remains the primary specialty. While the plain dumpling soup also appeared appealing, we ordered the medium size of the dumpling hot pot, which was verified on my previous visit. It provides a sufficient portion for two people.

While the side dishes were simple, the dumpling hot pot itself was of high quality.

​

The hot pot is prepared with enoki mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, green onions, and cabbage in a brisket-based beef broth, resembling a dumpling shabu-shabu. The broth delivers a rich, smooth flavor. It resembles home-style dumpling soup; my wife noted that it tasted like the dumpling soup her mother makes.

​

A seasoned beef paste (dadaegi) is mixed into the pot, providing high-quality beef pieces.

​

The combination of broth, beef, shiitake, and enoki mushrooms is savory. The seasoning paste adds a reddish color to the broth without making it spicy.

While the dumplings are the primary focus of the hot pot, I was also satisfied with the other ingredients. The dumplings are bite-sized and plentiful, offering a pleasant taste. Pairing them with the house-made kimchi balances the flavors.

​

I would rate this restaurant 4.3 out of 5 stars. It provides a satisfying meal with generous portions, and I would consider returning when traveling through the area. A minor drawback is its location, which is less accessible without a vehicle. It is a walk from Seonbawi Station and sits in a less developed area, so utilizing the spacious parking lot and driving is recommended.
