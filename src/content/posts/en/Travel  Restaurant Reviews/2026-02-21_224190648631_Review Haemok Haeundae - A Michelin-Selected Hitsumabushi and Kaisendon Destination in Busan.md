Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

In this post, I review the restaurant that provided the highest satisfaction during my recent trip to Busan. Having booked accommodation in Haeundae, I researched local dining options. Originally, I planned a trip to Nagoya, Japan, but after it was cancelled, I redirected to Busan and selected Japanese menu items, including Hitsumabushi (grilled eel over rice).

​

Busan is known for two primary Hitsumabushi venues: Tokyo Babsang, managed by the 'Buchae Dosa' (Paper Fan Master) chef from Culinary Class Wars, and **Haemok**. I selected Haemok because its online waitlist management makes queueing more convenient.

​

When the restaurant opened, I registered on the Tabling app. As the queue moved, I walked over from my accommodation. Because this restaurant consistently runs a queue, registering online in advance is preferable to waiting on-site.

Both dishes were successful. If I had to select one, the Hitsumabushi is slightly superior, though ordering both is recommended for groups of two. The portions are sufficient for adult preferences.

​

I rate this restaurant **4.5 out of 5 stars**. While the prices are high, the quality of the food and the dining environment justify the cost. Among the three Michelin-selected venues I visited in Busan during this trip, this branch provided the highest satisfaction. The large table count ensures the queue moves efficiently, keeping wait times manageable.

​

Haemok operates five locations: three in Seoul, one in Daegu, and this branch in Busan. Since I live in Songdo, Incheon, I plan to visit the Seoul branches when I want to eat this style of eel bowl again. If you are visiting Haeundae, this restaurant is worth considering.
