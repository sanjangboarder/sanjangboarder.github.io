Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

With the year-end holiday season here, my schedule has been filled with dinner appointments and gatherings. Recently, I visited the buffet at the Mayfield Hotel in Gangseo-gu after a long absence, and today I share my review.

​

Access to the Mayfield Hotel is somewhat inconvenient by public transport, as it is located at a distance from the nearest subway station, and bus lines directly to the entrance are limited. Driving a personal vehicle is recommended. Refer to the map below for the exact location.

​

Upon arrival, the hotel exterior showed a festive year-end atmosphere. Compared to pre-COVID years, the classic Christmas atmosphere with carol music is less prominent, but the hotel setup still captures the season's feel.

My first plate included LA galbi, which was slightly dry. However, the boiled pork jowl (*항정살 수육*) was tender and matched my taste. The grilled eel was well-prepared, and the cold jelly dish (*yangjangpi*) was decent. The radish water kimchi (*dongchimi*) I brought to cleanse the palate was refreshing.

​

The grilled king prawns, steak, and boiled pork belly slices were somewhat dry and fell short of expectations. My assessment is based on the expectation for a buffet in this price bracket rather than indicating that the food was unpalatable.

​

Considering the price point of 135,000 KRW, I would rate the Castle Terrace Buffet at the Mayfield Hotel in Gangseo a **3.8 out of 5.0**. While the menu selection and overall quality were reasonable, the taste profile of several core dishes left room for improvement.

​

My visit was on a weekday evening, which offered a relaxed dining atmosphere. Since reservations are relatively easy to secure compared to other major hotel buffets, it remains a viable option for year-end gatherings.
