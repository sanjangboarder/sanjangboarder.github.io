Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I frequently travel to Seosan, Taean, and Anmyeondo Island for fishing. Whether boat fishing or shore fishing, the return trip usually begins around sunset. Because these are rural areas, finding open restaurants at night is often difficult, making dining choices challenging.

​

On the route towards Hongseong IC, many establishments serve gegukji (crab and kimchi stew), which is not my preference. While looking for a place serving kimchi stew, I happened to find this location.

​

It is named **Bird House**. Despite the name, it is a restaurant serving grilled fish alongside aged kimchi pork stew. The location is detailed on the map above. Situated on the road leading to Hongseong IC, it is convenient for a meal after a fishing session.

The kimchi stew is prepared with a generous portion of pork. It represents a standard menu choice that met expectations.

While it lacks a highly distinct character, it is a balanced stew with no major shortcomings.

​

The primary draw appears to be the grilled fish. The kitchen uses semi-dried fish rather than fresh catches.

​

Both the Atka mackerel and mackerel are cooked in a brazier oven, arriving with a browned exterior and remaining moist inside.

Personally, I would have preferred a slightly higher salt level to bring out the flavor.

​

<div class="single-image">

​

Between the two options, the Atka mackerel suited my tastes better. Using semi-dried fish with slightly heavier salting might be a functional adjustment.

​

For anglers returning from Anmyeondo, Anheung Port, or Baeksajang Port towards Hongseong IC, this restaurant serves as a practical option for a hot meal, compared to limited options at highway rest areas.

​

While dining choices in this corridor are limited, the food is reliable. I would rate this establishment **3.5 out of 5 stars** and plan to stop by again during future return trips.
