During my recent trip to Busan and Gyeongju, I visited several restaurants. Among them, I found another venue that scored above 4 out of 5 on my personal rating system, which I introduce in this post.

​

I previously considered myself someone who did not favor Japanese cuisine. However, trying Hitsumabushi (eel rice bowl) on this trip made me realize I enjoy Japanese-style eel bowls, and visiting **Nagahama Mangetsu** confirmed that I appreciate Japanese ramen as well. It seems that regardless of the cuisine—Japanese, Korean, or Chinese—high-quality food transcends genres.

​

Nagahama Mangetsu has two locations in Busan; this main store in Haeundae, and another in Gwangalli. Since my accommodation was in Haeundae, I visited the main branch. While it is near Haeridan-gil, it is located in a quiet residential area on the opposite side of Haeundae Station, which is an unusual placement for a popular venue. It is also selected in the Michelin Guide Busan.

​

Online reservations are not accepted, and only on-site queuing is available. I arrived after 1:00 PM for a late lunch, but was informed they had stopped accepting lunchtime guests and to return for the dinner opening at 4:30 PM. They start registering waitlist numbers at 4:00 PM. During the break, we visited Haedong Yonggungsa Temple.

The primary item is the Nagahama Ramen. Nagahama ramen is a Hakata-style tonkotsu ramen originating in Fukuoka, characterized by thin noodles and a concentrated pork bone broth. The presentation matches this description.

The thin, firm noodles paired well with the broth. The broth is seasoned; it tastes slightly salty on its own, but holds the correct salinity when eaten with the noodles. The chashu pork was tender and featured a flame-torched (*Aburi*) finish. The soft-boiled egg (*Ajitama*) was prepared correctly.

​

The Nagahama Ramen at Haeundae's Nagahama Mangetsu was a positive dining experience. If I were to note minor limitations, the gyoza could have featured a more distinct flavor, and the chili paste (*Dadaegi*) was hot, meaning my wife's broth became spicy quickly after adding it. Otherwise, there were few drawbacks.

​

I rate this restaurant **4.4 out of 5 stars**. If I return to Busan, I plan to visit this location again. For visitors to Haeundae who are willing to manage the queue times, it represents a solid dining option.
