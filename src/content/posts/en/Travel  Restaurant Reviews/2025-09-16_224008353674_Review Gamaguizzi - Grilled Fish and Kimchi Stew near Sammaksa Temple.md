​

As the summer heat faded, I wanted to eat grilled fish and visited a restaurant located in the Sammaksa food alley district in Anyang. The location details are shown in the map information.

​

The restaurant provides dedicated parking in front of the building. I arrived just before the dinner rush, allowing me to park easily.

​

The menu board is displayed near the entrance, allowing visitors to check options before entering. I selected the Guizzi course menu, priced at 14,000 KRW per person. This course combines grilled mackerel, bossam (boiled pork wraps), braised kimchi (kimchi-jjim), and hot pot rice (sotbap), which seemed like a reasonable combination for the price.

While I visit this district regularly, this was my first time dining at Gamaguizzi.

​

The dining room layout is tidy and clean. While there are no notable views since it faces the main road, it serves as a functional space for family dining.

All entrees include cast iron hot pot rice (gamasotbap) and access to a salad bar. The salad bar contains refills for the standard side dishes. It also features seaweed soup (miyeok-guk), which is not served automatically with the table setting. I missed trying the soup because I focused on the braised kimchi.

The grilled mackerel is served as one half fillet per person. It was cooked to order and served hot, which I prefer over pre-grilled fish that is reheated.

​

While the mackerel was prepared well, the fillet size was relatively small. The flavor was standard.

The bossam (boiled pork wraps) is served in small slices. The preparation felt more like a basic side dish rather than specialty bossam. It was edible, but on future visits, I would likely bypass the courses that contain it.

​

The braised kimchi (kimchi-jjim) was the most successful dish of the meal. It was interesting that the braised kimchi outshone the grilled mackerel at a grilled fish establishment.

​

The pork cooked inside the kimchi stew was tender, and the overall seasoning balance paired well with the hot pot rice. On a future visit, I would prefer ordering a set focusing on the grilled fish and braised kimchi.

The hot pot rice was prepared correctly.

​

You scoop the rice into a separate bowl and pour warm green tea water into the iron pot to prepare scorched rice water (sungnyung), which is a common setup.

​

​

The course menu represents a reasonable setup for the price of 14,000 KRW. The primary drawback was the lack of variety in the standard side dishes; adding seasoned greens or high-quality salted seafood (jeotgal) would improve the meal. While the fish size was relatively small for a grilled fish diner, the braised kimchi was flavorful.

​

The cost performance is acceptable. I would rate this restaurant **3.5 out of 5.0 stars**. I plan to return primarily for the braised kimchi.

​

A dessert corner is available near the exit. A meal for three at 42,000 KRW is reasonable for this district.
