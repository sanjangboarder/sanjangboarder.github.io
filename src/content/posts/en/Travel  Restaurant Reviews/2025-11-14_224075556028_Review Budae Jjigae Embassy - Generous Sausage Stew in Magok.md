Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

While spicy stir-fried pork and kimchi stew are common lunch choices, budae jjigae (army base stew) remains another popular option. Having not eaten budae jjigae in a while, I searched on NAVER Maps for a nearby venue and visited this establishment.

​

The restaurant is located near Magok Station, within the commercial district adjacent to Magok Science Park. I have worked in Magok for ten years, and restaurants in this area must maintain high standards to survive beyond a couple of years. This was my first time visiting this spot, which occupies a space previously used by a Chinese restaurant.

​

The name of the restaurant is **Budae Jjigae Embassy**, indicating its specialization.

​

Signboards out front advertise unlimited rice and ramen noodle refills, noting a large-portion challenge that I bypassed.

Service is rapid; the table was set within ten seconds of sitting down. No ordering was required because the restaurant serves a single lunch menu: budae jjigae. For office workers with a limited one-hour lunch break, service speed is a key convenience factor.

The side dish is limited to kimchi. The bean sprouts are intended to be added to the stew once it starts boiling.

The kimchi appears to be standard commercial kimchi. The restaurant's approach focuses resources on the main stew rather than side dishes.

Piles of Jin Ramen Spicy are stacked near the tables. Instead of plain ramen noodles, the restaurant provides full instant ramen packages, allowing patrons to use the seasoning powder if needed. Extra broth is also available at the table, allowing diners to cook multiple ramen packets. However, the stew itself was filling enough that I managed only one.

While the stew and rice were sufficient for a full meal, I added a packet of ramen. I poured in extra broth and added some of the seasoning packet to adjust the flavor as the liquid reduced.

​

I was unable to finish the entire serving of ramen due to the generous portions.

​

I visited without consulting reviews beforehand and found the meal to be of good quality.

​

I would rate this restaurant 4.5 out of 5 stars for a lunch option. I plan to return when looking for budae jjigae in the area. The rapid setup time is convenient, the sausage quality is good, and the unlimited rice and Jin Ramen refills provide good value. It represents a practical lunch option in the Magok district.
