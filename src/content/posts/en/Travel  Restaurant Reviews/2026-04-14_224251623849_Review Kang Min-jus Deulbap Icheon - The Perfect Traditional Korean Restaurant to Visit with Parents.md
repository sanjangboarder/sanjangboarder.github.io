Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I remember a branch of the traditional Korean restaurant **Kang Min-ju's Deulbap** used to operate in Songdo. At the time, I found the food average and did not revisit, even after the restaurant rebranded to Songdo Deulbap. Recently, however, my wife visited the Icheon Main Branch and praised it, suggesting we bring my mother. Given her recommendation, we decided to visit.

​

We drove two hours from Songdo to the main branch. The restaurant sits in a quiet rural area, but despite the remote location, it draws massive crowds. We visited on a Friday during lunch, and the place was busy. To avoid waiting, arriving by 11:00 AM is highly recommended. The parking lot is exceptionally spacious, making parking hassle-free.

It was cloudy during our visit, which made the exterior look slightly dark. On clear days, the cherry blossom trees around the property likely make for a beautiful view.

​

The main branch is quite large. A sign highlights barley-aged yellow croaker (bori-gulbi) and marinated crab (ganjang-gejang) as their specialties. Primarily, it operates as a traditional multi-course Korean restaurant (Hanjeongsik) featuring a variety of side dishes.

​

To accommodate regular wait times, they provide a clean waiting zone. We saw numerous traditional earthenware pots (jangdokdae) in the yard. Their house-made fish sauce (eo-ganjang) is highly regarded. My wife purchased a bottle; it is salty and excellent for seasoning vegetable side dishes.

The standard Deulbap course is priced at 17,000 KRW. However, the standard selection includes vegetable dishes only. To add protein, we ordered a grilled mackerel. For diners who prefer vegetarian options, the base course is sufficient.

This is the table setting for 3 people, including the additional grilled mackerel. The meal features 12 side dishes, a house-made rich soybean paste stew (cheonggukjang), and complimentary rice cakes (given to mark a recent culinary award). The initial portions are small, but they are easily refilled at the bar.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MTNfMTcw/MDAxNzc2MDUwMTk1NjUx.7usHdk4VrgEPVtxOk4LxdpyESrW50oob6xXOBqphCZYg.rJN7mhcLcATIbzrixJDBlJZgW29Jy1WRVWkJdti53IUg.JPEG/SE-70e4f84d-36cf-11f1-8665-d9826cee3e56.jpg?type=w800)

First, the rich soybean paste stew is a rustic, country-style stew. It is house-made, rather than store-bought, and delivers a clean flavor without an overpowering smell.

The grilled mackerel was large enough to share comfortably among 3 people. The fish paired well with the lettuce wraps, and the house-made ssamjang (seasoned soybean paste) tasted excellent. If you enjoy wraps, ordering the mackerel is highly recommended.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MTNfMTIx/MDAxNzc2MDUwMjgzNDA3.1Eun6bXss63crig9vxdYNvuEKubxl-Z9ERCV39AcwFIg.xiQ5XBWNjzh0dAEqayY-zHyEAg3Putt_iPk90uAlB98g.JPEG/SE-70e7694f-36cf-11f1-8665-bb4366476df3.jpg?type=w800)

The hot stone pot rice utilized premium local Icheon rice. The rice itself was outstanding, carrying a sweet, glossy finish.

None of the side dishes tasted processed; everything was fresh and prepared in-house daily.

For lovers of traditional Korean countryside dining, the vegetable selections are excellent. My wife thoroughly enjoyed the vegetable side dishes, though as a meat-lover, I preferred having the fish.

Near the register, they sell packaged side dishes, fish sauce, crispy seaweed chips (bugak), and barley bread. The food was good, but it did not feel necessary to purchase items to take home.

---

​

Kang Min-ju's Deulbap is a highly satisfying restaurant for parents who appreciate fresh vegetables and diners looking for healthy, low-meat meals. Applying my ratings, I rate this restaurant **3.9 out of 5 stars**, while my vegetable-loving wife rates it **4.5 out of 5 stars**. If you are visiting the Icheon area, it is a recommended dining destination.
