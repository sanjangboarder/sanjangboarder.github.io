Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Two major commercial complexes, One Grove and K-Square, have opened near my office at Magok Science Park (LG Science Park area). The underground levels of these two complexes are connected, forming a single shopping area. I recently visited a Chinese restaurant that opened in this underground passage to have lunch, and I present my review below.

​

The restaurant is located along the underground connection linking Magok Station and Magoknaru Station. It is situated on the basement level of the K-Square building, adjacent to a popular dak-galbi (spicy stir-fried chicken) venue called Tongnamu Dak-galbi.

​

The storefront is clean, reflecting its recent opening. The name of the restaurant is **Ilil Mimi**. The signage outside reads "Freshly Stir-fried Jjajangmyeon," suggesting that black bean paste noodles are their primary menu item.

Orders are placed via the table tablet, with payment handled at the front counter upon departure.

​

Given their focus on black bean noodles, we ordered one Gan-jjajangmyeon (freshly stir-fried black bean sauce noodles). We also selected the Dim Sum Platter from the lunch special menu, priced at 22,000 KRW, alongside a medium portion of Tangsuyuk (sweet and sour pork), also priced at 22,000 KRW.

The Shumai, featuring a firm pork and shrimp filling, was the most successful dumpling, while the chive option offered a clean flavor profile when dipped in soy sauce. Although not matching specialized dim sum houses in quality, it represents a satisfactory standard for a neighborhood Chinese restaurant.

​

The Tangsuyuk was served with the sauce pre-poured over the pork. Serving sweet and sour pork with the sauce pre-poured suggests confidence in the kitchen's ability to maintain a crispy exterior.

​

The exterior coating was crispy, but the interior pork was somewhat dry. However, the meat thickness and the balanced sweet-and-sour profile of the sauce should suit general preferences. The pork fell slightly below the standards set by the noodles and dim sum.

​

The restaurant also serves Jjampong (spicy seafood noodle soup) which has received positive local reviews; I plan to test it on a future visit. For subsequent lunch visits, I would likely order the dim sum platter alongside individual noodle bowls rather than the sweet and sour pork.

​

I rate this venue **3.9 out of 5 stars**. The quality is sufficient to warrant return visits. While the Tangsuyuk was dry, the Jjajangmyeon and dim sum items were correct.

​

Although not a destination worth traveling to, it represents a solid lunch option for staff working in the Magok area.
