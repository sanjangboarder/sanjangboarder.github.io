Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

After landing two big flatfish last week, my spirits were high. I headed back to Daecheon Port to ride the **Yangpa-ho** with fellow TEAM MJT members. Since big flatfish fishing involves a high degree of luck, I kept my expectations modest, doubting that such luck would strike twice in a row.

​

We stopped by Daecheon Port's Hi Fishing shop to fill out the Yangpa-ho boarding manifest. They seem to share office space now, which features a wide variety of tackle to browse while filling out the manifest.

Since the shop is a short distance from the slips, we parked at the public lot and walked down. The captain requested we arrive by 4:10 AM, meaning I had barely managed an hour or two of sleep before driving down. All TEAM MJT members arrived on time, and we set sail around 4:30 AM.

The captain ran the boat roughly an hour and a half to reach our first spot, situated between Oepasudo and Hodo islands. It was a long run, slightly further out than last week, and multiple boats from the Chungnam region were already gathered to target big flatfish.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA1MjRfNjQg/MDAxNzc5NTk5MDQ3NDY4.O3wB0p1hrZKnVuuUyaxl7j-R4TtPj4XSnSFYJw8eJSUg.noJpLqwiTTFZDbYfmil_YAZvWAFR2b_AzTd8PMoeC-Ig.JPEG/SE-568fd5ef-5716-11f1-b0e7-714a1f23700a.jpg?type=w800" />

I brought two tackle setups. Since the tide was moderate (tide 13) and currents were manageable, I fished with my sensitive JS Company B61-3 rod all day. The 3-power blank provides excellent sensitivity to bites and manages sinkers without dragging, making operations comfortable.

​

> **Yangpa-ho Flatfish Downshot Gear & Rig Specs**
>
> *   **Rod**: JS Company Liberty Ocean B61-3
> *   **Reel**: Creman Little Giant LG
> *   **Line**: Haedong Limit X9 PE 1.5
> *   **Rig**: Deli Rig Lux Straight Hook 4/0
> *   **Worms**: Trigger X (TB, PP, PK, WH), Berkley HD
> *   *SanjangBorder*

​

🔗 [JS Company Liberty Ocean Rod (Naver)](https://naver.me/GmpVLFLr)

​

For my reel, I fished with the Creman Little Giant LG. I spotted it at the fishing show last year but it was repeatedly sold out; I finally managed to acquire one. I'm preparing a full review post, but my initial impression after a full day of use is that it is compact, smooth, and looks excellent. For worms, I rotated through 8 different colors to trigger strikes, which eventually yielded excellent results.

Although the forecast predicted clear skies, the morning was overcast. The colors that worked well last week went ignored. I rotated through different shades, and watched what was working for other TEAM MJT members who were landing fish.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA1MjRfMjcx/MDAxNzc5NjAwMzUzMjM4.CVn6CJ4j-RVOmiq47KX3qEIG1IZ5KlgeDnOtRH9dtnEg.9GbqQeNt349aR6GAkIqIyleZLgkPSvh555rIpnvuFyIg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" />

We started fishing before 6:00 AM, and for the next 5 hours, I didn't get a single bite. While the boat wasn't on fire, fellow anglers around me were landing fish. Only myself and another member, who landed 3 large flatfish last week, remained empty-handed.

​

My confidence was starting to slide, and I tried to console myself that I had used up my luck last week. Just then, a strong flatfish thump struck my rod.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA1MjRfMTU5/MDAxNzc5NTk4OTUwNjAy.Gx7qiBiZQRz-47f8phftZrY-gwFVzMm5e0D3qiI1ojIg.vN7kPwEoqoTzMOEPH5jqb2W4hgiUAcoDciOakXpklsYg.JPEG/SE-5680bab4-5716-11f1-b0e7-c5ffc6f9b03e.jpg?type=w800" />
</div>

The fish took a pink Trigger X worm. Up to that point, roughly two-thirds of the flatfish landed on the boat had taken pink. While my previous rotations through mackerel, motor oil, and chartreuse went ignored, switching to the popular color immediately produced results.

​

🔗 [Rapala Trigger X Minnow Worm PK 5-inch (Naver)](https://naver.me/IFEG0qAw)

​

With my confidence restored, I switched to a Berkley Power Jerkshad HD in mackerel (blue/silver). Within 30 minutes, I added two more flatfish. Landing 3 flatfish on a single trip is a personal record for me.
