Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

The 2025 webfoot octopus and cuttlefish boat fishing season is entering its final stretch. For my last outing of October, I joined the TEAM MJT regular outing on the **Valkyrie** (operated by Ocheon Lure School) from Ocheonhang Port, Boryeong. By late October, the summer heat has passed, replaced by cold morning air.

​

Although the weather warmed slightly compared to a cold spell during the week, autumn is clearly transitioning into winter. Ocheonhang Port, a central location for West Coast boat fishing, was crowded despite the late-season date. I arrived in time for the early morning departure and managed to secure a spot in the paid parking lot.

This trip comes a month after my previous outing on Valkyrie 7. Having boarded the Valkyrie several times, I find the setup familiar. The fleet operates with an intensive fishing style, extending the time on the water, which can be physically demanding.

​

​

Because the trip coincided with a spring tide, water movement was significant. Additionally, water temperatures had dropped during the week. Consequently, the captain moved to spots north of Yeondo Island. This was a long run of nearly 30 km from Ocheonhang Port. The Valkyrie targets a mix of webfoot octopus and cuttlefish on these trips for a 90,000 KRW fee, whereas dedicated cuttlefish trips generally cost 110,000 KRW.

​

​

For gear, I used a Choipro Seohae metal-tip rod, which is suited for sinker sizes 16 to 18 as recommended by the captain. I also brought an NS Cuttlefish rod, but realized at the parking lot that I had brought the incorrect tip section, so I operated with a single rod setup.

​

> **Gear and Rig Specifications**
>
> *   **Rod:** Choipro Seohae Rod
> *   **Reel:** NH Korea Core Solid Baitcasting Reel
> *   **Line:** Haedong Pinatura PE Professional 1.0 braided line
> *   **Sinker:** Moncross Dimple Sinker Size 18
> *   **Rig:** Tulbo Fishing BB Custom 20 cm branch line rig
> *   **Egi selection:** C-Nex Webfoot Octopus 5-color set, C-Nex watermelon/anchovy egi, Yozuri Ultra Laser HRM, Yamashita Toto Sutte AJ, Gochujang (red head) egi.

​

The BB Custom 20 cm rig worked well. While I had considered making my own rigs, this ready-made version is practical. It maintains a fixed branch line height, and I adjusted the leader length based on the current speed.

​

My egi rotation focused on targeting webfoot octopus alongside cuttlefish.

​

Among the C-Nex 5-color egi set, the horse mackerel pattern and the Gochujang laser pattern performed best. My plan was to target webfoot octopus with a Gochujang egi on the sinker, while running a horse mackerel egi on the branch line to target cuttlefish.

​

More than half of the larger cuttlefish I collected on this day were caught on the Gochujang laser egi attached directly to the sinker.

We arrived at the first spot around 7:00 AM. Although the boat rolled during the transit, we found the wind at the location to be stronger than expected. The forecast indicated 3 to 5 m/s wind speeds, but actual conditions felt closer to 7 to 9 m/s. While safe for operation, it generated significant wave action.

While I rarely experience motion sickness, rough waves have caused issues recently. I took Aneron medicine, but had to rest for about 30 minutes until it took effect.

​

Upon resuming, the catch rate was steady, with cuttlefish and webfoot octopus caught in a near 1:1 ratio. Under these conditions, a mixed-target trip represents a practical alternative to a dedicated cuttlefish outing.

​

By 2:00 PM, the wind increased, and the captain moved the boat toward Wonsando Island. The island blocked the wind, but catch rates dropped, and we finished the trip early at 4:00 PM. We returned to the harbor around 5:00 PM.

​

My final catch was 38 cuttlefish and 39 webfoot octopus. Given the wind, waves, and seasickness, it was a reasonable result.
