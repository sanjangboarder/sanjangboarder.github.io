Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

My fifth outing of the cuttlefish season was aboard the **Island-ho**, operating out of Daeyado. The Island-ho is an established boat in the area, often associated with high cuttlefish counts. During our club outing in mid-September last year, we secured numbers but noted many small specimens. For this reason, I scheduled this trip for late September to target larger sizes.

​

Because the boat travels to the Shinjindo deep-sea area, the departure time was scheduled for 4:00 AM. Although the early start was demanding, particularly after a dinner engagement the previous night, I arrived at Daeyado after an hour of rest.

​

The Island-ho office operates a diner serving breakfast. The house-style set meal (baekban) is available for 8,000 KRW and represents a functional option.

​

Our TEAM MJT club members assembled by 4:00 AM, and we departed on schedule. The Island-ho shares hull characteristics with the Valkyrie 7, which I board frequently in Ocheon Port. The side decks are narrow, but the boat operates at speed. The captain ran the engines fully, reaching the grounds near Ongdo and Heukdo Islands in just over two hours. I anticipated a three-hour run, so the speed was functional.

​

Because the cabin lacked seating space during the run, I sat on the deck, which contributed to motion sickness. I had skipped taking medication due to forecasts of low wind and wave action, and spent the first two hours on the grounds recovering.

Cuttlefish in the Shinjindo area are targeted in deep waters. Because depths range from 40 meters to 60–70 meters, the captain recommended size 30 sinkers. This required me to adjust the setups I had prepared.

​

> **Tackle Specifications**
>
> *   **Setup 1:**
>     *   **Rod:** Aomax Pattern Black
>     *   **Reel:** Doyo Coba 3 Gold 5.6:1 baitcasting reel
>     *   **Line:** Haedong Limit X9 1.2 braided line
> *   **Setup 2:**
>     *   **Rod:** Roguman Hwan 175MT
>     *   **Reel:** Banax Kaigen 100B electric reel
>     *   **Line:** Shimizu 8-carrier 1.0 braided line
> *   **Rig:** Size 30 sinkers
> *   **Jigs:** Sene Jota Shrimp, C-Nex Anchovy, Yo-Zuri Sam-bong Purple, Yo-Zuri Tinsel L6 Size 3, Yo-Zuri Ultra Laser HRM, Yamashita Toto Sutte AJ, local shrimp/cuttlefish jigs, DIY branch line/direct rigs.

​

I paired the Aomax Pattern Black with the Doyo Coba 3. For deep-water cuttlefish, a reel with a 5.6:1 gear ratio is more manageable than a higher 6.0:1 or 7.0:1 ratio under load. I also set up the Roguman Hwan rod with the electric reel, which performed effectively in deep-water drifts last year.

The deep-water cuttlefish fishery near Shinjindo was less active than last year. Consistent numbers appear to require favorable conditions. During slow current periods, targeting these deep areas can be challenging. On this trip, the slow current limited overall activity.

Despite the long transit time, Shinjindo drifts remain of interest. The average size of the cuttlefish caught was good, with both branch line and direct rigs producing strikes.

​

Because the bite was slow, the electric reel setup lacked feedback. I relied primarily on the Aomax Pattern Black. While I generally prefer fast-action rods, using the moderate-action Pattern Black helped me identify light touches in slow conditions. The flexible blank was functional for detecting subtle weight changes compared to a stiff 9:1 rod.

My spot was on the left deck forward of the cabin. On this day, the boat drifted stern-first, meaning the anglers at the back had first access to the drifts. Given the low density of cuttlefish, strikes rarely reached my position forward. The club members at the stern landed fish consistently while I worked to pick up occasional fish on the drifts.
