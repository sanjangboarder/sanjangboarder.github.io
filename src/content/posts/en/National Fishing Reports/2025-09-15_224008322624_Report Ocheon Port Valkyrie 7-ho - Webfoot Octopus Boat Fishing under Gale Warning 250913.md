Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last weekend, we held the TEAM MJT webfoot octopus boat fishing outing out of Ocheon Port. Prior to the trip, I spent considerable time monitoring wind forecasts on Windfinder and Windy, as the conditions fluctuated between strong winds and brief periods of calm, making it uncertain if charter boats could operate.

The captain of the Valkyrie (Ocheon Lure School) had notified us on Wednesday that because of the volatile weather, he would offer full refunds to anyone wishing to cancel. I verified this with our 21 TEAM MJT members. Five members requested cancellations, but another five registered immediately, so we proceeded with our full charter reservation for 21 anglers.

​

By Friday evening, the forecast suggested the high wind warning would be lifted by 6:00 AM on Saturday. We planned to gather at the port office by 6:30 AM. However, around 5:00 AM, while driving to the port, the captain contacted me to report that the Coast Guard had extended the gale warning (high wind advisory) for the Chungnam sector, meaning boats were restricted from leaving. In ten years of boat fishing, I had heard of such sudden extensions but had not experienced one.

​

I notified the TEAM MJT members. Since most had already reached the port office, I met them there. Since we could not depart, we had breakfast together. Mankul Fishing, Breadry, and I had prepared giveaway items for the event, so we distributed them using rock-paper-scissors games and took our group photos ahead of schedule. By 7:00 AM, the sun was out and conditions looked calm, but the warning remained active, preventing departures. We decided to wait until 8:00 AM to see if the restriction would clear, giving members the option to return home. Only three members chose to leave.

​

I planned to wait until 9:00 AM before heading back. Fortuitously, around 9:10 AM, we received notification that the gale warning would be lifted at 10:00 AM. After verifying with the Coast Guard, we confirmed we could depart at 10:00 AM. One member, Overhead Kick, who was waiting at the port, joined our group, bringing the total to 19 anglers.

​

By 9:30 AM, Ocheon Port was calm. Once the restriction cleared, over twenty charter boats prepared to leave.

​

We were glad to get on the water after the delay. The members had shown high interest in going out despite the weather, so canceling the session would have been disappointing. We were set for a busy session.

​

The boats departed at 10:00 AM. The Valkyrie 7-ho cleared the harbor around 10:10 AM. We ran for about 40 minutes, passing Wonsandaegyo Bridge to reach grounds between Wonsando Island and Godaedo Island.

​

I set up my tackle on the deck during the run.

​

For this session, I added the Fishing Korea Keutpanwang Jjugab rod to my rotation, pairing it with the KastKing Cestrel reel fitted with a double power handle. My primary setup remains the Dark Air rod (47g) and Creman A9 Plus reel. This lightweight configuration has a compact profile that fits well in the hand, making it suitable for active octopus drifts.

​

The Keutpanwang Jjugab rod worked well during its first outing. Given its price point of around 50,000 KRW, its cost-to-performance ratio is notable.

My rig relied on the Etech Omija weighted jig (aeja), rotated with Etech Odd Eye and Cynex Chokkomi watermelon laser jigs. Although water clarity was moderate, all three patterns produced strikes. During the morning, about 70% of the webfoot octopus were caught on the Omija weighted jig. After 3:00 PM, the response to the weighted jig decreased, and about 70% of the catches shifted to the standard jigs.
