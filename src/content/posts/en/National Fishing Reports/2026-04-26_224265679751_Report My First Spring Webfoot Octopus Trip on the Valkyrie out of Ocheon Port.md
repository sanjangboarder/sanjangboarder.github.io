Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last Saturday, I went webfoot octopus fishing out of Ocheon Port. Spring egg-carrying webfoot octopus boat fishing is a popular seasonal genre in the West Sea. While I had not targeted them during the spring before, reports of last year's strong catches drew my interest, prompting me to book a trip.

​

Because the water is cold and the octopus are sluggish during spawning season, high catch counts are uncommon. The optimal conditions occur during weak tidal currents. According to the tide table, the periods of April 25–27 and May 9–10 represent the best tide windows.

​

​

The webfoot octopus closed season (*closed season*) starts on May 11, meaning this is the final window for spring trips. Webfoot octopus begin moving when water temperatures reach 8°C. Recent Boryeong water temperatures sat between 12°C and 14°C, providing suitable conditions alongside clear weather.

​

Returning to Ocheon Port six months after the autumn season, the atmosphere was busy. While some fleets targeted webfoot octopus, others prepared for the opening of the monster flatfish season.

​

This trip was an official TEAM MJT outing, boarding the **Valkyrie**, a prominent cephalopod charter vessel out of Ocheon Port.

*Left: Ashika Mahon | Right: Daiwa Rise*

​

I used the Choi-pro West Sea rod paired with the lightweight Ashika Mahon reel and 130mm double handle during the morning. The setup weighed under 200g, reducing arm fatigue. This was my first time using the Ashika Mahon 100B reel; the drag and retrieve handled the load well when paired with the 130mm handle.

​

🔗 [Ashika Mahon 100B Baitcasting Reel (Naver Purchase)](https://naver.me/x0OMctnh)

​

My second setup paired the Oslo 39g rod with the Daiwa Rise reel. The Oslo 39g is a light rod. Although I prefer rigid rods, this moderate 8:2 action rod registered the subtle weight of sluggish spring octopus.

​

🔗 [Daiwa 26 Rise Saltwater Baitcasting Reel (Naver Purchase)](https://naver.me/GNJBkGLH)
