Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I hope you had a pleasant Chuseok holiday. Last Saturday, I went cuttlefish boat fishing out of Daecheon Port aboard the Yangpa-ho. Due to holiday commitments, this report is slightly delayed. Following a disappointing trip to Shinjindo aboard the Island-ho, I scheduled this trip to the Sanghongseo and Hahongseo reefs in Daecheon to target cuttlefish.

​

Although it was the second day of the Chuseok holiday, traffic to Daecheon Port was clear. The parking area was less crowded than anticipated, likely because the long holiday period distributed the visitors. The return trip was also free of delays.

​

It has been a year since my last trip on the Yangpa-ho. The captain welcomed us as usual. Because he is attentive and works consistently throughout the session, I selected this boat for our TEAM MJT club event.

​

The outfitter indicated water depths would range from 15 to 40 meters, so I prepared sinkers from size 18 to 30 alongside rods rated for those weights. The N.S. Cuttlefish rod is suited for heavier sinkers rather than light size 10 sinkers. Using a size 18 sinker, the rod offered clear sensitivity, but switching to size 30 reduced target feedback regardless of depth.

​

> **Tackle Configurations**
>
> *   **Setup 1:**
>     *   **Rod:** N.S. Cuttlefish RS 1452H
>     *   **Reel:** NH Korea Core Solid baitcasting reel
>     *   **Line:** Haedong Pinatura PE Professional 1.0 braided line
> *   **Setup 2:**
>     *   **Rod:** Aomax Pattern Black
>     *   **Reel:** Doyo Coba 3 Gold 5.6:1 baitcasting reel
>     *   **Line:** Haedong Limit X9 1.2 braided line
> *   **Rig:** Size 18–30 sinkers
> *   **Jigs:** Sene Jota Shrimp, C-Nex Anchovy, Yo-Zuri Tinsel L6 Size 3, Yo-Zuri Ultra Laser HRM, Yamashita Toto Sutte AJ, local shrimp/cuttlefish jigs, DIY branch line/direct rigs.

​

The Aomax Pattern Black was comfortable to manage when set up with a size 20 sinker. Although I switched to a size 30 sinker when moving deeper, I kept it mounted when returning to shallow water to avoid retying. The delayed feedback on the moderate-action rod made detecting strikes difficult, though it was slightly more responsive than the fast-action Cuttlefish rod under that load. 

​

To summarize the session, my target numbers were not met. Reflecting on the trip, my strike detection was functional in the morning while using size 18 and 20 sinkers in shallow water. However, after switching to size 30 for the deep run, I lost the feel of the rig and spent two to three hours without positive feedback. Keeping the heavy sinker in shallow water was an error. Switching back to size 20 near the end of the day improved detection.

I rotated jigs throughout the morning, but no single design emerged as highly effective. Cuttlefish activity levels were low, requiring consistent effort for individual catches. The Yo-Zuri Tinsel L6 Size 3 provided the most consistent results. Other anglers on board reported success using the Yamashita AJ, while the Yo-Zuri Sam-bong jig did not receive interest.

In the morning, we fished near Hwasado Island. Despite the shallow depth, the cuttlefish caught were of a good average size, larger than those from the previous Shinjindo outing. Later, we fished deep waters near Sanghongseo and Hahongseo, where both small and large cuttlefish were caught in combination.

While weather conditions were favorable during the previous Daeyado trip, this session also offered functional conditions: overcast skies but low wind and minimal swell. Despite the stable parameters, cuttlefish activity remained low.

Catches were made at a slow pace. During the deep-water drifts, a purple shrimp-imitation jig produced several strikes. When the current slowed, a direct droploop rig generated positive responses. Some short strikes occurred, leaving cuttlefish skin fragments and slime on the hooks, suggesting my timing was slightly behind.

My afternoon catch count matched the morning results. Had I adjusted the sinker weight sooner, my final count would likely have been higher; the quiet period during the middle of the day was an oversight.

My final count was 32 cuttlefish and 5 webfoot octopuses. The top angler on board landed approximately 50 cuttlefish.

​

My position on the right bow was favorable, making the low count a result of my own technical adjustments.
