Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I went on an unscheduled fishing trip due to a last-minute cancellation, completing my final webfoot octopus outing of the season aboard the **Mageompo Jayeon Fishing**. The trip coincided with a spring tide and a sudden temperature drop, which lowered the water temperature and promised challenging conditions.

​

Even in tough conditions, we head out when the boats are running. Nearshore cuttlefish had mostly moved out, and deep-sea cuttlefish reports from Shinjindo were sparse, so I focused on webfoot octopus. After filling out the boarding manifest at the Jayeon Fishing office, I headed to the Mageompo Breakwater.

​

By late November, the cuttlefish season is largely over, and webfoot octopus is expected to taper off within a couple of weeks. Even if the octopus remain active, the cold temperatures will limit fishing. I will need to look for sheltered locations or indoor venues for the winter.

We cruised toward Geoado Island. Since the deep-sea cuttlefish yields near Shinjindo had been low the previous day, the captain opted to target nearshore webfoot octopus while keeping an eye out for remaining cuttlefish.

Regarding gear, I had brought my Oslo 39G rod, but after learning we would be using size 20 sinkers, I left it in the vehicle. Instead, I set up two heavier rods suitable for size 18 to 20 sinkers: the Choi Pro Seohae rod with a size 18 sinker, and the NS Cuttlefish RS 1452H with a size 20 sinker.

​

> **Mageompo Webfoot Octopus Gear & Tackle**
>
> *   **Rod 1:** Choi Pro Seohae
> *   **Reel 1:** NH Korea Core Solid
> *   **Line 1:** Haedong Pinatura PE Professional 1.0 PE
>
> *   **Rod 2:** NS Cuttlefish RS 1452H
> *   **Reel 2:** Doyo Coba 3 Gold (5.6:1 gear ratio)
> *   **Line 2:** Haedong LIMIT X9 1.2 PE
>
> *   **Tackle:** Sinker size 18 to 20, Delli Rig BB Custom (20cm leader), various egi (C-Nex multicolor laser/watermelon/red laser, Yo-Zuri Tinsel L6 Size 3/Ultra Laser HRM, Yamashita Toto Sutte AJ, YL open/spiny lobster).

​

Because the octopus were sparse, I rotated through several egi styles to find active colors.

​

No single egi style dominated the catch. During the rotation, we found that octopus would strike various colors, though the overall population density in the area had clearly thinned out.

The afternoon yield was similar to the morning's. I did not keep a precise count, but estimated my total catch at around 50 octopus.

​

The top angler on the boat landed approximately 100 octopus, so my egi choices may have been less effective on the day.

​

The webfoot octopus and cuttlefish season, which started in September, is winding down. I had aimed to fill two freezer compartments with this season's catch, and achieved about two-thirds of that goal. After sharing the catch with friends, I have about half a compartment remaining.

​

The 2025 season had its share of challenging weather, though it remained productive. Anglers wrapping up their seasons should clean and store their gear in preparation for next year.
