Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

November 1st was scheduled for the TEAM MJT monthly outing. We planned to target deep-sea cuttlefish on the Jayeon Fishing out of Mageompo Port, but the weather was unfavorable. Throughout this autumn, weekends have consistently seen poor weather, a pattern that weekend anglers will likely recognize.

​

Along the Taean coast, there are few natural barriers to block the wind compared to the sheltered waters of Cheonsuman Bay. Consequently, wind speeds between 7 and 10 m/s often result in trip cancellations. The operator initially cancelled the trip the day before, but reinstated it once the forecast improved slightly.

​

Despite the strong wind warnings, the conditions inside Mageompo Port were relatively calm. Although my expectations were modest due to the wind forecast, the trip went ahead. Deep-sea fishing around Shinjindo Island, Heukdo Island, and Gungsido Island was not possible due to rough seas, so the captain planned to operate in the sheltered inner bay not far from Mageompo.

​

The Jayeon Fishing team, including the captain and deckhand, are consistently helpful, which makes for a reliable charter experience. The captain indicated that decent-sized cuttlefish could still be targeted in the inner bay despite the wind, so we proceeded.

​

Given the windy conditions, I set up a single rod. Although the inner bay spots were relatively shallow, the wind caused the boat to drift rapidly. The captain requested that we use size 18 sinkers to maintain bottom contact, so I chose the Choipro Seohae metal tip rod, which handles size 16 to 18 weights effectively.

​

Because we were targeting both cuttlefish and webfoot octopus, I used a double branch line rig with two egis.

​

> **Tackle Specifications**
>
> *   **Rod:** Choipro Seohae Rod
> *   **Reel:** NH Korea Core Solid Baitcasting Reel
> *   **Line:** Haedong Pinatura PE Professional 1.0 braided line
> *   **Sinker:** Moncross Dimple Sinker Size 18
> *   **Rig:** Tulbo Fishing BB Custom branch line rig with 20 cm spacing
> *   **Egis:** C-Nex Chokkomi (horse mackerel, red-head laser, crystal watermelon), watermelon anchovy, Ultra Laser HRM, Yamashital Toto Sutte AJ, military camo egi, Deep Slow watermelon

​

The most effective rig combination in the morning consisted of the military camo egi and the C-Nex Chokkomi Crystal in watermelon. These watermelon and camo patterns are generally effective for cuttlefish and webfoot octopus, and they performed well during this outing.

Conditions improved in the afternoon as wind speeds dropped to around 5 m/s, but the morning session was characterized by significant waves.

The fishing spots were located close to Geoyado Island near Mageompo Port. Due to the high winds, other charter boats from Mageompo operated in similar areas. Although the boat drifted rapidly, cuttlefish were active in these inner bay spots.

​

The average size was larger than the deep-sea catches from the Shinjindo region earlier this year. I landed about two-thirds of the day's cuttlefish before lunch. The red-head laser pattern was effective, suggesting it is a viable option for shallower inner bay settings.

My overall catch for the season has been moderate compared to my targets. Although webfoot octopus have been abundant this year, my outings consistently coincided with rain, wind, or freshwater inflow, which limited the total catch. This leaves me looking forward to next season.

​

With the cuttlefish and octopus season winding down, I plan to target rockfish, focusing on trips to Seonjae Fishing Park. I will share reports from those outings as they occur.
