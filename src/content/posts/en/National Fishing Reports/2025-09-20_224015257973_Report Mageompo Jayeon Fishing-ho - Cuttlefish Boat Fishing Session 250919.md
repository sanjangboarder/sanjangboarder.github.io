Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I went on my first cuttlefish boat fishing trip of the 2025 season to Taean, boarding the **Jayeon Fishing-ho** from Mageompo Port. Although it is still early in the cuttlefish season, I was looking forward to this trip, remembering the active deep-sea cuttlefish bite around Ongdo Island and Gungsido Island near Shinjindo Island last year.

​

Let's look at how the early deep-sea cuttlefish action compares to last season.

​

It has been a little over a month since my last trip on the Jayeon Fishing-ho for white croaker in August. Visiting the office again, the staff noted that the first few days of the cuttlefish season this week had been challenging. Although that was slightly concerning, I headed to the boat with anticipation, as fishing conditions can change throughout the day.

​

With several boats transferring operations to Mageompo Port last year, the port area becomes active during the webfoot octopus and cuttlefish seasons. Parking is manageable, though you may need to park further away and walk to the docks during peak seasons.

Here is the tackle configuration I prepared for this cuttlefish trip.

​

I targeted a setup weight under 200g. I used the NH Korea Dark Air rod, which has a fast, lightweight action. I paired it with a Creman A9 Plus reel tuned with a Seahawk double handle, bringing the reel weight to approximately 135g. The total setup weight is just over 180g. The 9:1 action and fast response made this my primary setup for the day.

​

I also prepared a Loguman Hwan rod paired with a Banax Kaigen 100B electric reel in case we fished deep drifts (40–60 meters). A longer rod can be helpful when managing deep-water rigs.

​

Early in the session, I used a weighted jig (aeja) alongside standard jigs to target both webfoot octopus and cuttlefish. Because the drifts were centered on cuttlefish grounds, the weighted jig saw little action, so I transitioned to a standard sinker setup using size 18 to 20 sinkers.

We spent the morning fishing the incoming tide around Geoyado Island. Cuttlefish were caught consistently, though not in large schools. The depth ranged from 10 to 20 meters, which made for manageable fishing conditions.

​

Before lunch, we moved near the Shinjindo breakwater area. We fished depths of 30 to 40 meters. I expected larger average sizes in the deeper water, but the size distribution was similar to the Geoyado grounds. I had caught approximately 30 cuttlefish by noon. The bite was subtle, with few distinct strikes; most fish slowly loaded the rod tip or touched the jig briefly before releasing, requiring careful attention to detect.

In the afternoon, the Real Shrimp jig in watermelon pattern produced results, and the Spiny Lobster pattern produced 13 consecutive strikes, indicating a shift in feeding preferences. Although the fish were active, they continued to touch the jig lightly, resulting in several tentacle skins on the hook points from missed sets.

Because the waves were high offshore, the captain decided to return to the Geoyado area. Offshore Shinjindo had notable swell due to the depth, but the 10–20 meter depths near Geoyado were more shielded.

​

The feeding patterns remained consistent after moving, but I adjusted my rigs. In deep water, a direct-connection rig was effective. In the shallower waters near Geoyado, a branch-line setup with a 15cm dropper and a 20cm branch line produced more responses. Cuttlefish continued to bite at a steady pace.

​

​

With the constant rain, my hands became cold. I removed my gloves near the end of the session, which led to an issue.

​

While clearing a bottom snag, my primary setup—the NH Korea Dark Air rod and Creman A9 Plus reel—slipped from my wet hands and was lost in the water. I had managed the gear all day with gloves on, but removing them for the final drift resulted in this loss. This highlights the importance of wearing fishing gloves in wet conditions.

​

As it is early in the season, I was hoping to reach 100 cuttlefish, but the weather conditions and feeding activity made it challenging. I missed several strikes due to delayed hook sets. Prompt hook sets are critical for cuttlefish, which I need to focus on next time. I have another deep-sea cuttlefish trip scheduled from Mageompo Port in October.
