Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Among West Sea boat fishing targets in May, flatfish downshotting is the most popular, while spring cuttlefish (*bom-gap-i*) and tairaba snapper fishing pick up in mid-May. Having completed three flatfish trips, I turned my attention to spring cuttlefish and snapper. Last weekend, I visited Ocheon Port with a small group of TEAM MJT members to target spring cuttlefish on the **Valkyrie**, a premier cephalopod charter boat.

​

Catches from the Valkyrie and sibling ship Val-7-i on the preceding days showed top anglers landing three to four cuttlefish.

Ocheon Port is a major hub for West Sea boat charters, and the Valkyrie is well known. Operating out of the Ocheon Lure School fleet, we gathered at the office at 4:30 AM to sign the manifest before heading to the boarding pontoon.

​

​

We departed before sunrise. The Valkyrie's skipper is known for running "combat sessions"—departing before sunrise and returning only when the sun sets. During May, June, and July, this means departure before 5:00 AM and return between 7:00 PM and 8:00 PM.

​

*Note: Anglers utilizing commercial bus charters should note these timings, as they can cause scheduling conflicts. However, anglers who prefer max time on the water favor this schedule.*

For this trip, I used the same rods I configured for spring webfoot octopus. Because we operated Size 16 to 20 sinkers, choosing rods that match the target sinker weights is key. I selected the lightweight Oslo 39g rod and the Choi-pro West Sea model.

​

> **Ocheon Port Cuttlefish Gear Configurations**
>
> *   **Setup 1:** Oslo 39g Rod | Daiwa Rise 100 Reel | Haedong LIMIT X9 PE 1.2 Braided Line
> *   **Setup 2:** Choi-pro West Sea Rod | Creman Little Giant Reel | Haedong LIMIT X9 PE 1.5 Braided Line
> *   **Rigs:** Tulbo Fishing Deli-rig BB Custom
> *   **Squid Jigs (Egis):** C-Nex Eongttungi, Silver-Scale Anchovy, Camel Jig, Delion Sambong (triple hook), AJ jigs
> *   **Sinkers:** Size 16 – 20 (60g - 75g)
>
> *SanjangBorder*

​

To avoid losing high-quality cuttlefish jigs to snags, I used slightly heavier braided main lines. The Haedong LIMIT X9 line is thin and smooth, so using PE 1.2 or 1.5 did not cause excessive water drag.

​

My reel choices were the Daiwa Rise and the Creman Little Giant.
