Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

ŌĆŗ

July and August mark the peak of the West Sea white croaker (Baekjogi) boat fishing season. While many anglers also target flounder via downshot, red seabream via tai rubber, or seabass via live-shrimp waesujil, white croaker remains the most popular target species north of the Chungnam region. It is the easiest boat fishing genre for beginners and guarantees a decent harvest of edible fish to take home.

ŌĆŗ

A few years ago, there was a common saying that a successful white croaker trip meant catching 100 fish. However, this count typically consists of small-sized fish, which I personally find less appealing. If you prefer high quantities, heading to Hongwon Port, Daecheon Port, Muchangpo, or Ocheon Port is recommended. If you want fewer but significantly larger, giant-sized white croaker, you should select a charter boat that targets giant white croaker spots.

Mageompo Port's *Jayeon Fishing* is a legendary charter boat that pioneered targeting giant white croakers in Korea years ago. I book trips with them every year. Focusing on size rather than quantity, I made two bookings for this season. Finding myself back at Mageompo Port for the first time since last fall, I noticed that several new charter boats have launched, and the port facilities and buildings have been modernized and improved.

The chart below shows the depths around Taeseo, which typically range from 40 to 50 meters. Since you must add the tide level at low/high tide to these numbers, the actual depth on your fishfinder is usually 5 to 10 meters deeper.

ŌĆŗ

This is a deep-sea white croaker spot, which is rare in the shallower West Sea. While white croaker is the main target, you can also catch croaker (min-eo), red seabream, conger eel, rockfish, and greenling.

*Taeseo Giant White Croaker Spot in Mageompo Offing*

ŌĆŗ

Since the giant white croaker spot is deep, a stiff rod is easier to manage than a soft rod. I packed the stiffest rod in my collection, the JS Company Liberty Ocean 4-power. I also brought an AOMEX Navigator, but realizing at the port that I had mistakenly brought the Tai Rubber model, I stuck to a single rod. I brought two reels just in caseŌĆöa baitcasting reel and an electric reel.

ŌĆŗ

Unfortunately, the PE line on my Creman Little Giant snapped in the middle of the morning session. For the remaining hours, I operated my Banax Kaigen 100B electric reel.

ŌĆŗ

Using an electric reel in deep water is incredibly convenient. While a manual baitcaster is twice as light and offers much better sensitivity for feeling subtle bites, switching to an electric reel is a smarter decision during the intense summer heat to conserve energy.

I have visited Taeseo for about 5 years, and it is never an easy spot. On this day, a typhoon passing near China created massive swells. Standing on the bow, I began feeling seasick within 5 minutes, but managed to push through after taking motion sickness medication.

ŌĆŗ

Fortunately, the giant white croakers showed up. The largest fish reached up to 40cm, and the majority were solid fish around 30cm. While we caught some smaller fish mixed in, the average size was far superior to other quantity-focused spots in the Chungnam area.

One of the reasons I fish out of Mageompo is that it is the only place in Korea with local businesses offering semi-drying (ban-geon-jo) services for your catch. When I dropped off my 5kg catch, I paid 8,000 KRW per kg. While it is slightly expensive, hand-cleaning this quantity of fish in the summer heat is something I wanted to avoid. I also took a photo with my fishing partner, Mankul Fishing.

Despite the heat, I enjoyed the steady action and returned safely to the port. On a great day, the catch can easily exceed 10kg. I have another trip booked for August 1st, and hope to report a massive catch then.

ŌĆŗ

If you are planning a giant white croaker trip, keep Mageompo's *Jayeon Fishing* in mind. You will find that booking a spot with them is highly competitive. If you plan to go, book early. Booking is also available through the *About Fishing* app, which offers discounts during events.
