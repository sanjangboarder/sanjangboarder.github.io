Heading into the final group match, South Korea faced strict criteria to advance: they needed to win by at least a 5-run margin while allowing 2 runs or fewer. Statistically, this represented a 15% probability. Despite the pressure, the players maintained their focus to secure their spot in the quarterfinals.

The upcoming WBC quarterfinal schedule begins on March 14. Having cleared the group stage, South Korea travels to LoanDepot Park in Miami, Florida. From the quarterfinals onward, the tournament transitions to a single-elimination bracket format.

​

According to the WBC schedule, the South Korean quarterfinal match is scheduled for the morning of March 14 (KST). With games split between Houston and Miami, South Korea is bracketed in Miami. Fans in Korea will be able to watch the first single-elimination match against the Dominican Republic early Saturday morning. Many, like myself, will be tuning in live.

From the quarterfinals through the championship game, a single defeat results in elimination. Because a single error can decide the outcome, strategic decisions regarding starting rotations, bullpen usage, and lineup construction are critical for each game.

​

Our quarterfinal opponent, the Dominican Republic, holds a statistical advantage. International media outlets, including MLB.com, rank the Dominican Republic as the No. 3 contender for the championship title, while ranking South Korea at No. 9.
