### 🤖 Crimson Desert Auto-Fishing

​

For players with limited active time or those who want to leave the game running overnight, the auto-fishing system is a useful feature. While auto-fishing takes longer to set hooks compared to manual fishing and carries a higher chance of losing top-grade fish, the convenience is significant.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MThfMTYx/MDAxNzc2NTEzMDIyMTEx.N5OmzwAhDf3muS6D7y6uLHRfhrWKBS5rg2nKY7vxcyYg.ZGsgzebo0Z52GXhf2uji2BLTcK2nZjsI9DWL0jP0jsEg.PNG/naver_blog_img_1776512952489_7.png?type=w800" alt="Auto-Fishing Setup" />
</div>

​

Before leaving your character, empty your inventory, restock your bait, position yourself at a safe spot, and enable auto-fishing. You will return to a full inventory of fish.

Avoid selling your catch to the nearest merchant. Certain trade merchants or chef NPCs in specific towns buy certain species at higher rates. Identifying these buyers can increase your returns by up to 1.5 times.

​

Additionally, using fish as cooking ingredients produces buffs. Preparing meals using your catches before entering high-level hunting zones is a useful preparation step, saving gold on consumable items.
