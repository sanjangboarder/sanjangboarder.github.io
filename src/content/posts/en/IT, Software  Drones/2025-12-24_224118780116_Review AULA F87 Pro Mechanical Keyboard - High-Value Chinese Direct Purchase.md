​

> **AULA F87 Pro**

​

This mechanical keyboard is widely known in South Korea as the **"poisonous spider"** (*독거미*) keyboard. It has built a reputation for offering exceptional performance relative to its price point.

​

While the product name is AULA F87 Pro, "AULA" itself does not translate to "poisonous spider." The Chinese brand name is *Lanzhu* (狼蛛), which translates to "wolf spider." However, South Korean users started calling it the "poisonous spider" keyboard, and it has since become the standard nickname in the domestic community.

​

​

Compared to high-end mechanical keyboards, the AULA F87 Pro offers significant value. This is largely due to the satisfying typing feel relative to its cost. The Gray V4 switches have an actuation force of around 40g, which is not overly light, but they press down smoothly and offer a springy return. The typing sound is a clean, pebble-like clack (often described in Korea as *"dogak-dogak"*). While it produces some sound, the profile feels refined rather than noisy.

​

If you plan to use it in a very quiet office, the sound might be noticeable to coworkers. For such environments, a version with silent switches would be more appropriate. In my office, the sound level did not cause any issues.
