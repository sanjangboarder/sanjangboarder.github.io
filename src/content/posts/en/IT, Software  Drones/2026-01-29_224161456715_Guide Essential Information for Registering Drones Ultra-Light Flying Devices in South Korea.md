Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Lately, I have been posting updates on drone regulations. Under South Korean aviation law, there is a requirement known as the registration of ultra-light flying devices. While recreational pilots operating small drones generally do not need to register, today I will examine the specific rules in detail.

​

---

​

Although consumer drones are classified as ultra-light flying devices under local law, registration is only mandatory under specific conditions defined by Article 2 of the Aviation Business Act and Article 24 of the Enforcement Decree of the Aviation Safety Act.

​

First, if you operate a drone for **commercial purposes (for profit)**, you must register the device before flying, regardless of its weight.

​

"Commercial purposes" refers to any activity where the captured media generates financial revenue. Common examples include providing paid photography services or uploading videos to YouTube to earn ad revenue.

​

If your YouTube channel has fewer than 1,000 subscribers and does not generate revenue, immediate registration is not legally required. However, if your channel subsequently grows and meets monetization requirements, ads will run on your older videos as well, which can retrospectively categorize those flights as commercial. If your goal is to grow your channel, it is best to register the device early.

​

Second, even for non-commercial flights, registration is mandatory if the drone's **maximum takeoff weight (MTOW) exceeds 2kg**. Most consumer camera drones weigh less than 2kg. Drones exceeding this limit are typically industrial models used for agricultural spraying or firefighting.

​

**"In summary, if you operate a consumer drone solely for recreational use, registration is not required. However, if you fly for commercial purposes or exceed 2kg MTOW, you must register the device."**

Let's look at the classifications of unmanned flying devices.

​

Drones fall under the category of **Unmanned Multicopters**. This is the option you select when registering your device for commercial use on the portal.

​

Assuming you need to register a drone for commercial purposes, here is the registration process.

​

Access the Drone One-Stop portal linked below. On the main page, select the first icon: **Device Registration (Registration of Ultra-Light Flying Device)**.

​

🔗 [Drone One-Stop Civil Service Portal](https://drone.onestop.go.kr/)

​

The submission process is straightforward. I will guide you through the forms step by step.

​

First, you need the model name, manufacturer, and production date of your device. If the production date is not listed on the packaging, you can enter the purchase date or an approximate date.
