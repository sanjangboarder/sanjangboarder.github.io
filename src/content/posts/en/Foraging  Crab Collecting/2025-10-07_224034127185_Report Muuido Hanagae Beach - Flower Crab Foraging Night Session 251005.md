Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Incheon features several prominent locations for night foraging (haerujil), including Muuido Island, Yeongjongdo Island, Yeongheungdo Island, and the Sihwa Seawall. Among these, Hanagae Beach on Muuido Island is noted for blue crabs (flower crabs). Since the blue crab ban period lifted in August, I visited once, but I was unable to capture photographs on that trip. On this occasion, I took several pictures to share.

​

To participate in blue crab foraging, you can navigate to Hanagae Beach in Incheon.

​

Because the summer season has concluded, the public beach is officially closed. However, looking out into the distance, you can see numerous lights as shown below. Many foragers gathered despite the holiday period.

To find the correct tide times for foraging at Hanagae Beach, you should consult the Muuido or Incheon tide tables. Foraging is generally practical when the low tide water level drops below 150 cm. My visit on October 5 coincided with a low tide level of 97 cm at 9:51 PM, which was the first day of the sequence below 150 cm.

​

October 8 features a negative tide, where the low tide level falls to -7 cm. On such days, deeper areas that are normally submerged are exposed, offering potential for higher catches.

The foraging grounds at Hanagae Beach are divided into Zone 1 and Zone 2.

​

Beginners should limit their activity to Zone 1. Hanagae Beach is a location where tidal accidents occur, and you should avoid venturing too far out.

​

It is recommended to enter 1.5 to 2 hours before low tide, set an alarm for the low tide time, and begin returning immediately once the alarm sounds.

​

Beginners should not go alone and should be accompanied by experienced foragers.

​

Zone 2 requires crossing a tidal channel (water channel) and presents higher risks. The approximate location of the channel is marked below. Because you must navigate around the channel, entry is only practical during spring tides, and returning can be difficult if you are unfamiliar with the terrain. Beginners should avoid this area, and solo entry should be avoided.

Although it was only the first day of the spring tide sequence when the water does not recede fully, I entered expecting to find crabs, and succeeded.

​

Because searching requires wading up to waist depth in chest waders, I could only take photographs after returning to the shore. I caught approximately 10 blue crabs. Spotting crabs swimming in the water using the flashlight and catching them with the hand net is engaging, which is why many anglers pursue this activity.

The catch was primarily male crabs, showing a meat yield of around 80%. While the yield was moderate, I expect crabs in the next spring tide sequence to show higher yield as they prepare for colder conditions, though they may migrate slightly deeper.

​

The crabs provided a pleasant breakfast for the family during the holiday. The spring tide conditions during the holiday are suitable for foraging in the Incheon area. Anglers planning a trip should check the weather and tide tables to ensure a safe outing.
