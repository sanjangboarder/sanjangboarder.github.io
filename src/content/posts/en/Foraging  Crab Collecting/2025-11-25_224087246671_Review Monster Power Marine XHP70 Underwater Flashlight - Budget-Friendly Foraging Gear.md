Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

In this post, I introduce a budget-friendly piece of night tidal flat foraging gear. When walking the tidal flats at night, flashlight brightness is a key factor affecting your catch rate. I generally use a 1,000 to 2,000-lumen headlamp as a continuous safety light, combined with a high-power handheld flashlight to scan longer distances.

​

A handheld flashlight needs to deliver over 3,000 lumens to penetrate the water surface effectively. Having a fully waterproof underwater flashlight is ideal, and I located a budget-friendly option matching these requirements: the **Monster Power Marine XHP70**. I purchased the full kit to evaluate its performance and features.

​

The kit arrived in a large packaging box containing a wide range of accessories.

This level of illumination is sufficient for identifying crabs or conches in shallow water. A minor drawback is the lack of brightness adjustments and the presence of a high-frequency flicker effect. Because typical foraging trips last only two to three hours, and the flashlight can run at full power for that duration, the lack of dimming is not a significant issue.

Anglers who have visited the tidal flats at night may have observed others using high-power searchlights. The Monster Power Marine XHP70 belongs to this category of high-intensity lights. Flashlights brighter than this model are generally limited to heavy searchlight systems with external battery packs. While external pack searchlights offer maximum brightness, they are heavy and significantly more expensive. For recreational foraging, the Monster Power Marine XHP70 represents a practical balance of brightness and cost-effectiveness.
