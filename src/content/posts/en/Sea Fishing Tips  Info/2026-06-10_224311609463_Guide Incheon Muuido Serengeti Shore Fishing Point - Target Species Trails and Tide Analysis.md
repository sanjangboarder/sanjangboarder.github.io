Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

In the metropolitan area, two primary shore-casting spots have gained heavy popularity for large Spanish mackerel: the Sihwa Seawall and Muuido Island in Incheon. Muuido holds several rock points, including the Deokjeom/Deokjin breakwaters and adjacent rocks, the Serengeti point, the Silmido rock fields, and the Muuidaegyo Bridge zone. Among these, the most popular spots are the Deokjeom Breakwater and the Serengeti.

​

Most Muuido points yield seasonal catches:

*   **Spring:** Rockfish, greenling, and egg-bearing webfoot octopus.
*   **Early Summer:** Large Spanish mackerel, flatfish (flounder), and flathead (*jang-dae*).
*   **Autumn:** Spanish mackerel, flatfish, flathead, webfoot octopus, and cuttlefish.

​

This is a premier shore-fishing zone in the metropolitan area. Today, I introduce the Serengeti point, with a guide to the Deokjeom Breakwater coming in my next post. This guide is tailored for beginners looking to visit Muuido or anglers planning their first trek to the Serengeti.

### Muuido Serengeti Entrance Routes

The Serengeti point (locally referred to as *Muraingeti*) can be reached via several paths, though the most popular option is the mountain hiking trail.

​

​

The trail route is outlined on the map below. Starting from the Muuido Gwangmyeong Port Public Parking Lot, the trail spans roughly 2.6km. It is a hiking path with repeating inclines and declines, crossing three hills. Outside the peak summer heat, the hike takes about 40 minutes. Experienced hikers or trail runners can cover it in 30 minutes.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfMjQx/MDAxNzgwODI5ODc1NDEz.tV7kchi9ew1uC6vV7_MwNXmy7c6K6WgFkQ9EkKxZXXQg.H7VreHsjdWtPAw0I7T0Xa0isvIfMSMQL6gZH_n_wuocg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Serengeti Hiking Trail Route" />
</div>

​

Alternatively, you can walk along the rocky shoreline. While it avoids elevation changes, the walk is longer at 3.3km. Walking over uneven rocks and boulder fields is physically taxing. Furthermore, high tides submerge the rocks, limiting access to mid-falling tides and below. Expect a walk of about an hour along this route (45–50 minutes for fast walkers), which takes longer than the mountain trail.

​

*Tip: If you can park near the Deokjeom Breakwater, the walking distance is reduced by over 1km. However, given the heavy volume of anglers visiting Muuido, securing a spot near the breakwater is difficult on weekends, though possible during off-peak weekday hours.*

​

The advantage of walking the shoreline instead of the mountain path is that the shoreline itself holds productive casting zones, allowing you to target flatfish and flathead without walking all the way to the Serengeti.

​

​

Navigating these routes using static maps can be challenging. I recommend downloading a tracking app like **Trangle** on your smartphone. Open the app and search for **"Muuido Backpacking"** (무의도 백패킹). The recorded routes will guide you along the shoreline access path. This app is highly useful for first-time visitors.

### The Muuido Serengeti (Muraingeti)

This location was originally an active stone quarry. Because the flat clearing resembles a wild grassy savannah, it became known as "Muraingeti" (Muuido + Serengeti). Before anglers arrived in numbers, it was popular as a primitive wild backpacking destination. Because it cannot be accessed by vehicles and holds shallow waters that prevent boats from beaching, it remains a secluded spot for wilderness backpackers.

On weekends, you will regularly see backpackers camping in the clearing. Carrying heavy backpacking packs over the mountain trail or rock fields requires significant effort. Note that the area holds no public facilities, toilets, or trash cans. It is a wilderness zone, and visitors must carry out all trash.

By studying satellite photography, we can measure the width of the rocky zone exposed at low tide. This is critical information for planning casts.

Measured from the high-tide line, the rocky reef beds in front of the Serengeti clearing extend out 50 to 70 meters. At the adjacent **Gate of Fortune** (Eobok-ui-mun) point, the rocky reef extends out 30 to 50 meters.

​

To reach the deeper blue water zones, your cast must clear 50 to 130 meters from the shoreline.

### Target Species and Tide Windows

At low tide, the rocky reef beds are exposed, yielding to mud flats beyond. You will primarily target species that feed over these sandy mud bottoms.

​

Depending on water temperatures, flatfish and flathead are the primary targets, and webfoot octopus appear in numbers during autumn. In the 2026 spring-summer transition, flathead feeding was active, and Spanish mackerel catches were steady. In September 2024, the webfoot octopus bite was highly active, yielding triple-digit catches. While target activity varies annually, flathead, flatfish, and webfoot octopus remain reliable options.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfMjIw/MDAxNzgwODMyMDIyMTI2.qIv7cPybqpD6a5Pmj3gIJPwEmxlcKUL69MCf0d16HyYg.di9pMA9nMoV91r3Lu2wIIWpNRhs_P0gqt1IKyyu5nHQg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Low Tide Reef Exposure" />
</div>

​

Measured from the dry high-tide line, the closest edge of the intertidal flat is 58 meters away. At spring low tides, you can walk out onto the exposed flats, allowing you to cast further into the main channels. The period from low tide through the early rising tide represents the most active bite window.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfMjk5/MDAxNzgwODMwODM0NDYx.JqgTlkwv_wtZr2NCxAbRhoCd8FOyjzKNUWv5YmpzdMsg.oEgKhPg0LRyQ3B3WGU2QxE034hSoMklzo9F54FCL288g.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Low Tide Access Zone" />
</div>

​

The active fishing shoreline spans approximately 350 meters. Casting inside the shallow coves is unproductive, as they lack sufficient depth even at high tide.

​

This season, large Spanish mackerel have drawn heavy crowds. Given the 350-meter shoreline, spacing anglers at 5-meter intervals limits capacity to roughly 70 people. When I arrived at 4:00 AM on a recent Saturday, over 100 anglers were already on the beach. Weekdays draw around 50 anglers, meaning that to secure a prime spot, you must hike in the previous evening.

​

​

By mid-June, Spanish mackerel numbers decline, and target focus shifts to flathead and flatfish. In autumn, medium-sized Spanish mackerel return alongside webfoot octopus. Depending on the year, cutlassfish and chub mackerel also move inshore.

### The Gate of Fortune (Eobok-ui-mun) Point

Located closer than the main Serengeti beach, this point features a rock formation resembling a gateway, from which it takes its name.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfNzUg/MDAxNzgwODMxMzE2ODI3.PmoOqOR2JDXzJzArzx0FHs1SQJKBSbNOiVk_uzz4KW0g.-LBxWvQDcurTlo0Tb6krOOXunfhfnnZD9vFQIrAc0v0g.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Gate of Fortune Rock" />
</div>

​

This point is structured as a rocky headland (spit). At high tide, it accommodates only a few anglers, though low tide exposes space for about ten people.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfNjQg/MDAxNzgwODMwOTY0Disy.4x3LRATKP3kLFKf5VtXiDXh0y4WFEW2Y4AClywsn2NMg.BwzMGZKpTV8EOlrfB5qMsSDdwZkGLiVyRUL93YELqM8g.PNG/SE-4d7f23bf-8833-4358-b57d-fcaf9e5f18f7.png?type=w800" alt="Gate of Fortune Layout" />
</div>

​

During spring high tides, you must cast at least 100 meters to reach the drop-off, which is more challenging than the Serengeti beach.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA2MDdfMjA1/MDAxNzgwODMwODU3OTAx.dRsoxROaQBwAaX885rhKqO1E-j8cPFGAkPxzTKj3qOQg.yF4-it2RYG2SeMndFek-UNeyp7EmJfBv699Wb4b8t0sg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Gate of Fortune High Tide Depth" />

​

However, at low tide, you can walk out to the edge of the rocks, where a 50-meter cast reaches depths of 7 meters, making it a productive zone.

The catch profiles match the Serengeti beach. Because standing room is limited, it is less popular. Additionally, as rising tides reduce standing room, crowded conditions can lead to disputes, making it best suited for falling tide windows.

### Muuido Crowd Indicators

By mid-June, Spanish mackerel numbers decrease, which will ease crowding. However, weekend mornings will remain busy through June, requiring a 1:00 AM to 2:00 AM arrival to secure a spot.

​

​

To gauge crowd density before hiking: if the Gwangmyeong Port and Deokjeom Breakwater road parking is full but the main public parking lot is empty, you will likely find the last available spots on the beach. If cars are parked down the Gwangmyeong Port access descent and the main public lot is already filling, the beach will be full, and hiking in will likely not yield a spot.
