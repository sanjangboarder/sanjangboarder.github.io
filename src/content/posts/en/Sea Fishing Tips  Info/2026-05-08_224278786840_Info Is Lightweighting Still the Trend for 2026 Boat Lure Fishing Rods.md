Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Looking at the boat and lure fishing rods released since last season, the most dominant and distinct trend is **lightweighting**. Until two or three years ago, lightweight blanks were restricted to mid-to-high-end rods priced above 100,000 to 200,000 KRW. Today, this lightweight trend has trickled down to budget rods under 100,000 KRW, making it the industry standard.

​

While reduced weight offers massive advantages in mitigating fatigue, lightweighting is not a cure-all. In this post, I share my thoughts and analysis on what this lightweight trend represents for anglers.

### Advantages of Lightweighting

​

#### 1. Reduced Fatigue

The primary benefit of a lighter rod is a massive reduction in angler fatigue, particularly during full-day boat fishing trips where you hold the rod continuously. The benefit varies depending on the fishing genre, but lighter setups generally provide the greatest advantages when using lightweight rigs.

​

For instance, flatfish downshotting typically utilizes sinkers around size 40 (approx. 150g), whereas webfoot octopus (jukkumi) fishing uses sinkers around size 10 (approx. 37g). Lighter gear yields a more noticeable drop in fatigue in webfoot octopus fishing where the rig itself is light.

​

Reduced fatigue not only prevents general arm fatigue but also dramatically reduces joint pain in the fingers, palms, and wrists used to grip the reel seat. In my experience, when I went webfoot octopus and cuttlefish fishing every weekend three years ago, I suffered from finger joint pain that lasted through the winter. Since switching to an ultra-lightweight setup two years ago, I have been completely free of such joint pain.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMDdfMjMx/MDAxNzcyODcyMzQxNjY3.7gVLzJhN4UJmjfb5iXkeYX5v3uervE-WmfFLM1tzQCEg.H75I6CcwQXVAC1S9JIMDTf_bv3wnChdmxj0gA-r-B2Yg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

#### 2. Advanced Blank Materials

Carbon fiber is the standard blank material. High-elasticity carbon improves rod sensitivity and action but carries lower physical durability. You have likely noticed carbon designations like Toray's T700, T800, T1000, or T1100G in rod marketing. Other carbon manufacturers use designations like 20-ton, 30-ton, or 36-ton carbon. Toray, however, specializes in manufacturing carbon fiber that delivers high durability relative to its modulus, using proprietary model names.

​

If you are looking for an ultra-lightweight rod today, models utilizing Toray's T1100G, M40X, or M46X represent high-end, high-elasticity, lightweight blanks.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMDdfMTkw/MDAxNzcyODgxNTA2NjY5.Wjxs0KOnqi1qfDclg0gd348eutavuX7FCN82GE0GH9cg.HxA7ccf0EJ1QtHF4mCuBBDvAJOvl7T5vUTitIvSCSTcg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

#### 3. Premium Guides, Reel Seats, and Rear Hoods

Utilizing titanium guide frames and Torzite rings is another primary way to shed weight. Selecting Fuji's titanium Torzite guides is the premium standard, though they are rarely seen on boat rods due to high component costs. Consequently, manufacturers often select third-party titanium frames or modern carbon fiber guide systems.

​

For reel seats, manufacturers reduce weight by using minimalist skeleton-style seats or carbon-infused resins. Since genuine Fuji reel seats are expensive, many manufacturers turn to Chinese components or proprietary custom designs.

​

For the rear hood (butt grip), while contoured EVA foam grips were historically popular for aesthetics, modern lightweight rods often feature highly minimized, compact grip designs to shed grams.

​

While guides, reel seats, and butt grips have evolved to support lighter designs, the high cost of these parts means that rods featuring name-brand Fuji components are generally restricted to premium price ranges.

​

### Weaknesses of Lightweighting

​

#### 1. Reduced Durability

As mentioned, high-elasticity carbon blanks deliver excellent responsiveness but are inherently more fragile. To reduce weight without compromising durability, material costs rise exponentially. Consequently, many rods accept a minor tradeoff in durability to hit a weight target.

​

Manufacturers claim to counter this using proprietary wrapping technologies, but many are simply marketing names. The most reliable solution is using advanced, durable high-modulus carbons like Toray's T1100G or M40X. If a rod's specifications list standard 40-ton or 46-ton carbon without advanced fibers, it is safe to assume it carries lower impact durability.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMDdfMTEz/MDAxNzcyODgxNzc5NzU1.Hm2J-EH8Sz_xtdmHfz5AlDP2HRg9PC7QntTtD1uHgI0g.-zAbeB9s8T8s-j6WiSdnCc4ukuLy1PFGAfrwrrfzRWkg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

#### 2. Price Increases

Similarly, moving towards high-modulus carbon blanks naturally increases manufacturing costs. Beyond blank materials, premium components like titanium and Torzite guides double or triple the final price of the rod. Minimizing reel seat weight with exotic composites also contributes to price increases.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMDdfNDkg/MDAxNzcyODgxODgzOTQz.PNwZsjrl7CM0o-Zxe89Enl6f8iw6gZX-IesQ-B7rFY8g.SxcsTQt6eTXbre7fX28LKJWkBA3Rsra1TKDfM-F7DQgg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

#### 3. Rod Balance

A lighter physical weight does not automatically translate to a lighter feel in the hand; rod balance plays a major role. Positioning the center of gravity closer to where your hand grips the reel seat makes the rod feel significantly lighter in action. A well-balanced rod positions its pivot point near the reel seat even before mounting a reel.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMDdfMjkz/MDAxNzcyODgzODM2MjA0.BYm_xgze2MFvlm3eKA0lRR2woc0cWTCsMUM6VrVHOxog.T-lz_tINoAn7NgCT8GZ5LHurLMT9qJcxKCbELDoFNDUg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

Achieving ideal balance requires keeping the rod tip section exceptionally light and shifting weight towards the butt section. However, because modern lightweighting trends also minimize the butt grip, balancing the rod is becoming harder. Shifting weight via the blank itself adds physical weight, making the ideal tuning difficult. As a result, some ultra-lightweight rods carry poor physical balance, which is a key drawback.

​

### Conclusion

​

Looking at the West Sea boat fishing scene, flatfish downshot, slow jigging, and light jigging rods under 100g are becoming common. Specialized webfoot octopus rods are emerging under the 50g mark, with models as light as 37g or 39g launching this season. Cuttlefish rods are also common around 60g. These weights represent nearly half of what rods weighed a few years ago, and buyers are increasingly filtering purchases based on weight.

​

For mid-range boat rods targeting the West Sea, weights around 80g to 90g (or lower) are becoming the mainstream standard. Similarly, walking inshore sea fishing rods are moving under the 100g threshold.
