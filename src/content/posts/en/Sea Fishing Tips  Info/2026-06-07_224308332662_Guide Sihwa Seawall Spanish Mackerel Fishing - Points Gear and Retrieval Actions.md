### Sihwa Seawall Spanish Mackerel Casting Points

Spanish mackerel are migratory pelagic fish that follow schools of baitfish like anchovies and gizzard shad. Point selection is driven by where these baitfish push close to the shore. Here are the primary casting zones:

тАЛ

#### 1. Narae Service Area to Daebudo Island Section (Point 1)

This section acts as a massive daily casting field. Spanning the seawall between the Narae Service Area and Daebudo Island, this zone yields steady daily catches ranging from three to over ten large fish.

This zone is highly popular because of its easy access. On weekends, anglers line the 3km stretch of the seawall at 3-to-4-meter intervals, accommodating over 500 people. Due to the high density, line tangles are common, requiring mutual consideration. Water depths range from 8 to 10 meters at low tide when casting long distances, and spring high tides can bring depths of up to 18 meters, creating favorable conditions.

#### 2. Tidal Power Station to Oido Pier Section (Point 2)

I prefer to avoid the crowds at Point 1. The stretch between Narae Service Area and Oido Pier remains open and less crowded, even on weekends. Spanish mackerel run through this zone, and I have had steady success here. The waters close to the Tidal Power Station are deeper than Point 1 and hold strong currents. This spot is primarily visited by local anglers, as the walk from Narae Service Area keeps casual visitors away.

тАЛ

The advantage here is the lack of crowds, allowing you to cast wide angles with minimal risk of line tangles.

However, water depths decrease as you walk toward Oido Pier, so avoid visiting at low tide. If fishing at low tide, stay near the Tidal Power Station where depths remain sufficient. For roughly 700 meters from the station, depths are deeper than Point 1, and the remaining stretch remains deeper than the Bangameori area.

### Maximizing Success: Peak Times and Tides

Early summer Spanish mackerel are targeted almost exclusively via metal jig casting. While I switch to float setups in autumn, early summer casting relies on a critical factor:

тАЛ

**You must arrive at the point one hour before sunrise, and start casting at civil twilight.**

тАЛ

Over ten years of targeting Spanish mackerel, the most active bite window near the seawall consistently occurs from civil twilight through the first two hours of sunrise. Regardless of the tide state, this sunrise window represents the primary peak time. Check tide apps (selecting Songdo, Sihwaho, or Bangameori) to verify daily sunrise and tide timings.

### Retrieval Actions and Techniques

Observe the water surface. Look for current rips, diving seagulls, surface baitfish boiling, and where other anglers are casting. If the seawall is crowded, choose sections with lower angler density to avoid tangles.

тАЛ

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA6MDdfNDYg/MDAxNzgwNzkyOTM2ODY1.SwVt9vUeH8CV9jKZhNtfKp3EKJfDpctrpUFHQUahYKIg.MOnem1nltlDJ2honlPXO2vIeOpIJG5ibuAwJtaxJdRog.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Seawall Crowd Management" />
</div>

тАЛ

If currents are hard to read visually, monitor where your line drifts after casting. Focus your effort when tidal currents change direction or slacken. You can measure current speed by counting how many seconds it takes for your jig to reach the bottom.

тАЛ

If seagulls gather or fish boil on the surface, cast as close to the activity as possible. If no surface activity is visible, cast long distances to search varying depths.

тАЛ

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA6MDdfMTQ5/MDAxNzgwNzkzMTAzODQ5.BlHajXX5XnYAm6qNIOZsXXna_ffDNoDgxPv2WPq4wR8g.naua494DnD9J2nSvKiqBwtibnHt3uFo9lPLPFQA3WLAg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Bird Activity" />
</div>

тАЛ

Upon splashdown, choose between a free fall or a curve (tension) fall. A free fall allows the jig to drop vertically by releasing line, while a curve fall holds line tension with the bail closed as the jig swings back. Use free falls when space is clear, and switch to curve falls when the seawall is crowded or crosswinds blow your line. Count down the fall to gauge depth and current speed.

тАЛ

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA6MDdfOTQg/MDAxNzgwNzkyODA1MDAy.pv58EEmBV7-QX2WoohjADLc8TZ2Z7oAWTSwOOxPWEQ8g.vBQe7P0ECubRGn2rtTuNrzO5j19kBd5HrgzHDN7gTaMg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Casting Fall Choices" />
</div>

тАЛ

After the jig hits bottom, run two or three sharp jerks and let the jig fall back to the bottom. Alternatively, run continuous jerks before the jig hits bottom, counting how many seconds the jig takes to fall back. This allows you to estimate what depth your jig is running at.

тАЛ

тАЛ

Vary your retrieve speed. Fast retrieves pull the jig toward the surface, while slow retrieves allow the jig to swim deeper. Adjusting retrieve speeds helps identify what depth the fish are holding at, which is critical as Spanish mackerel feed from the surface down to the bottom.

тАЛ

If someone nearby hook-sets a fish, observe their retrieve speed or ask what depth they targeted. Gathering information from nearby anglers helps identify the active pattern quickly.

### Common Beginner Pitfalls

While beginners can seek advice from experienced anglers, many rely on online reports and overlook the importance of **civil twilight**. Arriving at the seawall before sunrise requires early hours, and casual anglers often miss this window, which reduces success rates. Spanish mackerel casting requires maximizing your chances, and starting before sunrise is the primary step.

тАЛ

Beginners also overlook the importance of casting distance. Configuring a casting setup independently can be difficult. I recommend matching the rod length, reel size, braided line power, and metal jig weights used by experienced anglers to build casting efficiency before upgrading your gear.

тАЛ

If you plan to target Spanish mackerel this season, prioritize two rules: fish the sunrise window, and configure your tackle to maximize casting distance. The early summer season runs for about a month, and I wish you success.
