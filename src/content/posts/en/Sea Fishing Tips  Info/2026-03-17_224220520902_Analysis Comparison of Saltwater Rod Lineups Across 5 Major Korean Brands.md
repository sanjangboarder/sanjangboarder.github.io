Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I was curious about which domestic Korean tackle brands manufacture the largest number of rod models and cover the widest range of fishing genres. Since this information is not readily available online, I conducted an independent database review.

​

To establish a clear standard, I collected product specifications from official websites and catalog databases for five representative domestic manufacturers. I attempted to automate this search using AI tools, but due to database structural variations, I ended up researching and cross-referencing the specifications manually.

​

This survey covers five leading Korean brands. I focused on rods currently marketed for saltwater use. Because shopping portals categorize rods differently, I relied strictly on official catalog data from websites and manufacturer forums. Saltwater categories are divided into four main groups:

*   **Saltwater Lure Rods:** Inshore utility, sea bass, rockfish (*mebaru*), horse mackerel (*aji*), and eging (squid) rods.
*   **Boat Fishing Rods:** Flatfish downshot, webfoot octopus, cuttlefish, tip-run, tenbin, and cutlassfish (*beltfish*) rods.
*   **Rock/Float Fishing Rods:** Iso (float) rods (primarily 4.5m to 5.3m specifications) and telescopic whip poles (*Minjangdae*).
*   **Surf Casting Rods:** Surf casting models and heavy rockfish/striped beakfish (*Ishidai*) rods.

​

A total of 585 rod models were analyzed. **Banax** offered the largest total catalog volume, and **Boat Fishing Rods** constituted the largest category by volume across all brands.

​

Below is a brand-by-brand breakdown.

​

### 1. JS Company

In my opinion, JS Company designs some of the most visually appealing blanks in the domestic market. Their focus centers primarily on lure rods for both casting and boat applications. They place significant technical focus on blank engineering. Having used several of their rods, their action is reliable, though their long-term durability is average.

​

*   **Lure Rods:** 24 models
*   **Boat Rods:** 83 models
*   **Float Rods:** 0 models
*   **Surf Rods:** 3 models

### 2. Banax

Banax is likely the largest domestic tackle brand in Korea. Beyond rods, they offer a full lineup of reels, apparel, and general accessories. They have a good reputation for reel engineering. Based on my use of their rods and reels, they offer high cost-effectiveness. One disadvantage is that their cosmetic designs are conservative.

​

*   **Lure Rods:** 20 models
*   **Boat Rods:** 129 models
*   **Float Rods:** 34 models
*   **Surf Rods:** 15 models

​

Banax is one of the few domestic brands that maintains a complete portfolio across every category.

### 3. N.S (N.S Rods)

N.S Rods is a standard domestic brand that focuses on producing reliable, moderately styled rods. While they offer a small selection of accessories, their technical resources are concentrated on blank and guide layout development.

​

They cover most saltwater disciplines available in Korea, maintaining a diverse catalog:

*   **Lure Rods:** 44 models
*   **Boat Rods:** 66 models
*   **Float Rods:** 22 models
*   **Surf Rods:** 7 models

### 4. AOMEX

This is a brand I have monitored closely since last year. They introduced a large number of new models at the recent Korea International Fishing Show, though these are excluded from this review as their official website specifications are not yet updated. Their current catalog is focused on popular genres, featuring distinct cosmetics.

​

They offer products from entry-level to higher-spec brackets, and represent a brand to watch. Rods constitute their main business area.

*   **Lure Rods:** 16 models
*   **Boat Rods:** 97 models
*   **Float Rods:** 1 model
*   **Surf Rods:** 4 models

### 5. HDF (Haedong)

Haedong is widely recognized for its "Charisma" line of hard coolers. They operate as a general tackle distributor, offering a large variety of accessories alongside their rod line. Their rod catalog is compact, and unlike other brands, they have a higher proportion of rock/iso float rods.

*   **Lure Rods:** 4 models
*   **Boat Rods:** 5 models
*   **Float Rods:** 9 models
*   **Surf Rods:** 2 models

Prior to compiling these figures, I did not anticipate that boat rods would occupy such a dominant share. This reflects the popularity of boat charter fishing in Korea, showing that casting, shore, or float-fishing venues have a smaller market footprint.

​

Regarding total rod model counts, Banax is significantly larger than its competitors. N.S follows in total volume. Aomex and JS Company occupy a similar volume tier. Based on their releases at the recent fishing show, I expect Aomex to expand its lineup to match N.S in the near future.
