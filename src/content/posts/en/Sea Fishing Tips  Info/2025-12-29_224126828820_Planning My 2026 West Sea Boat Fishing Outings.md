Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

To plan my fishing activities for 2026 and secure early bookings, I drafted a tentative schedule based on the annual tide tables. For the upcoming year, the weekend tidal conditions appear more favorable for recreational anglers compared to 2025.

​

As regular readers know, my fishing routine consists of lure and float fishing at paid commercial fishing ponds from winter to spring. During this period, I occasionally organize expeditions to the South Sea or Jeju Island depending on conditions.

​

From late spring to late autumn, I focus on shore lure fishing and charter boat fishing in the West Sea. Because shore fishing is physically demanding, I have been increasing the proportion of boat fishing. Over several seasons, I have established a set of preferred target species for each season in the West Sea.

The calendar above shows the regular and flash outing schedule for the **TEAM MJT sea fishing community**. I selected optimal Saturdays or Sundays based on the 2026 tide tables for regular club trips. Because tidal cycles repeat every two weeks, the weeks with less favorable tides have been designated for smaller flash outings.

​

If you are planning boat fishing trips in the West Sea next year, this schedule provides a useful reference. For reference, recruitment for these trips is underway on the TEAM MJT Naver Cafe. Due to the favorable dates, approximately two-thirds of the autumn webfoot octopus and cuttlefish bookings filled in under five minutes.

​

If you would like to join our outings, consider registering at the TEAM MJT cafe. Booking for the autumn octopus trips will be finalized in January, while the remaining trips open for reservations approximately one to two months before the scheduled date.
