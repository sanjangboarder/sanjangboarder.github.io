**1. Daiwa's Two Design Philosophies: LT and SF**

Recently, Daiwa's versatile spinning reels are released with models prefixed as **LT (Light & Tough)** or **SF (Super Finesse)**.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA3MDRfMjc2/MDAxNzgzMTM1OTY1OTg0.00t7V04aburwEVayAHi1EEPScT5mhb2mjCIhaUwl7Rwg.T5837lBilFKvtJ49U8-kdg4XQBZ5AABweuYSOzUTdEsg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" />
</div>

**LT (Light & Tough)** is Daiwa's core design concept that thoroughly pursues weight reduction in all delicate parts—body, spool, and handle—while maintaining the toughness required to withstand harsh environments. You have likely seen the "LT" prefix on many Daiwa reels for years.

​

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA3MDRfMzEg/MDAxNzgzMTM1OTkwNDg0.0n4zFaVgZ6TD0Tw0nSO3QPFhw5apa66BatbKvKEYUp8g.-KrzcdZ3Ws1TeEyXJJ1--ecp2X6lfJa5FCxPpsjWMlgg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" />
</div>

On the other hand, the newer concept **SF (Super Finesse)** represents a design dedicated to finesse fishing, allowing millimeter-level precise control. By shortening the spool, line friction is minimized, achieving a drastic weight reduction to just 140g (for the 2500 size). Additionally, it improves vibration sensitivity by about 16%, and increases casting distance and swing speed, making it highly specialized for precise manipulation of lightweight lures. Last year's Airity series introduced these SF models.
