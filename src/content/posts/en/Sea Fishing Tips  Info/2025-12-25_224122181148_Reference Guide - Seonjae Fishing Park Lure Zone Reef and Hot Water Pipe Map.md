Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

During the winter season at Seonjae Fishing Park on Seonjae Island, Incheon, rockfish generally gather around stone break lines, artificial fish reefs, net cages, steel bridges, and heat pump hot water outlets. Depending on water temperature variations and the characteristics of the stocked rockfish, the best spots can shift among these structures. Based on experience, you can generally expect action at two of these five types of points on any given day, which makes referencing a terrain map useful.

​

Seonjae Fishing Park is unique in operating heat pumps directly within the saltwater pay-fishing pond. The lure fishing zone is entering its fourth year of operation. You can review my detailed breakdown of the hot water pipe layout from earlier this year in the link below.

Recent catch patterns at the park have shifted from net cages to stone break lines and back. Last week, fish were caught consistently around the reefs. Rather than focusing on a single structure, trying multiple locations is key. The hot water pipes are scheduled to begin operating on December 25, which generally draws rockfish to these areas.

​

The yellow lines on the map represent the hot water pipe layout. Anglers can reference this to plan their casting directions.
