Due to scheduling conflicts, I have not booked a trip on the **Victory-ho** out of Jindu Port, Yeongheungdo, which is a popular boat fishing charter in Incheon. I missed the booking window for this holiday weekend as well.

‚Äã

While Incheon boat fishing charters operate out of Yeonan Pier and South Port, the primary fishing grounds are located near Jawoldo, Muuido, or further out near Deokjeokdo. Jindu Port on Yeongheungdo offers the fastest access to these key points.

‚Äã

Jindu Port hosts numerous charter operators. Since the choice of boat regulates the day's catch, selecting a reliable charter is important. My primary choice is the Victory-ho.

‚Äã

Their office is located in the Coast Guard alley. Manager Hong assists visitors upon arrival, and the shop is stocked with tackle, rigs, and rental gear, allowing anglers to visit without bringing personal gear.

The Victory-ho fleet operates three boats. While all are reliable, this post focuses on the main Victory-ho. This boat is a 3-ton, 9-passenger vessel, operating as an outboard motorboat configured to carry up to 8 anglers.

‚Äã

Although smaller than standard 9.77-ton commercial passenger vessels, it accommodates four anglers on the port side and four on the starboard side, providing generous casting space for each passenger. Additionally, a group of 8 anglers can book the entire boat as a private charter‚Äîa rare option among Incheon charter fleets.

The Victory-ho targets different species depending on the season. Flatfish have appeared early this year; the boat targets monster flatfish in May and shifts focus to red seabream (snapper) once the Tairaba season begins in June.

‚Äã

The 2026 booking schedule shows bookings are open for flatfish downshotting and Tairaba based on tide cycles for May and June.

One of the skipper's specialties is live shrimp bait fishing (*oesujil*). He targets artificial reefs and rocky reef zones. The compact outboard motor design of the Victory-ho allows it to maneuver into tight reef structures where larger commercial vessels cannot fish.

Tairaba setups catch both flatfish and red seabream. With bookings for red seabream starting in May, it is a fun option for target variety. I enjoy Tairaba because it yields a range of species‚Äîincluding rockfish, greenling, and flathead‚Äîespecially from mid-May onward when activity rises.
