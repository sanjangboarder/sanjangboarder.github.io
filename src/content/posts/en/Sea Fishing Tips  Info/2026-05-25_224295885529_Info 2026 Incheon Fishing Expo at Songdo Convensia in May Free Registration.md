Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

I regularly share updates on domestic fishing expos. The second of the three major fishing expos held in the Seoul metropolitan area this year is starting this Friday in Songdo, Incheon. Since the exhibitor list has been released, I have compiled a quick summary of the event details.

​

Up until last year, the Incheon Fishing Expo at Songdo Convensia was held in August. This year, the organizers moved it forward to May. Since the same organization runs the subsequent "Recreational Fishing Expo," they likely swapped the order of the two events.

​

​

You can register for free entry using the link below. If you are debating whether to attend, I recommend signing up. It takes only 30 seconds to register, and doing so avoids having to pay an admission fee at the door if you decide to go.

🔗 [2026 Incheon Fishing Expo Official Site](https://sjinfotec.com/incheonfishingkorea/index2026.asp)

​

The 2026 Incheon Fishing Expo runs from Friday, May 29th to Sunday, May 31st. I plan to visit on Friday morning. Since I live in Songdo, it is just a 10-minute bus ride or a short stroll.

Now, let's examine the exhibitor list. I have included last year's list below for comparison, and unfortunately, this year's show is even smaller. The Incheon show is historically the smallest of the three major regional expos, and seeing it contract further is disappointing.

​

Looking at the brands I typically check out, they include V.KOOL sunglasses, Oriental, Yujeong Fishing, EGI-TEC, Pro Fishing (Daiwa), and Big Hit Fishing. As you can see, major tackle manufacturers are absent. If you want to see major global brands, the Korea International Fishing Show held in KINTEX, Ilsan every March is the best choice.

While I visit every year since it is close to home, I doubt it is worth a long trip to Songdo just for the expo. However, if you live nearby or within a one-hour drive, combining the visit with a walk in Songdo Central Park and dining at local restaurants makes for a pleasant weekend outing.

​
