​

During the winter season of January and February, the Lure Zone at Seonjae Fishing Park in Incheon closed for renovations. Following this period, the facility reopened on Saturday, February 28.

​

Seonjae Fishing Park is recognized for its main float-fishing pond, and its Lure Zone is also popular among casting enthusiasts.

​

​

The Lure Zone was previously one of the few commercial ponds dedicated strictly to artificial lure casting. Following the current updates, the dedicated lure section is limited to the former B, C, and D lines, while the A and E lines have been converted into a **Mini Float Zone**.

​

Given these changes, referring to the entire area as the \"Walking Zone\"—split into the Lure Pond and the Mini Float Pond—is more accurate.

​

The original basin was divided such that the Lure Pond is slightly larger than the Mini Float Pond.

While the reduction in lure casting area is a limitation, admission fees have been adjusted from 120,000 KRW for 10 hours to **100,000 KRW for 12 hours**. This reduces the hourly rate by approximately 30%, improving access for anglers.

​

### The Lure Pond

The key updates in the Lure Pond center on the waterwheel configuration. The waterwheels near the B-line and C-line coves have been reactivated, establishing a clockwise current flow through the basin as shown on the map.

Regarding rig rules, **casting balls (up to 10g)** are now permitted in the Lure Pond. Previously, the prohibition of casting floats meant that 90% of anglers targeted the pond floor. The introduction of casting balls allows presenting lures in the middle and upper water columns.

​

While casting balls are typically used to increase casting distance, at Seonjae they will function primarily as adjustable float systems. This will be practical during the red sea bream season to hold lures at specific depths.

​

Equipping a casting ball with an electric chemical light or using a weighted float allows tracking your line's entry point during night sessions.

### The Mini Float Pond

Stocked facilities for float fishing include the Mint pond at Manjeong in Yeongjongdo and the Dokdo Mini pond in Daebudo. Seonjae's configuration differs by combining a compact basin size with a walking layout, allowing anglers to move around the entire pond.

​

The pond utilizes the former A and E lines. The cages are configured as floating units. Only one waterwheel is currently active in this section.

Regarding equipment rules, rods are limited to a maximum length of 3 meters (*accommodating standard lure rods up to 9 feet*). Rock/iso float rods under 3 meters are also permitted. Telescopic whip poles (*Minjangdae*) are allowed up to 7 meters. The allowance of 7-meter poles may lead to fixed-point fishing in mobile zones, which is a minor limitation; a uniform 3-meter limit would be preferred.

​

Additionally, **braided lines are prohibited** in this zone. Anglers must use nylon or fluorocarbon lines (*floating or semi-floating*). This rule is intended to minimize line tangles, but represents a restriction for anglers transitioning from the Lure Pond.

​

For safety reasons, heavy bullet floats (*Daepo-jji*) are banned. Standard stick floats or dome floats are permitted, and are sufficient for the distances involved in this basin.
