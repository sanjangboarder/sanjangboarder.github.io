As March draws to a close, April is approaching. Among the popular saltwater boat fishing genres in the West Sea during April, spring webfoot octopus (*egg-carrying webfoot octopus*) is a key target. While I only go out a few times per season for this specific fishery, selecting a reliable charter boat improves the experience. Today, I share my recommendations.

​

Anglers typically target spring webfoot octopus in the Incheon and Chungnam regions. The Chungnam region—particularly around Ocheon Port—is a primary hub for this fishery, offering higher catch volumes due to a larger resident population. The 2025 autumn season was productive, which suggests a healthy population of overwintered octopus returning to spawn in spring 2026.

​

Webfoot octopus are sensitive to water temperatures. In low water temperatures, they remain inactive inside conch shell traps or structured cover. The spring season begins to produce consistent catches after mid-April, when water temperatures reach the 10°C to 12°C range. Even as water temperatures rise, they prepare for spawning inside conch traps, so catch rates do not reach autumn levels; instead, they strike jigs during brief feeding windows.

During the spring spawning season, commercial fishermen harvest large volumes of octopus using conch shell traps (*Sora-bang*), which exceeds the volume taken by recreational anglers. While fresh octopus is less common in wholesale markets during autumn, spring markets carry a large supply of egg-carrying octopus, making them a popular culinary item.

Because spring octopus are less active and more sensitive than autumn ones, selecting the correct tide cycle is critical. My experience suggests that days with slower tidal currents yield better results. Avoiding periods immediately after strong winds, which create turbid (*muddy*) water, is also important.

The annual closed season for webfoot octopus runs from May 11 to August 31. Water temperatures make early April to early May the target window. While May offers stable water temperatures, I recommend mid-to-late April as the peak period.

Just as heavy pressure at the start of the autumn season quickly depletes numbers, waiting too late in spring reduces the available stock. Because commercial conch traps harvest large volumes, timing your trip is key.

​

To allow less active octopus to track and strike your jig, slow currents are preferred. Tides from **1-물 to 3-물 (neap tides)** are optimal. While neap tide to 5-물 tides are fishable, targeting the 1-물 to 3-물 window is recommended for best results.

​

Unlike the competitive autumn booking season, reserving berths on charter boats in spring is straightforward, allowing you to select preferred dates.

​

Selecting the correct charter boat is important for spring octopus. You want captains who are familiar with spawning grounds where commercial fishers do not set conch lines, or those who have mapped specific structures where octopus hold during different tide stages. My recommended boats are the **Valkyrie** and **Valchiri** operating out of Ocheon Port.
