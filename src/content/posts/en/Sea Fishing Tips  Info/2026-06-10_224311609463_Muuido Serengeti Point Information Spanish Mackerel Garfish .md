Hello, this is Sanjangbodeo.

Recently, two places have gained popularity as major Spanish mackerel fishing spots in the Seoul metropolitan area: Sihwa Seawall and Muui-do Island in Incheon. Muui-do Island has more fishing spots than one might expect, including Deokjeom/Deokjin Breakwater and rock formations, Serengeti, the rock formations near Silmi-do Island, and Muui Bridge. Among these, the most popular spots are Deokjeom Breakwater and Serengeti. Most of the fishing spots on Muui-do Island offer a chance to catch most seasonal fish species.

In spring, rockfish, wrasse, and egg-laying webfoot octopus can be found, while in early summer, large Spanish mackerel, flounder, and croaker become the main targets. In the fall season, you can expect Spanish mackerel, flounder, and croaker as usual, plus webfoot octopus and cuttlefish – making it a rare and excellent fishing spot in the metropolitan area.

In this post, I'd like to introduce Serengeti, and in the next post, I'll introduce Deokjeom Breakwater. I hope this information will be helpful for beginners planning to visit Muui-do Island or those who haven't yet been to Serengeti.

**How to Access Muui-do Serengeti**

Serengeti is also known as Mu-rengeti (Muui+Serengeti). While there are several ways to access it, the most popular route is via the hiking trail.

You can get a general idea of the hiking trail access by looking at the map below. It's about 2.6km from Muui Gwangmyeong Aviation Public Parking Lot. It's a hiking trail with repeating ascents and descents, with about three hills and three downhill sections. Excluding the summer season when temperatures rise, it takes about 40 minutes to arrive. Those who run or are professional hikers can complete it in about 30 minutes.

Access via the rock formations is also possible. It might seem easier because there are no uphill sections, but the distance is longer at 3.3km, and as those who have walked on rock formations know, walking over irregular stones is not easy. During high tide, the rock formations are submerged, making access difficult. You can only attempt entry after mid-ebb tide, and you should allow about an hour. If you're good at navigating rock formations, you can get there in about 45-50 minutes, but it generally takes longer than using the hiking trail.

However, if you can start from Deokjeom Breakwater, the distance is reduced by over 1km. It might still take slightly longer than the hiking trail, but recently, there are so many anglers visiting Muui-do Island even on weekdays that it's impossible on weekends, though it might be possible during odd weekday hours.

The advantage of accessing via the rock formations instead of the hiking trail is that the rock formations themselves form fishing spots, so you can target the same fish species without having to go all the way to Serengeti.

Honestly, finding the access route just by looking at a map can be difficult. But what era are we living in, right? Haha. There's a way to try it even alone by tracking your current location with a smartphone.

Download an app called **Tranggle** to your smartphone. After launching the app, if you search for **"Muui-do Backpacking"** in the 'Find' section, you can follow the displayed route by looking at your smartphone. Among the routes I mentioned above, you can take the rock formation access route. I recommend this method for first-time visitors.

**Muui-do Serengeti 'Mu-rengeti'**

In the past, this area was a quarry, and because its landscape resembles a savanna, it started to be called Mu-rengeti. It was initially known not so much by anglers, but as a sacred place for wild backpacking. It's inaccessible by car and difficult to reach by boat due to shallow waters, so many backpackers seeking romance visit this spot.

If you look closely, you'll see it better. Based on where the water reaches its peak at high tide, Serengeti is composed of rocky flats for about 50-70m, and Eobog-eui-mun Point is about 30-50m away.

The image below shows the terrain elevation provided by 'Gaebanghae' (Open Ocean). The ivory-colored areas represent land on the map – land that isn't submerged even during extreme spring tides. The green areas are designated as sea, indicating parts that are submerged or not as the water comes in up to about 9m from sea level during extreme spring tides. The blue areas indicate where there's always water, regardless of low or high tide. Based on this, you'd need to cast about 50-130m from land to consistently reach water.

So, what fish species can you expect at Serengeti? Looking at the image below, you can see the entire rocky and reef flat exposed at full low tide. Beyond that, it's mostly flat mudflats. This means you can target fish species that prefer this type of terrain.

Depending on water temperature, flounder and croaker are the main species. In the autumn season, webfoot octopus also appear quite frequently. In '26, flounder appeared in the spring season, but croaker were particularly abundant. Spanish mackerel are also appearing very well compared to previous years. In '24, on September 1st, there was a huge success here with over a hundred webfoot octopus caught. While it's not guaranteed that specific species will appear every year, you can certainly expect croaker, flounder, and webfoot octopus as a baseline at this spot.

Based on 'Gaebanghae' (Open Ocean) data, the closest distance from the white areas (where water doesn't reach even during extreme spring tides) to the actual sea level is 58m. During extreme spring low tides, you can advance almost to the water's edge, which is when you can explore the widest range of the fishing spot. In fact, the best activity is often observed when the tide turns from low to incoming.

The actual fishable length of the Serengeti fishing spot is about 350m. The sections entering the inlets are practically meaningless as the water depth isn't sufficient for forward casting even at high tide, making them very inefficient.

This year, an exceptionally large number of anglers are visiting to target large Spanish mackerel. With a point length of about 350m, if people stand 5m apart, a maximum of about 70 people can fit tightly along the spot. I went in around 4 AM last Saturday, haha, and there were over 100 people there. They say about 50 people come even on weekdays, so to secure a good spot, you'd need to hike in the evening before.

Once the large Spanish mackerel season passes mid-June, the catch numbers will drop, and naturally, fewer people will target them. From then on, croaker or flounder will likely become the main targets for fishing. And in the autumn season, you can again target Spanish mackerel of 30-40cm, and webfoot octopus will appear. Although it varies each year, there have also been times when hairtail or mackerel came in.

According to the 'Gaebanghae' map, to cast to sea level during an extreme spring high tide, you'd need a casting distance of about 100m. This means the conditions are a bit less ideal compared to Serengeti.

However, the conditions change during low tide. At low tide, you can advance almost to the water's edge, and even casting just 50m forward can reach depths of up to about 7m, making it an excellent spot.

The fish species found here are essentially the same as those at Serengeti. Compared to Serengeti, there isn't as much space to stand, so it's not as preferred a spot. Especially if people are spread out fishing during low tide and the water comes in, the standing space becomes very narrow. So, it's worth trying during the high tide to low tide transition, but during the reverse, it creates conditions ripe for conflicts.

Now, as we enter mid-June, the popularity of large Spanish mackerel may wane a bit, but I still expect there to be no spots available during early morning hours on weekends throughout June. To secure a spot, I think you'd need to arrive by at least 1 or 2 AM.

To judge whether Mu-rengeti will be crowded, if the Gwangmyeong Port/Deokjeom Breakwater parking is full and the public parking lot is empty, you can consider yourself to be catching the last train (i.e., just barely getting a spot). If there are many cars starting from the downhill road of Muui Gwangmyeong Aviation Public Parking Lot and the public parking lot is already starting to fill up, it's highly likely you won't get a spot even if you struggle to get into Mu-rengeti.
