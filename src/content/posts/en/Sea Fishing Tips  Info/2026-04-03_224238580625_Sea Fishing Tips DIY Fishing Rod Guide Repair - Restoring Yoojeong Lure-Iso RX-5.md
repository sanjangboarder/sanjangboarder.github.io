Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

When float fishing along the Sihwa Seawall or Songdo, my primary setups are a 3.3m float rod purchased from AliExpress and the **Yoojeong Lure-Iso RX-5**. While the AliExpress rod is serviceable, the Yoojeong model offers significantly better build quality. However, a damaged guide ring last season kept it out of service. Before the new season starts, I decided to run a self-repair.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfMTEy/MDAxNzc0ODMyMDk3OTYw.k_daI3ovs6iDu-X0_bVy9A64dYp6yV5e98LwCuOHuKog.c8wOvKaA4Gf5fpa7rPNipjv0XppWIhwGL9_GU1O3vkAg.JPEG/SE-8e0fe670-2b2f-11f1-9502-bdc5b68806e6.jpg?type=w800)

​

The rod is shown above. I purchased and reviewed it over three years ago, and it remains one of the best hybrid lifestyle float-fishing rods on the market. For details, see my previous review:

🔗 [Yoojeong Rx-5 Lure-Iso Rod Review (Naver)](https://blog.naver.com/sanjangboarder/222860070838)

​

These are the guides on the Yoojeong Lure-Iso RX-5. In my rod reviews, I regularly express my preference for Fuji guides because of their durability. The guides on this rod are domestic DM-style guides. While the manufacturer does not specify Samyang, Kigan, or other suppliers, they are likely from Samyang or Kigan. While both brands produce decent guides that outperform generic imports, they are less durable than Fuji models.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfMTMg/MDAxNzc0ODMyNTY4MzAz.JXymUS3f3Fx6sqV36cJaVYXNpVKfPxwdndqJk7aBOBwg.eE5rQpVvbXSfdo_YESMnkRgUxM00UkSuB4S76I9jl2Qg.JPEG/SE-8e10a9c1-2b2f-11f1-9502-4b68963713b0.jpg?type=w800)

​

As shown by the arrow in the photo below, the ceramic insert (guide ring) of the fifth guide—the first guide on the second blank section below the tip—popped out. I have experienced this frequently with generic imported guides, and have now seen it on domestic options.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfMTI1/MDAxNzc0ODMyNzc2Nzgz.QfpWp9NndUbGk2xX_1a8-Qjr27V-6tubMj_KSzuWGkMg.OW9TsS2Jthj2Lgp8FKJNokMMFUVgrhoBzZU5d1jrH-cg.JPEG/SE-8e116d12-2b2f-11f1-9502-7781078aae4c.jpg?type=w800)

​

The tip section holds three sliding (floating) guides and one tip guide. While I considered replacing all of them as preventative maintenance, I decided to replace only the tip guide and the damaged fifth guide. I replaced the tip guide because removing it requires applying heat, which can discolor the tip or degrade its durability. Replacing it completely is cleaner.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfOTgg/MDAxNzc0ODMyODQ1NjAz.LpBZnsC-l1lyN5Mrvk_9cZZZI38O_-H3-Rb1Y7g-ibMg.NKlx5dkUj8EKkfy7dG7ejjdT-Sh4iEpWO4TbafzN5DIg.JPEG/SE-8e123063-2b2f-11f1-9502-558ecb06b3e3.jpg?type=w800)

​

While I could have sent the rod to the manufacturer for service, I decided on a self-repair and purchased replacement guides through the Yoojeong Fishing website. Some domestic tackle brands sell individual component parts online, and I appreciate Yoojeong's support for DIY repairs.

​

​

The tools required for DIY guide repair are simple: a lighter, forceps, sandpaper to clean off old adhesive, and super glue to mount the new guides.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzFfMTcy/MDAxNzc0OTMwMzQ5MTQ5.AqNj9Co9GJwJp6d7wVPFz8uYn2H9volIfvKgZwol5WUg.FbmBYt33ssOT4K7JjMnjmI04A5l__WDGLtkADMtcLyYg.JPEG/SE-ebe624dd-2bd6-11f1-bfb8-9da95b02d43a.jpg?type=w800)
![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfMjIg/MDAxNzc0ODMzNzg3NzE2.J9RXr4gJuuhoyHGwO9ePtKpM0dTb-prsXIEH15qXRkEg.4T3ksBqZuwi8En8Plo_yhe1wLA8fLDPBg1lBRWiFPY4g.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260329%EF%BC%BF150744.jpg?type=w800)

​

Gently apply heat to the old guide with a lighter. Avoid placing the flame directly on the blank for too long; rotate the tip while heating to soften the glue inside. Once hot, grip the guide with forceps and slide it off. Forceps are necessary because the heated metal becomes hot.

​

I removed the fifth guide using the same heating method. After sliding the guide off, clean the remaining adhesive off the blank using sandpaper.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzFfMTUg/MDAxNzc0OTMwMzg1NDQw.usPcYbAuS68dEFGEmdDmnQmyeEza3-jPAnb72SKASPUg.RzQIBlUHl4ij-hyVKnQOSDvstm_ssXgjpLWzDmrtDRcg.JPEG/SE-ebe6e82e-2bd6-11f1-bfb8-51612f653ed1.jpg?type=w800)

​

​

Prepare the replacement guides and inspect them for defects.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzBfMjU2/MDAxNzc0ODMzNzg4NDg5.Ihmrkb3WvQ4BGCdNXldJ_LqQM_8Zl1cdMWMCgp194iAg.XK_nhzmBVHC7BaCFRrYkP94kZpFEJaIJ7shN_iQnYd4g.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260329%EF%BC%BF150943.jpg?type=w800)

​

Before applying super glue, test-fit the guides to ensure they seat correctly on the blank.

​

This dry run is critical. The RX-5 blank features guide alignment marks, and you must verify that the guide rings align with these lines without catching.

​

Apply a small amount of super glue to the seat, slide the guide on, and align it.

​

​

The guide repair finished cleanly. I replaced the discolored tip guide, and the new fifth guide insert is secure.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMzFfMTAg/MDAxNzc0OTMwNDM3NjE3.tETFGwUxE-TxdIjrR6Ri6f2xMnsVvqDgywLXajHM0zQg.CLJT5nCBTo4YbDNblb3z-lewFDzbkaJVA6ruGiT3vTwg.JPEG/SE-ebee6246-2bd6-11f1-bfb8-95e58837b72e.jpg?type=w800)

​

Replacing guides on telesco-style float or shore rods is straightforward. With a lighter, forceps, and super glue, anyone can perform basic repairs at home. Take care not to overheat and damage the graphite blank.

​

In my previous review of the Seonjae rod, I expressed dissatisfaction with the factory guides. If you measure the blank tip diameter and guide positions, you can purchase upgraded guides online to custom-tune your setups.
