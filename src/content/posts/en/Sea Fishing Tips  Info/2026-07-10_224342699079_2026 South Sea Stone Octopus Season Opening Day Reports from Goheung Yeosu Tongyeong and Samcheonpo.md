Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

July 9th marked the opening day of the South Sea stone octopus (Dolmuneo) fishing season, which brought thousands of anglers from across the country to key South Sea ports. Eager to see where the octopus were biting best, I reviewed and analyzed opening day reports from various regions.

Across all ports, the bite was slow during the early morning but exploded as the sun rose.

​

The absolute winner of the day was Yeosu, while Geoje was somewhat disappointing. Last year, Nokdong/Goheung was the hotspot, which proves how difficult it is to predict which port will hit the jackpot each year. To summarize the 2026 opening day results in one sentence:

​

> **Yeosu hit a massive jackpot!**
>
> **Other regions performed average or above-average.**

​

Here is a summary of the opening day boat fishing results as we move from west to east:

​

---

​

**1. Nokdong & Goheung Region**

While Nokdong and Goheung had slightly lower numbers compared to last year, the average size of the octopus was excellent. Reports noted that Wando was exceptionally slow, prompting some boats to travel all the way to Goheung waters to fish. While filling a 40L cooler was rare, there were multiple reports of anglers filling 24L coolers. Thus, while it wasn't a legendary jackpot, the region delivered solid, above-average results.

**2. Yeosu Region**

The crown for the biggest jackpot of 2026 goes to Yeosu. The waters around Hwatae Bridge were especially hot, though other spots also delivered outstanding numbers.

​

Before noon, some anglers had landed 70 octopus near Hwatae Bridge, matching the catch rates of webfoot octopus. By 1 PM, 40-50L coolers began filling up. The focus was on quantity rather than size, but the average size was decent, with some reporting total catches exceeding 200 octopus. Many anglers filled their 24L coolers early and had to buy additional boxes to hold their catch, and multiple 40L full-cooler reports were recorded.

**4. Tongyeong & Geoje Region**

Tongyeong showed high variability depending on the charter boat, with rental boats (seon-wae-gi) recording remarkable results. While the average catch was somewhat modest, some boats hit massive numbers using specific drift patterns, with a few reports of full 40L coolers. The results depended heavily on the captain's experience and navigation.

​

Typically, the opening day rush (the "opening bump") lasts for about 3 days. Since tide and weather conditions remain favorable through the weekend, we expect the catch rate to follow the typical pattern of dropping to 50% on the second day and 30% on the third day relative to the opening day.

​

With excellent weather forecasted across the South Sea this weekend, many octopus will likely be caught, after which numbers will return to standard levels. If you are planning a trip, I highly recommend Yeosu. Walking lure anglers reported that catching around 10 octopus is a realistic goal.

​

Since booking charter boats during the opening week is highly competitive, finding open slots in Yeosu is near impossible. A smart alternative is to monitor Nokdong, where slightly lower initial numbers might trigger last-minute cancellations. Additionally, booking a small rental boat (seon-wae-gi) is a great strategy to access shallow reefs that larger commercial vessels cannot reach.

​

Boat fishing for octopus will remain highly popular through next week. I wish everyone a successful and safe trip!
