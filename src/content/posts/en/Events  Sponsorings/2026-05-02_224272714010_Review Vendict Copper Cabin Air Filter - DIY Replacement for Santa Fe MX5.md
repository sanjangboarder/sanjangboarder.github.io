Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

In this post, I introduce the Vendict Copper Cabin Air Filter, an automotive consumable designed to manage cabin air quality during seasons marked by high fine dust and yellow dust.

​

Cabin air filter replacement intervals are similar to engine oil changes, but replacing them more frequently during spring and winter—when ventilation is restricted—helps manage cabin air. Official Hyundai service centers charge around 40,000 to 50,000 KRW (including labor), but replacing it yourself costs around 15,000 KRW. Purchasing a 1+1 bundle with a 10% discount reduces the cost further, making it an easy DIY maintenance task.

​

My vehicle is a Hyundai Santa Fe MX5, which I have operated since January 2024. While I want to maintain its new condition, exhaust fumes and ultrafine dust enter the cabin. With recent rain and rising humidity, a musty odor became noticeable when running the air conditioning, prompting me to replace the cabin filter. After comparing options, I selected the Vendict Copper Cabin Air Filter.

​

The Vendict filter arrived in sealed packaging. The box was protected, ensuring safe delivery.

The key feature of the Vendict cabin filter is its copper (Cu) coating. The copper coating targets airborne contaminants, helping manage cabin air quality.

​

Additionally, the four-sided frame is rigid, supporting the edges unlike budget filters that use soft borders. If a filter twists or leaves gaps during installation, dust bypasses the media. This rigid design supports a sealed fit.

​

The filter media utilizes a 4-layer structure. This system filters large dust particles down to fine particles in successive stages, including the copper layer.

​

It targets ultrafine dust based on an MPPS (Most Penetrating Particle Size, 0.3–5㎛) dust collection profile, protecting against fine particulate matter.
