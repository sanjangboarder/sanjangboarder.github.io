‚Äã

Lately, I have been using AI for both business tasks and daily activities. Today, I discuss using generative AI for image design. Although AI image generation has been available for several years, its development pace has accelerated over the past few months.

‚Äã

Subscribers to Google Gemini, as well as free users, can access the **Nano Banana** AI Agent, which functions as an image generation service. I used Google Nano Banana to design banners for recruiting members for the **TEAM MJT sea fishing community**'s regular and flash outings. Below, I share my experience.

‚Äã

---

‚Äã

My initial goal was to take an existing image and modify specific details. I submitted a request to Nano Banana using the image below. In this scenario, the AI can adjust key elements while preserving the original art style and text layout.

‚Äã

‚Äã

The initial output met my expectations. Since our club hosts both regular trips and spontaneous "flash outings," I asked the AI to modify the text. The update was processed successfully. I then requested the removal of unnecessary details from the background of the original image, which was also handled correctly.

‚Äã

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAxMDRfMTU4/MDAxNzY3NTE5NjM2Mjk2.6o_DsHo5MmsbeDaHPLsKpPH7G9CDdUSlBfgekfl9ycgg.5jXDK_5f9rdr_Xq6bpiP1mfoyaZrACujcwcCusybUUgg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Text changed to flash outing" />
</div>

‚Äã

I requested that the edits be applied to the regular outing banner as well, which produced the intended layout. If you have experience with older AI image generators, you will note the improvement. Previously, text rendering was often garbled, and securing a matching result required highly detailed prompts.

‚Äã

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAxMDRfODYg/MDAxNzY3NTE5NjczNjk0.tv_722rkpVZ341q_b3MKIKUUPZXVWMnKS9k7XbpcME8g.tcNg7kAWtrxAYIaTAiWEnBN-x8n_WkB7jj9IP63QvEgg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Regular outing text update" />
</div>

‚Äã

Because the banners are posted on our community forum, I adjusted the aspect ratio. Since many of our outings target webfoot octopus and cuttlefish, I requested that the target species in the illustration be updated. However, the AI had difficulty identifying cuttlefish. It rendered them as standard squid, and the webfoot octopus resembled common octopuses, resulting in a layout that differed from my concept.

‚Äã

<div class="single-image">
<img src="https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAxMDRfNTgg/MDAxNzY3NTE5Njg0NTkz.7j5qNCsycU7fb8WBSiDDSTbtZ2fDMfP08-y7JGqwRtAg.366ZuHLK1Z1uxrfOIMAuuyjnxK2psKssBbLYHVmRVqAg.PNG/image.png?type=w800" alt="Aspect ratio and species change attempt" />
</div>

‚Äã

I submitted a prompt requesting the squid be replaced specifically with cuttlefish, but the model did not generate a noticeable change, possibly due to a lack of training data for cuttlefish illustrations in Korean.

</div>

I then provided a reference photo of a cuttlefish and asked the AI to incorporate it into the drawing style.

The output was not what I intended. I submitted a more detailed prompt to specify the placement and style of the cuttlefish. Nano Banana processed this instruction successfully.

‚Äã

Although the cuttlefish illustration was rendered correctly, the squid was still present. Rather than using text descriptions, I cropped the squid section in the editor and asked Nano Banana to remove that specific element. However, the output featured an unexpected replacement.

‚Äã

‚Äã

While Google Nano Banana shows significant improvement, it does not produce fully accurate results on every request. I estimate the accuracy is around 90%. Given the speed of AI updates, the remaining details are likely to be addressed in future releases.
