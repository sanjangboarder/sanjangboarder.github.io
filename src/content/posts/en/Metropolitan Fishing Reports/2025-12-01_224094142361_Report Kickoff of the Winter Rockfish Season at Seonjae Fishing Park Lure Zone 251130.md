Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

At this time of year, boat fishing opportunities are limited by weather conditions. The West Sea coastal walking lure season has largely concluded. Through next April, saltwater fishing ponds (*유료 바다낚시터*) serve as the primary option, though most of these venues close from mid-December through early February due to low water temperatures.

​

To my knowledge, the only saltwater fishing pond in Korea that stays open 365 days a year without winter closures is **Seonjae Fishing Park** on Seonjae Island, Incheon. The park operates a float fishing pond (*찌터*) and a lure fishing pond (*루어터*). The float pond operates continuously, and the lure pond remains open unless a severe cold wave sets in. This winter operation is possible because **hot water pipes** maintain manageable water temperatures. For my winter fishing schedule, Seonjae Fishing Park is the primary destination.

​

Rockfish (*Korean rockfish / 우럭*) serve as the main target species during the winter season. Because winter rockfish are active and offer good table quality, I decided to head out. Following a windy Saturday, Sunday brought calmer, warmer conditions, so I headed to the park. I arrived at approximately 5:00 AM.

​

Since rockfish are active under low-light conditions, I planned to target them before sunrise. After purchasing my ticket at the office, I headed to the pond.

​

For my gear setup, I often use a light rockfish (*mebaru*) rod. However, after receiving reports that the fish were holding near the net cages, I selected a stiffer rod to manage cage-fishing presentations. The NH Korea WE60 is designed as an eging rod, but it serves as a utility rod and works well for casting high-density soft plastics rigged for back-sliding.

​

> **Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone Rockfish Tackle Setup**
>
> *   **Rod**: NH Korea WE60 832ML
> *   **Reel**: Shimano 23 Vanquish C2500S
> *   **Main Line**: Haedong LIMIT X9 Braided Line No. 1
> *   **Leader**: Haedong Charisma Extreme No. 3.5 Shock Leader
> *   **Rig**: Haedong Wide-Gap Hook 4/0
> *   **Lures**: OMG Geoshigi Worm (No-Sinker King), Half Lure Yamadesu 2

​

While shorter rods offer some advantages for close-range cage casting by reducing the casting arc, an 8-foot rod is manageable even in crowded conditions. Rod stiffness is more critical than length for this application.

​

For my soft plastics, I focused on the **Yamadesu 2** and the **OMG Geoshigi Worm (No-Sinker King)**. Both are high-density baits weighing around 10g, allowing them to be fished on a no-sinker rig for a slow fall rate and rigged for back-sliding presentations.

​

These two options are among the most popular soft plastics for winter rockfish at Seonjae.

Since the cage pattern was widely known, approximately ten anglers gathered around the spot. The volume of stocked fish was substantial, and the stocking run produced an active bite.

​

Casting soft plastics close to the cages and letting them fall triggered strikes from the rockfish. The anglers maintained an organized rotation around the Line B cages. The active bite lasted for approximately 30 minutes following the stocking run, which was one of the stronger morning runs I have observed at Seonjae.

Winter rockfish lure fishing can produce numbers when feeding windows open. While I did not observe a sustained feeding window during my hours on the water, focusing on the stocking runs produced a respectable catch.
