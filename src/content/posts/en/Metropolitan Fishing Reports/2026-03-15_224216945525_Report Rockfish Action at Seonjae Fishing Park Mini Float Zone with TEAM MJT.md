Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

This is my third visit to Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone (*which I refer to as the Walking Zone*) in Seonjaedo, Incheon since its reopening. This is my second post on it. Last week, I fished the Lure Zone and caught nothing. This week, we scheduled a flash outing for TEAM MJT members at the same venue.

​

Four of us fished the **Mini Float Zone**, and two targeted the **Lure Zone**. I assisted the float fishers, while Mankul Fishing supported the lure anglers. Several members were visiting a stocked facility for the first time, and one was new to fishing, making for a challenging start.

​

The Lure and Mini Float zones operate on two rates: 100,000 KRW for 12 hours, or 70,000 KRW for a 6-hour short session (*Jjam-nak*). I recommend the 6-hour session for beginners. Twelve hours is a long duration for new anglers, whereas a 6-hour window is sufficient to learn the basics.

​

We paid our admission fees at the office and headed to the pond.

I configured the float rig depth to approximately 3 meters and applied continuous vertical jigging (*lift-and-drop*) to attract fish.

During my previous visit on March 8, a sudden cold snap dropped water temperatures, resulting in low activity. For this session on March 14, water temperatures were rising, which improved feeding activity as anticipated.

​

For the Mini Float Zone, I used my standard lure casting rod but modified the rest of the rig. Braided lines are prohibited. On opening day, I used a Jomusa Semi-Floating No. 3 line, but experienced frequent breakages. I upgraded to a No. 4 line for this session.

​

However, the strength of this No. 4 line was below expectations. Even with a lighter No. 3 leader line, the main line broke twice under load. I will need to select a different main line brand for future sessions.

​

> **Mini Float Zone Rig Settings**
> *   **Rod:** NH Korea WE60 832ML
> *   **Reel:** Shimano Stradic C3000
> *   **Main Line:** Jomusa Semi-Floating No. 4
> *   **Float:** HDF Master Cast No. 1 Float
> *   **Leader:** No. 3 fluorocarbon, No. 8 hook
> *   **Bait:** Dyed squid strips, live loach

​

In the Mini Float Zone, locating target structures is key. Current patterns showed fish holding near the cage nets. While the close cage (No. 1) was within casting range, the far cage (No. 2) required a longer cast. I rigged the HDF Master Cast No. 1 float to handle the distance.

​

Heavy bullet floats (*Daepo-jji*) are banned for safety because the opposite bank is only 30 to 50 meters away. The HDF Master Cast No. 1 provides sufficient weight to reach the cage nets without overshooting the opposite bank.

Following the stocking, rockfish strikes occurred around Cage 1. I landed two, and our team members landed their catches here. Since several were new, landing a single fish was the goal. Individual catches were 1, 4, and 0, averaging close to 2 fish per angler.

While I checked the artificial reef structures mapped in my previous guides, they did not yield fish during the day; all active targets held near the cage netting. Night anglers report fish along the breaklines, showing a difference between day and night patterns.

​

Our team landed 9 rockfish in the float zone, but 3 escaped through a hole in the keep net, leaving us with 6.
