Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Determining where to fish this week was challenging because of my tight schedule, and I almost canceled my plans. However, spending time by the water is my preferred way to relieve stress, so I decided to make the trip for a short Saturday night session (locally called "jjam-nak").

​

Last week, I reviewed a promising 3-300 rod designed for the Lure Zone Mini-Float Pond at Seonjae Fishing Park. I wanted to test this rod, so I packed a light setup as shown below.

​

The rod is fully rigged and ready, allowing for a 1-second setup at the pond. I also packed a small bait cooler to fish live shrimp.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfNDMg/MDAxNzc1MzU5NjQxOTQz.9dNnexXZh-ibTIXJsWQOLY07a5m1SRWqj3P_hFT2Mzcg.xYJN7f9TvdHUaULqoDGSTtHso7D9HyX0mbTuuxy4Sh4g.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260404%EF%BC%BF161016.jpg?type=w800)

​

I arrived at Seonjae Fishing Park around 5:40 PM, with about an hour of daylight remaining.

​

I stopped by the office, paid 70,000 KRW for a short session on the Mini-Float Pond, and entered.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMjk1/MDAxNzc1MzU5NjQwMzgz.R_uB-tKaVO61lUMr0jDkU_0Z7L2GgrN32a2j67CTAw4g.rxZz7tjF_1-KZBwbfaNCY-CZiPD-vYkicr8DMHCQfkQg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260404%EF%BC%BF173725.jpg?type=w800)

With the sun setting quickly, I set up my tackle. For details on my setup, see below:

​

> **Seonjae Fishing Park Float Fishing Tackle**
> *   **Rod:** AliExpress 3-300 Float Rod
> *   **Reel:** Shimano Stradic C3000
> *   **Line:** HDF Charisma Triple Semi-Floating Size 4.0
> *   **Float:** HDF Master Float 1.0 / Zzis Electronic Stick Float 1.0 (ZISS RB 1.0)
> *   **Bait:** Live Shrimp, Live Loaches

​

I planned to use an electronic stick float and fish live shrimp as my main bait. Water temperatures in Incheon ponds are currently recording around 12–14°C, which is generally acceptable for live shrimp. However, at water temperatures below 12°C, live shrimp can experience thermal shock and freeze up. As night fell, the air temperature dropped to 8°C with wind, and the shrimp quickly became inactive. For night fishing, using live shrimp is best reserved for water temperatures above 15°C.

Before sunset, the shrimp remained active. On my second cast, running a live shrimp at a 3m depth about 1m off the side of the cage, I landed a rockfish. Starting the session with an early fish was encouraging.

​

However, the rockfish was smaller than those caught on my previous trip. It resembled the smaller sizes stocked on Dobido floating piers.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfODYg/MDAxNzc1MzczNTIxOTcw.56oB_eiJZhKaIGSknpQHdM9vw1dxbUe_rnRm1i-Ldtgg.MXuCrfLlmmZIdOyAMNvxx_pp1OUHEOHUeK-nB3DKFBkg.JPEG/SE-58af906c-309f-11f1-8670-dfa4199ba430.jpg?type=w800)

​

After a dry spell, I targeted the artificial reefs near Fish Cage 2, but received no response. Looking at the stringers of nearby anglers, it appeared the bite was slow overall, possibly influenced by the strong wind.

I tried a few other spots before returning to Fish Cage 1. I cast my float to the left side of the cage and let it settle at a depth of 2.5m.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMTA1/MDAxNzc1MzczODA1MDMy._ffLlXPqKkqN3yZp1egqPbUzz2jzP39wE6FLEL-Q1pIg.HcTw9-VXpIW38-GS3TYbgvlaNZyc7CP4njYK8zX1Sosg.JPEG/SE-58b583e0-309f-11f1-8670-753fc64a36e9.jpg?type=w800)

A larger rockfish took the bait. It was a plump, heavy fish, weighing close to 1kg. This is the premium size standard for Seonjae.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfNTYg/MDAxNzc1MzczNjk2MTUy.hVXb6B-FPe7F9xCWTAsNXgLRJFM_x4nM4F4oHyIW-IMg.ixt61u5sgvAQfMRddU8jJ2u3LLK5zM4qhPY7S9RX6cAg.JPEG/SE-58bcd6e5-309f-11f1-8670-e9b93b271f8d.jpg?type=w800)

Once night fell, the atmosphere became peaceful. Watching illuminated floats bob on the water at night is highly relaxing, and I enjoyed the experience.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMjEy/MDAxNzc1MzU5NjM4MjQ5.J8M3LqCFsvt3hhL7rYCAqDvXWVW6650DoksnRG3Gtqkg.BGUrH3mY4dl5LNQRgzU3PhMSWL8ZIiIUAa_gzDivsCgg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260404%EF%BC%BF193756.jpg?type=w800)

​

Although the atmosphere was good, the bite died. I was hoping for action before the 9:00 PM stocking window, but received no hits.

​

I struggled to find a productive pattern, and targeting the break lines or artificial reefs near the cages failed to produce.

​

​

At night, Fish Cage 3 was relocated in front of Bungalow S5, where the stocking event took place. The staff announced they stocked 50 rockfish, though the darkness prevented me from counting. I would have preferred stocking near Cage 1, which was closer to my position.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMjMw/MDAxNzc1MzU5NjM4MjQ1.eTR5CAoXTBrrNID7y-ImfKvp9DidH54se8byaD2skosg.r_0_YBG3Y6v3qDHQKohtheeLg_hXTaNAEhxqorS6Nj0g.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260404%EF%BC%BF203738.jpg?type=w800)

​

The stocking-induced bite window was slower than on my previous trip. About 30 minutes after stocking, anglers began hooking fish at close range. I adjusted my depth to 2m and cast short, which produced a third rockfish. Unfortunately, it was another small fish.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMTA5/MDAxNzc1MzU5NjQyMDQ5.AG62WMbgVoiEtc-Qni_MWqYR73z41HAGP3Z4rA6sgnkg.GsZE-c2k6XCZtuQcnbu8Y0COuwZTX33w7je8x-IDqQUg.JPEG/900%EF%BC%BF20260404%EF%BC%BF211200.jpg?type=w800)

​

After landing the third fish, I still had time left on my ticket, but the cold forced me to pack up. The temperature showed 8°C, but the wind chill made it feel freezing. Despite wearing warm layers, surviving the wind was difficult. Heavy winter clothing is recommended for night sessions at this time of year.

​

While my catch was modest, I landed 3 rockfish. At the cleaning station, they weighed in at 1.9kg. The single large rockfish made up the bulk of the weight, while the other two were under 500g each.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjA0MDVfMTQ4/MDAxNzc1Mzc0MTM5NTcx.ceNzyR89uif5qshftl4sB86SX3ZOJiBAHY82vVJY-hIg.cPR6_xPLDE38AQW-ON696Y34yVX-ZE10TVrDuSD99kwg.JPEG/SE-58bf20d8-309f-11f1-8670-ef29d19ba5cb.jpg?type=w800)

​

It had been a while since my last Saturday night session at Seonjae. I was surprised by the high number of anglers despite the windy conditions. The crowds prevented me from exploring different spots, which was a drawback. I plan to return during a quieter weekday slot to run more gear tests.

​

As water temperatures rise past 12°C, the rockfish season will wind down, and the ponds will begin stocking red seabream and striped beakfish. My next pay-pond report will likely focus on seabream.

​

Next week, I will board the Jayeon Fishing-ho out of Mageompo to target wild-caught rockfish, greenling, and flatfish, marking the start of my boat fishing season. I will share updates from the salt.
