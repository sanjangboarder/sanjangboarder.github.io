Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

After skipping fishing last week, I was eager to get out on the water. I wanted to go rockfish (*Korean rockfish / 우럭*) fishing in the West Sea, but persistent winds and rough winter conditions have made it difficult for charter boats to head out. Under these conditions, Seonjae Fishing Park on Seonjae Island is my primary choice.

​

Temperatures were mild during the week, making the weekend forecast ideal. However, with a cold wave scheduled to begin next week, this was likely the last outing of the season with manageable winter temperatures. I registered for admission at the office and prepared my gear.

For my tackle setup, I selected the NH Korea WE60 rod, which provides the casting distance required to reach the net cages. Reports indicated that the rockfish had not been holding close to the cages recently, but I wanted to verify this on the water.

​

> **Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone Rockfish Tackle Setup**
>
> *   **Rod**: NH Korea WE60 832ML
> *   **Reel**: Shimano 23 Vanquish C2500S
> *   **Main Line**: Haedong LIMIT X9 Braided Line No. 1 (15lb)
> *   **Leader**: Haedong Charisma Extreme No. 3.5 Fluorocarbon Leader
> *   **Rig**: Haedong Wide-Gap Hook 4/0, 1/22oz jig head
> *   **Lures**: OMG Geoshigi Worm, Yamadesu 2 SP, Berkley Swimming Mullet 4-inch

The stocking process began around 7:00 AM. Seonjae Fishing Park is known for high stocking volumes. On this day, they stocked approximately two to three times the number of active anglers. I landed several rockfish from this reef. Because the current from the waterwheel was pushing the lure, I switched to a 1/8oz jig head to reach the bottom faster, landing one fish. I then switched to a weightless (no-sinker) setup, which produced two more fish despite the difficulty in maintaining bottom contact under current.

​

My final count was eight rockfish. One came from the net cages, one from the stone ledge break lines, and the remaining six were caught near the long-cast artificial reefs.

​

This outing confirms that targeting the artificial reefs is currently the most effective method. I plan to refine the Seonjae Fishing Park reef maps to make them easier to read for future outings.

​

December has been relatively warm compared to typical winter seasons. Last year, mid-December temperatures were significantly lower, making fishing difficult. Currently, conditions remain manageable.

​

Seonjae Fishing Park is notable for operating hot water pipes during the winter. Last year, the system was activated in early December due to cold temperatures, but it has not been turned on yet this season. With cold weather forecast for next week, activation is expected soon. The warm water should shift rockfish holding patterns once again.

​

If the cold wave is manageable next week, I will return with another field report.
