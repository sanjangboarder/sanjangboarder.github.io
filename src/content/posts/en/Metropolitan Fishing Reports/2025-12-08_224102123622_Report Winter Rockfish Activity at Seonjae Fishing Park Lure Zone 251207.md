Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last weekend, the temperatures warmed up slightly, though we experienced some wind. I debated whether to head out, but there is a saying: when in doubt, just go. I made my decision late and headed out on Sunday afternoon.

​

I entered Seonjae Fishing Park on Seonjae Island, Incheon, at approximately 2:40 PM. It was my first visit on a Sunday afternoon, and the environment was noticeably quiet. The peak crowd usually visits on Fridays and Saturdays, and by Sunday afternoon, the weekend rush clears out. This time slot is a viable option for a quiet outing.

​

The temperature was around 10°C, and as long as I blocked the wind, it did not feel overly cold. Based on the Ansan weather station observations, the water temperature was around 8°C. This is an active temperature range for rockfish (*Korean rockfish / 우럭*).

​

For my primary gear, I selected my NH Korea WE60 eging rod, which provides the casting distance required to reach the net cages. This rod works well for both near and distant cages; though it is long, the extra reach is helpful. I also set up a lighter rod because flatfish (*righthand flounder / 도다리*) had been reported near Zone A.

​

​

I spent most of my time targeting rockfish with the eging rod, using the Hues T583A setup for about an hour to target flounder.

I started at the Line A center bridge. As shown in the photo, the wind picked up in the afternoon. Since the wind blew directly from the entrance, managing the line bow was difficult.

​

Rockfish favor structures like stone ledge break lines, reefs, and bridges. I cast under the bridge structures and retrieved along the break lines, which produced a strong strike.

​

However, while applying heavy-handed pressure to prevent the fish from wrapping around the bridge pillars, the line snapped. Losing a fish at the start is always frustrating.

Despite a substantial volume of stocked fish, the expected active bite did not materialize, and casting along the net cage structures produced no response. Occasionally, my skipped Geoshigi Worm would land inside the cage nets, triggering an immediate bite. This suggests the fish were active but were dispersing quickly rather than holding near the cages.

I moved back to target the distant reef lines. A long cast over the reef produced another rockfish. I received consecutive strikes on the reef line, but the fish spat the hooks on the retrieve, likely due to the wide-gap hook configuration.

​

When rockfish swallow the bait deeply, hooksets are secure, but losing multiple fish in a row was frustrating. I regretted not using a standard jig head (*C-jig head / 씨지그*), but the opportunity had passed.

​

As the only dedicated lure pond among Incheon's saltwater options, Seonjae Fishing Park is entering its peak season. Summer was warm, and it remains to be seen if winter will bring severe cold. Water temperatures have dropped faster than last year. The venue typically operates through the winter unless the Han River freezes over, and I hope the temperatures remain manageable.

​

A substantial volume of fish was stocked during this outing, and a significant portion likely remained in the pond. This suggests that fishing conditions during the weekdays should be productive. A strong feeding window is likely to open between Tuesday and Thursday, and I will monitor reports to decide on my next outing.
