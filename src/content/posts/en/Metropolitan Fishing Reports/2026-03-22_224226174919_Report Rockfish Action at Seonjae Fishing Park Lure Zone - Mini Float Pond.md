Over the weekend, I decided to head out early Saturday morning to the Mini Float Pond located in the Lure Zone of Seonjae Fishing Park in Seonjaedo, Incheon.

‚Äã

Seonjae Fishing Park features two main basins: the main Float Pond and the Lure Zone. The fishing areas are divided into the main Float Pond, the Mini Float Pond, and the Lure Pond. I fished the Mini Float Pond within the Lure Zone, which is similar in scale to the Manjeong Mint Pond in Yeongjongdo.

‚Äã

I checked in at the office. The entrance fee for a 6-hour short session is 70,000 KRW for men and 60,000 KRW for women. A full 12-hour ticket is 100,000 KRW. Although the price difference is moderate, I prefer the 6-hour ticket as it allows me to fish through one stocking window before completing the session.

Before the fish stocking time, the fishing was slow. There were 7 to 8 anglers at the pond, and no catches were recorded early on. Water temperatures are stable, but rockfish are entering their spawning season, which generally reduces their feeding activity.

‚Äã

During the spawning season, rockfish seek cover in structure or hold close to obstacles. Unlike other commercial ponds, Seonjae's Mini Float Pond does not have defined underwater fish paths (*channels*). Consequently, the fish hold near the artificial shelters or along the net cage structures.

‚Äã

Stocking at the Mini Float Pond occurs between 10:00 AM and 10:30 AM. The volume is calculated at roughly twice the number of checked-in anglers. Typically, this equates to four fish per angler for a full-day ticket, or two fish per angler per session. Stocking volumes were higher during this visit due to an ongoing promotional event.

‚Äã

The stocking crew released roughly two fish per angler near Net Cage 1, and one fish per angler near Net Cage 2, providing a solid volume.

Following the recent renovation and reopening of the Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone, visitor numbers have increased. The entrance fee reduction of approximately 30% has contributed to this trend, reducing the price gap with nearby Seonjae Leisure Pond. The facility is also attracting more beginner anglers.

‚Äã

For beginners entering saltwater fishing, float fishing is often a practical starting point compared to lure fishing. Float setups are easier to teach, and the technical requirements are less complex. Pnd staff provide brief coaching for beginners in the Mini Float Pond. In contrast, lure fishing involves more technical elements, making individual coaching difficult.

‚Äã

Additionally, float fishing generally offers a higher probability of landing at least one fish. For beginners visiting Seonjae, starting at the Mini Float Pond is a reasonable choice. However, the fishing remains challenging, particularly during the spawning season when rockfish carry eggs. Focusing your efforts immediately after stocking times increases the chance of landing fish.
