Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Last week saw a great spring tide. While the webfoot octopus and cuttlefish season is wrapping up, I could have managed a few more trips. However, the strong currents during this tide led me to choose a more relaxed alternative: the Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone on Seonjae Island, Incheon.

​

I wanted to do some night fishing before the colder winter weather made overnight stays less practical. After checking in at the office and renting a bungalow, I headed to the pond.

​

Since the rockfish season is underway, I could have used lighter gear. However, because sea bass had also been stocked, I selected two light-class tackle sets to balance target sensitivity and landing capability.

​

I entered the facility around 11:00 PM on Saturday. The reports from the preceding week showed steady rockfish catches alongside the start of sea bass stocking, which raised my expectations for the start of the winter season.

​

> *   **Rigs:** 1/22 oz, 1/8 oz jig heads, wide gap hooks

I fished from Line A to Line C but did not receive any bites early on. I focused my initial casts on the bottom structures, under the bridges, and close to the net cage walls. I then alternated with long casts targeting the stone break lines and artificial fish reefs.

After two hours, I received a firm bite under the Line D steel bridge using a skipping cast. It felt like a sea bass, and I attempted to lead it away from the structural pillars, but the line rubbed against the bridge support and broke.

​

After returning from a walk around the pond, I cast close to the forward net cage on Line D. The net had been removed from this cage, so snagging was less of a concern. On the fall, I received a bite. Given the previous break, I brought it in carefully and landed a rockfish.

I walked the pond twice more but did not receive additional bites. When I shone my flashlight near the edge, I could see numerous rockfish close to the bank, but they swam away from the light. Although the fish were active, they were not taking the lures. The fasted fish stocked earlier in the week may have already been caught.

​

The ticket at the Seonjae Fishing Park Lure Zone allows for 10 hours of fishing. Because lure fishing requires constant movement, maintaining focus for the full duration is demanding. My final result of two rockfish and one lost sea bass after an overnight effort was modest.

​

The peak season for the Seonjae Lure Zone is winter. Once more sea bass are stocked and rockfish become the primary target, catch rates should improve. While the fishing was slow during this trip, the large volume of stocked fish suggests activity will improve during the weekdays as they settle.

​

For those considering a weekday trip, the accumulated stock should provide good opportunities.
