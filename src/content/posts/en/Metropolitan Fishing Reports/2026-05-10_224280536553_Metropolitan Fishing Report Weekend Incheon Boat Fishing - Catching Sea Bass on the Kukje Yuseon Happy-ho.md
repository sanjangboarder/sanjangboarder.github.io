Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Although Parent's Day fell on a Saturday and I originally had a family lunch gathering planned, my schedule cleared up in the evening. I took the opportunity to book a quick, convenient half-day fishing trip at Incheon South Port (Namhang) on **Kukje Yuseon's Happy-ho**.

​

Living in Songdo, Incheon, the South Port Amusement Pier is very close. Its excellent accessibility from the 2nd Gyeongin Expressway makes it highly convenient to reach even from Seoul.

​

Here is the South Port Amusement Pier, a highly popular harbor for Incheon boat fishing. Since I booked the morning session on the Happy-ho, I arrived comfortably around 5:30 AM. Starting in spring, full-day charters set sail very early between 4:00 AM and 4:30 AM, requiring early arrivals. However, half-day charters like the Happy-ho depart around 6:30 AM, meaning arriving by 5:30 AM is plenty of time.

​

For those taking half-day charters at Incheon South Port, the harbor staff opens the main parking lanes around 5:30 AM, allowing morning session guests to park directly along the access lanes (as shown below). Arriving around 5:30 AM guarantees a spot, eliminating parking concerns. Parking is also completely free.

Since we had plenty of time before departure, I set up my tackle. Being an experienced angler, I brought my personal gear. I heard flatfish were active, so I prepared a downshot setup. The standard rental rods are matched for flatfish downshotting with 2-hook bait rigs, so you can reference my specs below:

​

> **Incheon Boat Fishing Gear & Rig Specs**
>
> *   **Rod**: JS Company Charm Air B642MR
> *   **Reel**: Daiwa Rise Baitcasting Reel
> *   **Line**: Haedong Limit X9 PE 1.2
> *   **Rig**: Deli Rig Lux Straight Hook 4/0
> *   **Worms**: Trigger X (Pink, White), Damiki Worms
> *   **Sinker**: Fishingtem Sinker Size 40
> *   *SanjangBorder*

For my rig, I used Delion's Deli Rig Lux, a sliding downshot rig. It allows easy adjustment of the hook leader distance, which is highly useful when adjusting to water clarity.

The angler fishing next to me landed an amazing fish right at the buzzer. He initially thought his line was snagged on the bottom, but I noticed the rod tip bouncing and advised him to reel in slowly. He ended up landing a massive catch.

​

He landed a giant, near-80cm sea bass. He appeared to be a beginner, and landing such a memorable fish on rental gear will surely stay with him. Witnessing the fight next to him set my heart racing.

The run from the harbor to my house is only 10 minutes, so I arrived home before noon. Having spent a productive morning on the water felt excellent. For those visiting from out of town, visiting the nearby Yeonan Pier Fish Market after the trip is highly recommended.

​

While I was disappointed to miss out on flatfish this trip, I have another flatfish trip scheduled for next week and will share those results.
