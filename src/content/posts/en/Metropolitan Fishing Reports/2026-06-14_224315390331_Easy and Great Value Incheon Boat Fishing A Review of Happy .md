Hello, this is Sanjangbodeo.


I was thinking of trying out Incheon boat fishing to introduce my beginner-angler brother-in-law to the sport. The Gukje Yuseon time-based boat, which I frequently use for Incheon boat fishing, seemed like the perfect fit. If you ask me what's good for beginner fishing, I recommend Incheon boat fishing. Specifically, look for a boat like Happy Ho at Incheon Namhang Pleasure Boat Dock, where there's a purser who takes excellent care of beginners and an auntie who makes delicious tteokbokki.


Happy Ho departs twice a day, in the morning and afternoon. On weekends, you can enjoy fishing for an affordable 50,000 won per adult. Departures typically happen around 6:20 AM. Incheon Namhang also has parking available for morning boats, which opens around 5:20 AM. If you arrive before then, you can park close to the boarding location.

Happy Ho is spacious among Incheon boat fishing time-based boats, and with two pursers on board to assist, it's perfect for enjoying a comfortable fishing experience throughout the trip. Here's a tip: seating on Happy Ho's time-based boat is assigned on a first-come, first-served basis, determined by the order in which you set up your fishing rod. These days, we target rockfish/flounder. Since I was aiming for flounder with a downshot rig, I decided where to set up and ended up choosing a spot near the bow, right below the captain's cabin, along with my beginner brother-in-law.

At Incheon Boat Fishing Gukje Yuseon, if beginners come and rent equipment, everything is already set up, so there's no need to prepare anything. But since I'm a professional angler, haha, I set up my own prepared gear.


The gear I prepared was for Incheon boat fishing flounder downshot.

The tide that day was '4-mul' (a specific tidal condition), and the tidal range was around 600, which is exactly the kind of current flounder love. With slack tide expected early at 9 AM, I figured the best time to really focus on fishing would be from 7 AM to 9 AM, so I decided to concentrate hard during the early hours.


The captain skillfully controlled the large fishing boat, maneuvering it through rocky areas almost like island hopping...

Although the bites weren't as frequent as I hoped, I got a good look at Palmido thanks to it.
