Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Water temperatures are beginning to rise, bringing Incheon boat fishing back to mind. As of March 29, the local water temperature in Incheon is around 8.5°C. At this level, rockfish, greenling, yellowbelly rockfish, and gobies begin to feed. While their activity will peak once water temperatures reach 10–12°C, I booked a half-day session on the **Happy-ho** operated by Gukje Yuseon out of Namhang Pier to check the early conditions.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMjlfMjE0/MDAxNzc0NzkwMjc2NjA5.WccQDUkOCoGo3_gAGKFHCxAJO6uhmF3_yaPLzouCAFQg.0pb1hK0ixZFRDK3WT9zzeRvGPJHMV_4SJbl1BLAqqiAg.PNG/image.png?type=w800)

​

While it is still early in the boat fishing season, Incheon's passenger boats remain highly popular. I arrived at Namhang Pier around 6:00 AM expecting easy parking, but the main lot was already full. Fortunately, as it is not peak season yet, I managed to park inside the pier lot.

​

At Namhang, boats are divided into full-day and half-day (morning/afternoon) sessions. Morning session anglers can double-park or park along the access lanes, as morning boats return at the same time, allowing morning customers to clear their cars around 11:20 AM.

​

It has been about 5 months since my last trip with Gukje Yuseon during the autumn webfoot octopus season. The staff remains friendly, and rental tackle is available for anglers who do not own gear. Rigs and bait can be purchased at the office. Sinkers, hooks, gloves, coffee, and life jackets are provided free of charge. For beginners, booking with a major operator like Gukje Yuseon is highly convenient.

​

![](https://mblogthumb-phinf.pstatic.net/MjAyNjAzMjlfOTIg/MDAxNzc0Nzg5OTk4ODY5.64nbERDvV9o4xlwkIdro_pKXsWo4viwsYGDfqPokCaMg.A7JPG0lSVaTHHPIvkAFhlVGUl3S-JYkGTcxMVTcz62Ag.JPEG/SE-1e575f9f-2b26-11f1-9502-f1093b18e557.jpg?type=w800)

​

Incheon Namhang serves as a home port for large passenger vessels. The Happy-ho, which I boarded, operates separate morning and afternoon sessions. For beginner anglers, half-day sessions are more manageable than full-day commitments.

​

> *   **Location:** Namhang Passenger Ship Pier, Incheon
> *   **Operator:** Gukje Yuseon
> *   **Schedule:** Morning Session (6:30 AM–11:20 AM), Afternoon Session (12:10 PM–5:00 PM)

The HDF Cha-Pro rig is designed for inshore lifestyle target species like rockfish, greenling, yellowbelly rockfish, chub mackerel, and horse mackerel. It is structured to transmit subtle bites from smaller fish, and functioned as expected, landing small rockfish early in the session.
