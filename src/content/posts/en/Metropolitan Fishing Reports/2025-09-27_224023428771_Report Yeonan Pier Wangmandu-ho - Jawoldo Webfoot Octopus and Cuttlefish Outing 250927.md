Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

This season, I have focused my webfoot octopus and cuttlefish trips on the Taean and Boryeong areas. However, I recalled booking two Incheon trips earlier in the year. The primary departure points in Incheon are Namhang Port and Yeonan Pier. Among these, Yeonan Pier hosts a larger concentration of established operators. It offers the most convenient road access among Incheon ports. The competition among operators in this corridor is high, which leads to active service improvements and attracts a steady volume of visitors.

​

A new combined operation called **Khan Fishing** appears to have been established this year. It includes several high-speed vessels that previously operated independently. The Khan Fishing office was organized for the season. The shelves held multiple hard-to-find jig designs, and the competitive environment in Yeonan Pier remains beneficial for anglers.

My tackle setup for Incheon was configured for heavier loads. The pre-departure message from the operator recommended size 16 to 20 sinkers, which I interpreted as a deep-water plan. However, the actual depths fished were around 10 meters or shallower, meaning my heavy configuration limited my sensitivity.

​

> **Tackle Configurations**
>
> *   **Setup 1:**
>     *   **Rod:** Choipro Seohae 58g
>     *   **Reel:** Tomato 2 baitcasting reel + Gomexus double handle
>     *   **Line:** PE 1.0 braided line
> *   **Setup 2:**
>     *   **Rod:** Aomax Pattern Black 185B
>     *   **Reel:** NH Korea Core Speed Air
>     *   **Line:** Haedong Pinatura PE 8-carrier 1.0 braided line
> *   **Rig:** E-Tech Omija Size 15 & 17, Size 18–20 sinkers, Haedong cuttlefish rig
> *   **Jigs:** B-Bait Deep Slow, Yo-Zuri HRM, Yo-Zuri Tinsel, Yo-Zuri Sam-bong KVPU, army green/target shrimp mini jigs, Sene Purple.

​

Anticipating size 20 sinkers, I set up the stiff Choipro Seohae rod, which has a 9:1 action where only the solid tip flexes. I also rigged the limited-edition Aomax Pattern Black. The Pattern Black has a distinct taper that I wanted to test. I will share a detailed review of this rod in a future post.

​

I also paired the NH Korea Core Speed Air reel. It is lightweight, and despite its 7.1:1 gear ratio, it offered smooth retrieve action through the day.

Only three boats from the Incheon fleet targeted Jawoldo Island in the morning. While catches were low, the captain chose to remain at this location rather than relocate. In the afternoon, we moved to the Puldeung sandbar area between Jawoldo and Muuido Islands for two hours, which produced some activity near the low tide window before we returned to the island.

Given the bright skies and strong sunlight, I rotated through watermelon patterns and jigs featuring internal mirrors. At the sandbar and shore points where muddy water was present, I used army green and target shrimp patterns. The B-Bait Deep Slow watermelon jig was effective for webfoot octopus, while the Yo-Zuri Tinsel jig produced cuttlefish strikes.
