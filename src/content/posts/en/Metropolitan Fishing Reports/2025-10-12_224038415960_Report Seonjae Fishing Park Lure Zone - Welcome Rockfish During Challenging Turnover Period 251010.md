Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

As the long Chuseok holiday drew to a close, with rain forecast and fast currents expected, I decided to visit Seonjae Fishing Park. For angling under poor weather conditions, Seonjae Fishing Park on Seonjaedo Island, Incheon, remains a practical choice.

​

After registering at the office, I entered the pond area. Despite it being a month since my last visit, the Lure Zone was relatively empty.

I took a spot on the left side of the A-Line cage and noted only two other anglers in the area. The low turnout appeared to be related to the autumn water turnover, which typically reduces fish activity. While setting up my tackle, the remaining anglers packed up, leaving me with the entire pond to myself.

Rockfish stocking began approximately two weeks prior. Since red seabream and rockfish are the main targets through October, I returned to a lighter tackle configuration. Unlike summer species that require heavy setups, rockfish and seabream are manageable on light game gear.

​

> **Tackle Specifications**
>
> *   **Rod:** Seahawk Rock Star Pro 782L
> *   **Reel:** Shimano 23 Vanquish C2500S
> *   **Line:** Haedong Limit X9 1.0 braided line
> *   **Shock Leader:** Haedong Charisma Extreme 3.5 fluorocarbon
> *   **Rig:** 1/22 oz and 1/8 oz jigheads, wide gap hooks
> *   **Lures:** Berkley Gulp! 3-inch Swimming Mullet, Yamadeath 4-inch worm

The A cage housed red seabream. I observed them swimming close to the surface and cast repeatedly in their vicinity without response. A large quantity of small shrimp was visible under the A cage steel gangway. Although they were a practical size for bait, the seabream did not target them actively.

The D-Line goose zone was also clear but inactive. During the main rockfish season, rockfish typically gather around the structures. I hope activity levels improve as water temperatures decrease.

​

The net systems were not yet installed on the cages. As water temperatures drop further and become more favorable for rockfish, the nets will be lowered, which should improve catch rates.

​

Determining the cause of low activity on this day was difficult. In my experience, late September through mid-October is a transition period with rapidly declining water temperatures, which can lead to fish suspended in the mid-water columns, making bottom-focused techniques less effective.

​

As water temperatures decline below 20 degrees Celsius, red seabream activity should improve. Once temperatures drop to around 15 degrees Celsius, rockfish activity will likely increase, marking the start of the winter season. I prefer targeting rockfish during the colder months.

​

I expect to have more active rockfish sessions by early November. While this outing produced only a single rockfish, I will share reports with better counts in future updates.
