Hello, this is SanjangBorder.

​

Originally, I had scheduled a boat trip out of Mageompo Port targeting deep-sea cuttlefish in the Shinjindo area. However, as has been the case during weekends this autumn, the weather was poor. Rain fell in the early morning and high waves made it impossible for the boat to sail. With the cuttlefish trip cancelled, I looked for an alternative to start my week.

​

The previous week, I had a challenging session in the Lure Fishing Zone at Seonjae Fishing Park, landing only a single rockfish. Expecting better results in the Float Fishing Zone, I visited the park on Seonjaedo Island, Incheon, to fish their main float zone.

​

Although it rained until early morning, the precipitation had stopped by the time I arrived around 7:00 AM, leaving only light winds. I entered the office to select a bungalow.

​

At Seonjae Fishing Park, bungalows are selected using a real-time monitor in the office, which is also broadcast on YouTube to show availability.

​

On the monitor, green represents occupied bungalows, stars denote positions that can reach the cages, and artificial reefs are color-coded (red, yellow, white, black) to help plan casting locations. On this day, the wind was blowing from the bottom-left toward the top-right of the map, so I chose a bungalow on the lower left side.

​

The bungalows at Seonjae Fishing Park are equipped with air conditioning, a small refrigerator, and a television, making them suitable for outings with family, friends, or children.

​

Because it had been some time since my last float fishing trip, I set up a basic rig. Since the stocked species was red seabream, a standard setup was sufficient. I purchased live shrimp and green sandworms. Because the live shrimp were relatively large, they did not receive immediate interest, so I peeled them and used the sandworm and peeled shrimp combo.

​

> **Tackle Specifications**
>
> *   **Rod:** Jushen Kurodai-chi 1.5 Rod
> *   **Reel:** Shimano Stradic C3000
> *   **Line:** Jomusa Semi-Floating Line Size 3
> *   **Float:** Rocket Float Size 1.5
> *   **Leader:** Size 3 leader line, size 6 hook
> *   **Bait:** Live shrimp, green sandworms

​

My approach was to cast out to about 20 meters and retrieve the rig slowly toward the bungalow, adjusting the depth setting systematically.

​

The stocking session occurred around 9:00 AM, but I did not receive any bites. Cephalopods in the float zone appeared inactive prior to the stocking window.

​

Following my low count in the Lure Fishing Zone the previous week, the results in the Float Fishing Zone were also modest. During periods of fluctuating water temperatures, the Float Fishing Zone generally offers more stable conditions than the lure pond. While my catch was limited, some experienced anglers in the area landed multiple fish. I plan to return during the winter season to target rockfish around the cages.
