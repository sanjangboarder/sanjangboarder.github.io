The Songdo Moonlight Park RC Car Track is currently in its soft-launch pilot phase, and I have checked its status and opening schedule.

​

First, let's look at the schedule. Although the completion ceremony was held in March, you cannot simply bring your RC car and run it immediately. The facility is currently running pilot sessions while developing its online reservation system. Visiting the site is possible, and they briefly opened the reservation system before closing it, though registering on-site might let you use the tracks during this trial phase.

​

The official grand opening is scheduled for around June or July this year. Since it is a brand-new facility, safety checks and rule adjustments are underway. To avoid a wasted trip, make sure to check the official announcements and reservation options (general experience, personal gear sessions) before visiting. While the official contact details aren't listed yet, I have included the community links below.

Looking at the track design, it is not a simple dirt plot but an on-road circuit built to international specifications.

​

Forget about typical toy RC cars spinning up dust clouds in empty neighborhood plots. This facility was built as an on-road asphalt race track demanding precise control and high focus, matching international tournament standards. It is an excellent place to practice corner entry and racing lines, and has already been featured on local TV news.

I also want to introduce a budget-friendly RC car suitable for this track. Since this is a large facility, you'll need a capable model. I recommend starting with the **WLtoys 144001**. The track currently supports up to 1:10 scale models, and I will confirm size limitations once official guidelines are finalized.

​

Toy RC cars purchased from local supermarkets lack the performance required for a proper track. For beginners, the WLtoys line offers reliable performance and easy maintenance. Scale is important; a 1:14 scale is the minimum practical size for this track, while 1:10 scale models are optimal for the main track. While 1:10 models are excellent, they can be slightly expensive (ranging from 200,000 to 300,000 KRW for models like the 104019 or 104021) and harder for beginners to manage, which is why I recommend the 144001.

​
